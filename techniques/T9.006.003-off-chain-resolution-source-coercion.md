# OAK-T9.006.003 — Off-chain Resolution-Source Coercion

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T9 (Smart-Contract Exploit); sub-Technique of OAK-T9.006 (Subjective-Oracle Resolution Manipulation)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (Polymarket on Polygon canonical at v0.1)
**First documented:** 2026-03 (Times of Israel correspondent Emanuel Fabian, Iran-strike market)
**Aliases:** "journalist coercion", "off-chain reporter extortion", "resolution-input-layer attack", "oracle-input physical-coercion"

## Description

Attack on the **resolution-input layer** of subjective-oracle prediction markets. When a market's resolution depends on a single human reporter's published account or a narrow cohort of off-chain sources, the human reporter becomes an attack surface. Bettors with positions in the disfavoured outcome translate their economic bounty into pressure (threats, extortion, harassment) on the off-chain resolution source — demanding the reporter rewrite, retract, or suppress their account so the market resolves in the threatening bettors' favour.

The attack vector is not crypto-specific in technique — extortion of journalists has a long history outside crypto. What is structurally novel is that the **economic incentive for the extortion is created by the prediction market itself**. The market creates a bounty for a specific reporting outcome; bettors with positions translate that bounty into pressure on the human reporter.

This is a category-level new finding for OAK: an attack vector that operates **entirely off-chain, against humans, but whose economic motivation is generated on-chain**.

Distinct from sibling sub-Techniques:

- **T9.006.001 (DVM Vote Capture)** — attacks the oracle layer; T9.006.003 attacks upstream of the oracle, at the resolution-input layer.
- **T9.006.002 (Resolution-Spec Ambiguity Exploitation)** — attacks the spec layer; T9.006.003 attacks the input that the spec references.
- **T9.006.004 (Operational-Insider Trading)** — attacks the information-symmetry assumption; T9.006.003 attacks the resolution-input layer.

## Observed indicators

- Threats / extortion / harassment directed at named off-chain reporters or publications whose reporting is the resolution input for a high-volume disputed market.
- Bettor-to-resolution-source social-media interaction patterns: replies, DMs, mentions concentrated in the resolution window.
- Public first-person accounts from threatened reporters (Times of Israel / Fabian first-person account is the canonical reference).
- Single-source resolution input: markets where a single journalist or single publication is the dominant resolution input.

## Detection signals

- Pre-event flagging of high-volume markets where the reporting cohort is narrow.
- Bettor-position disclosure to potential resolution sources: journalists whose reporting is cited in a resolution spec should be informed before publication that their account may have economic influence.
- Platform-level threat-monitoring of bettor behaviour around the resolution window for markets with narrow reporting cohorts.
- Post-event corroboration: cross-reference between the threatened reporter's published account and independent news coverage (Washington Post / Haaretz corroboration of the Fabian case is the canonical pattern).

## Real-world examples

- **Polymarket Iran-strike market / journalist coercion — 2026-03 (canonical T9.006.003 case)** — \~\$900,000 in market positions held by Polymarket bettors who would have profited from a YES resolution. The economically operative event is the **threats issued against Times of Israel correspondent Emanuel Fabian** demanding he rewrite his report so the market would resolve in their favour. Fabian preserved evidence and published a first-person account in Times of Israel; corroborated by Washington Post and Haaretz coverage on March 17, 2026. The journalist did not change his reporting; the market resolved against the threatening bettors' position; the perpetrators were not publicly identified or prosecuted at v0.1. **First publicly documented case in crypto-prediction-market history of physical coercion of an off-chain human reporter as an attack vector against the resolution layer.** See [`examples/2026-03-polymarket-iran-strike-journalist-coercion.md`](../examples/2026-03-polymarket-iran-strike-journalist-coercion.md).

## Mitigations

- **Source-multiplicity in resolution specifications (highest-leverage):** markets requiring two or more independently-published sources for resolution, with no single human reporter's account being load-bearing. Market-design mitigation, not platform-design mitigation.
- **Bettor-position disclosure to potential resolution sources:** journalists whose reporting is cited in a resolution spec should be informed that their account may have economic influence. Operationally awkward but the only way to extend traditional journalistic threat-modelling into the crypto-prediction-market context.
- **Platform-level threat-monitoring of high-volume markets where reporting cohorts are narrow:** identify markets where a single journalist or single publication is the dominant resolution input; warrant heightened monitoring of bettor behaviour around the resolution window.
- **Machine-checkable resolution criteria** (parent-Technique mitigation): markets resolving on cryptographically-verifiable inputs are not exposed to T9.006.003.

## Discussion

The Fabian case proves a structural property of subjective-oracle prediction markets that was previously theoretical: **when the resolution input is a single human reporter's published account, the human reporter becomes an attack surface**. OAK-T9.001 (price-feed oracle manipulation) does not contemplate this attack class; the mitigations OAK lists for T9.001 (TWAP, multi-venue feeds, deviation halts — OAK-M09) are mechanical and apply to numerical price feeds. None of them apply to "the resolution depends on a journalist's interpretation of an event."

T9.006.003 is structurally the most concerning of the four T9.006 sub-patterns because the attack vector extends into physical coercion of off-chain humans. The defensive surface is real (market-spec design, source-multiplicity, journalist-side awareness) but is operationally outside the technical-mitigation domain that OAK Mitigations otherwise cover.

**Attribution for T9.006.003 incidents is structurally hard.** The threats come from individual anonymous accounts with no single operator-cluster signature. OAK should expect future cases of this class to remain `inferred-strong` or `confirmed-without-attribution` rather than reaching the per-operator `confirmed` standard.

OAK's mission is to document the real adversary surface against on-chain assets — including the parts that are not technical exploits. The Fabian case is on the public record (Washington Post, Haaretz, Times of Israel), the technique is generalisable (any subjective-oracle prediction market with narrow resolution-source cohorts), and the defensive surface is real. Documenting it explicitly is the only way an open framework can claim to cover the actual adversary surface rather than the comfortable subset of it.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once T9.006.003 carries multiple independent worked-example anchors beyond the canonical Fabian / Iran-strike case. The class is structurally generalisable; pipeline anchors will surface in any 2026+ subjective-oracle prediction market whose resolution-source cohort is narrow and whose disputed-market volume is large enough to incentivise off-chain coercion.
