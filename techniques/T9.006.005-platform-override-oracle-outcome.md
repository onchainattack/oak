# OAK-T9.006.005 — Platform-Override of Oracle Outcome

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T9 (Smart-Contract Exploit), OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation); sub-Technique of OAK-T9.006 (Subjective-Oracle Resolution Manipulation)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (Polymarket on Polygon / UMA DVM on Ethereum canonical at v0.1)
**First documented:** 2024-06 (Polymarket / DJT memecoin / Barron Trump market)
**Aliases:** "platform override", "oracle-outcome override", "discretionary-resolution override", "ultimate-discretion clause attack"

## Description

Attack on the **platform-level resolution** layer of prediction markets. When a prediction-market platform retains an "ultimate discretion" clause — the authority to override the designated decentralised oracle's outcome — that discretion is a structural backstop on top of the oracle mechanism. The oracle outcome is a signal input; the platform's override authority is the actual settlement authority. The gap between the oracle's stated outcome and the platform's actual resolution is the T9.006.005 detection surface.

The attack is not an attack *on* the oracle (T9.006.001 vote capture), *on* the spec (T9.006.002 ambiguity exploitation), *on* the off-chain data source (T9.006.003 coercion), or *on* the information-symmetry assumption (T9.006.004 insider trading). The platform override operates *above* all four sibling sub-Techniques: even if the oracle functions correctly, the spec is unambiguous, the data source is uncoerced, and no insider information is exploited — the platform can still override the outcome.

The structural feature is the **platform-discretion clause**: a ToS provision reserving the platform's right to override the oracle's outcome. For any DeFi protocol, DAO governance, or multi-sig committee that can override an oracle's output, the same structural property applies. The oracle is a signal, not a settlement — the override authority is the settlement.

Distinct from sibling sub-Techniques:

- **T9.006.001 (DVM Vote Capture)** — attacks the oracle layer; T9.006.005 operates above the oracle, at the platform-resolution layer.
- **T9.006.002 (Resolution-Spec Ambiguity Exploitation)** — attacks the spec layer; T9.006.005 operates above the spec — even if the spec is unambiguous, the platform can override.
- **T9.006.003 (Off-chain Resolution-Source Coercion)** — attacks the off-chain data-source layer; T9.006.005 operates entirely on-chain at the platform-governance layer.
- **T9.006.004 (Operational-Insider Trading)** — attacks the information-symmetry assumption; even in the absence of insider information, the platform can override.

## Observed indicators

- Prediction-market platform or DeFi protocol whose Terms of Service or governance documentation includes an "ultimate discretion" or "administrative override" clause for oracle resolution.
- Discrepancy between an oracle's published outcome and the platform's or protocol's actual resolution of the market or settlement.
- Platform governance or administrative action that reverses a decentralised oracle's outcome — the T9.006.005 signature event.
- Public dispute over oracle-vs-platform resolution divergence, with media coverage and bettor community reaction.
- Protocol governance votes or multi-sig transactions that post-hoc adjust or override a previously-recorded oracle outcome.

## Detection signals

- **Pre-market oracle-outcome-enforcement audit.** For every prediction market or DeFi protocol that uses a decentralised oracle: identify the override mechanism. Is there a governance vote, multi-sig, or platform-admin key that can reverse the oracle? If yes, the oracle outcome is a signal, not a settlement — the override mechanism is the settlement authority.
- **Platform-level override-event monitoring.** Monitor governance proposals, multi-sig transactions, and admin-key activity for transactions that reverse or adjust a previously-recorded oracle outcome.
- **Disclosure of override authority to market participants.** Prediction markets and DeFi protocols should disclose, in user-facing language, that the oracle outcome is subject to platform/protocol-governance override and that the override authority is the actual settlement authority.
- **Market-integrity event classification.** Platform-oracle-override events should be classified as T9.006.005 market-integrity events — not as oracle failures or spec failures — because the structural mechanism is platform-level discretion, not oracle-layer or spec-layer fault.

## Real-world examples

- **Polymarket DJT memecoin / Barron Trump oracle-override dispute — 2024-06 (canonical T9.006.005 case).** Polymarket prediction market: "Was Donald Trump involved in the DJT memecoin launch?" UMA's optimistic oracle voted NO — the evidence did not support Trump's involvement. Polymarket overrode the UMA outcome and resolved the market to YES, citing its Terms of Service "ultimate discretion" clause. The case is the canonical worked example establishing that platform-level discretion is a structural backstop on top of any decentralised oracle mechanism. See [`examples/2024-06-polymarket-djt-oracle-override.md`](../examples/2024-06-polymarket-djt-oracle-override.md).

## Mitigations

- **Disclosure of platform-override authority to market participants (highest-leverage).** Prediction-market platforms and DeFi protocols should disclose, in user-facing language, that oracle outcomes are subject to platform-level override. The disclosure should state under what conditions the override authority can be exercised and what recourse market participants have (if any).
- **Platform-override event registry.** Community-maintained registry of platform-override events as market-integrity events — the T9.006.005 event corpus. Enables bettor-side due-diligence and platform-comparison analysis.
- **Oracle-outcome-as-settlement commitment.** Platforms that commit, contractually, to treating the oracle outcome as final settlement (no override) eliminate T9.006.005 at the platform-governance layer. The commitment must be enforceable, not declaratory.
- **Multi-oracle-without-platform-override architecture.** Protocols that use multiple independent oracles with no single platform-level override authority eliminate the single-point-of-platform-discretion failure mode.

OAK Mitigation cross-references at v0.1: gap. T9.006.005 is a platform-governance-layer sub-Technique; the mitigation surface is at the protocol-design and platform-governance-disclosure layers rather than the runtime-detection layer.

## Discussion

**On why T9.006.005 is a standalone sub-Technique rather than a sub-case of T9.006.001.** T9.006.001 (DVM Vote Capture) attacks the oracle layer by corrupting the vote. T9.006.005 operates above the oracle — the platform overrides the oracle's outcome regardless of whether the vote was correctly conducted. Even if every UMA voter voted NO in good faith and the spec was unambiguous, the platform could still override. The structural mechanism (platform discretion vs. oracle corruption) is distinct, and the defender mitigation surface is different (disclosure and governance audit vs. oracle-vote monitoring).

**On the structural generality beyond prediction markets.** Any DeFi protocol that uses a decentralised oracle (Chainlink, UMA, Pyth, Redstone, etc.) for settlement — lending protocols, derivatives platforms, insurance protocols — that includes a governance vote, multi-sig, or admin-key mechanism that can override the oracle's outcome is exposed to T9.006.005. The pattern is: oracle provides a signal → protocol governance overrides the signal → protocol state updates on the overridden value. The DJT case is the cleanest worked example in the public record because the override was publicly documented and contested.

**On the relationship to platform risk.** T9.006.005 is not an "exploit" in the code-exploit sense — no smart contract was hacked, no oracle was corrupted, no spec was ambiguous. It is a platform-governance risk that manifests as a market-integrity event. The inclusion of T9.006.005 in OAK's T9 (Smart-Contract Exploit) Tactic recognises that smart-contract-level oracle integration surfaces are structurally incomplete without characterising the platform-governance layer above them.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once multiple independent worked-example anchors exist beyond the DJT case. Additional platform-override events are expected as prediction-market and DeFi-protocol governance mechanisms exercise and contest their override authority.
