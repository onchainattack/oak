# OAK-T7.002 — CEX Deposit-Address Layering

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T7 (Laundering)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana, Bitcoin, cross-chain
**First documented:** systematic from 2020 onward; characterised at scale in `[chainalysis2024laundering]`
**Aliases:** "deposit layering", "diversified-deposit laundering", "structured CEX off-ramp"

## Description

A class of laundering flows in which proceeds are routed into a centralised exchange (CEX) through many distinct deposit addresses — often nested through intermediary personal wallets in a "hop" pattern — with per-address values calibrated to keep individual deposit amounts below the freeze and reporting thresholds the receiving exchange's compliance posture is calibrated against. From a defender's perspective, T7.002 is a *post-extraction* Technique: the proceeds are already attacker-controlled, and the question is whether the cumulative inflow into one or more CEXes can be detected and frozen before the operator successfully off-ramps to fiat.

T7.002 is structurally distinct from T7.001 (Mixer-Routed Hop) because the compliance surface is different: T7.001 obscures the on-chain trail before reaching any KYCing venue; T7.002 reaches a KYCing venue directly but distributes the inflow to defeat per-deposit-address thresholds. Sophisticated operators frequently chain both — T7.001 for early-stage obfuscation, then T7.002 for the off-ramp.

## Observed indicators

- A single exchange with many distinct deposit addresses receiving inflows whose collective source-cluster is consistent (suggesting a single beneficial owner across many KYC-distinct customer accounts at the exchange).
- Per-deposit values calibrated below typical reporting / freeze thresholds ($1K, $3K, $10K bands documented in `[chainalysis2024laundering]`); spike density just below and just above these thresholds.
- "Hop" pattern: funds enter the exchange via intermediary personal wallets that have no other apparent purpose; the intermediary wallet is funded once, deposits to the exchange, and is then dormant.
- Deposit-address-cluster size growth: a step-function increase in the count of distinct deposit addresses receiving inflows from a single source cluster within a short window.
- Customer-account-creation cohort: time-correlated spike in newly-KYCed customer accounts at the receiving exchange whose first deposit comes from a common source-cluster.

## Detection signals

- Exchange-side compliance: cluster deposit addresses by upstream funding source within a rolling window; aggregate nominally-distinct customer deposits whose upstream source-clusters overlap.
- On-chain (vendor-side, e.g., Chainalysis Reactor, TRM Forensics, Elliptic Investigator): per-cluster aggregate inflow tracking against known illicit-cluster watchlists; alert when a watchlisted cluster reaches a threshold-multiple of cumulative deposit volume across one or more exchanges.
- Threshold-structuring: distribution-shape detection on per-deposit values; alert on bands just below $1K / $3K / $10K reporting thresholds.
- Per-cluster cardinality: alert on source-clusters whose distinct-deposit-address count over rolling windows exceeds a calibrated threshold (the canonical 2023 cohort metric in `[chainalysis2024laundering]` was 109 deposit addresses each receiving >$10M in illicit funds).

## Real-world examples

- **Tian Yinyin / Li Jiadong OFAC case — multi-exchange — 2020-03-02** — Treasury OFAC and DOJ jointly designated and indicted two PRC nationals for laundering ~$100M+ of DPRK-attributed cryptocurrency-exchange-heist proceeds (originating from a 2018 ~$250M exchange compromise) through structured deposits across multiple exchanges. Tian moved >$34M through a newly-added bank account linked to his exchange account, and converted ~$1.4M into prepaid Apple iTunes gift cards usable for further BTC purchase at certain venues. This is the canonical CEX-deposit-layering attribution case in the public record and the reference precedent that subsequent OAK-G01 layering operations are measured against. Sources: `<!-- TODO: cite URL -->` (Treasury press release SM924; DOJ press release).
- **Aggregate scale — multi-chain — 2023** — Per `[chainalysis2024laundering]`, in 2023 alone 109 exchange deposit addresses received over $10M each in illicit cryptocurrency for a collective $3.4B, while 1,425 addresses received over $1M each for a collective $6.7B (~46% of all illicit value received by exchanges that year). The diversification trend (more addresses receiving smaller per-address amounts) is the canonical T7.002 signature at scale.
- **Threshold structuring — 2023** — `[chainalysis2024laundering]` documents distribution-shape spikes just below the $1K, $3K, and $10K reporting thresholds — a textbook T7.002 indicator that compliance teams can use as a calibration target. The TradFi structured-deposit doctrine maps directly.
- **Garantex deposit-pattern enforcement — 2022 -> 2025** — Per `[ofac2022garantex]`, `[doj2025garantex]`, `[treasury2025garantexnetwork]`, `[chainalysis2025garantex]`, and `[trmlabs2025grinex]`, Garantex (sanctioned April 2022, dismantled by international action 2025) was a recurrent T7.002 off-ramp for OAK-G01 and ransomware-attributed flows; the post-2025 Grinex successor pattern continues to exhibit T7.002-style deposit clustering. The Garantex enforcement arc is the canonical regulator-in-the-loop T7.002 case.
- **Atomic Wallet -> exchange off-ramps — multi-chain — 2023-06** — Per `[ellipticatomic2023]`, the ~$100M+ Atomic Wallet extraction (T11.002, OAK-G01) was laundered partially through Garantex via T7.002-style structured deposits; subsequent industry reporting tracked recurring 2024-2025 OAK-G01 deposit-cluster patterns into multiple unnamed exchanges with insufficient compliance posture.

## Mitigations

- **Exchanges (highest-leverage):** integrate per-cluster aggregate inflow tracking from compliance providers; aggregate nominally-distinct customer deposits whose upstream source-clusters overlap; threshold-structuring detection on the deposit-amount distribution; per-customer KYC posture review when aggregate-cluster inflows exceed configured thresholds. See OAK-M27 (Travel Rule + KYC at the privacy-chain boundary) for the canonical mitigation reference.
- **Risk teams (organisational):** maintain illicit-cluster watchlists; cross-reference outbound transfers from organisational treasury wallets against destinations whose downstream cluster shows T7.002 signatures. See OAK-M07 (cross-chain attribution graph) for the upstream attribution surface.
- **Regulators:** ensure structuring-evasion thresholds in cryptocurrency align with established TradFi standards; the documented spikes around $1K / $3K / $10K mirror TradFi structured-deposit doctrine.

## Discussion

T7.002 is OAK's reframing of "exchange off-ramp laundering" as a Technique rather than as an undifferentiated risk class. Per `[chainalysis2024laundering]`, the deposit-address diversification trend has accelerated through 2022-2024 specifically as compliance teams have improved per-deposit-address detection — a defender's improvement at the per-address level induced an operator response at the across-address level. Contributors writing future T7.002 examples should preserve this defender-operator co-evolution dynamic; T7.002 mitigation is not a single intervention but a continuous arms race calibrated against evolving operator distribution-shapes.

**False-positive reasoning.** Per-cluster aggregate-inflow alerts at the exchange compliance layer carry meaningful false-positive cost: institutional liquidity providers, OTC desks, and high-frequency arbitrage clusters legitimately route through many deposit addresses. Effective T7.002 detection couples cluster-cardinality with upstream watchlist attribution rather than treating cardinality alone as evidence. The 2024 Chainalysis cohort metrics (109 addresses each >$10M; 1,425 each >$1M) anchor the *illicit* sub-population once watchlist filtering has been applied.

**Post-OFAC substrate evolution.** As individual deposit-pattern detection has improved, sophisticated cohorts have shifted toward (a) routing through privacy substrates first (T7.001) before T7.002 entry into a less-scrutinised venue, (b) using non-KYC or weak-KYC venues (eXch pre-2025, Garantex pre-dismantling, the Grinex successor pattern), and (c) chaining through cross-chain bridges (T7.003) before settlement. v0.x deferred work: a per-venue compliance-posture scoring rubric that links observed T7.002 cohort cardinality to the venue's documented KYC and Travel Rule posture, calibrated against the `[chainalysis2024laundering]` cohort metrics.
