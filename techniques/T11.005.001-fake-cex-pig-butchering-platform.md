# OAK-T11.005.001 — Fake-CEX / Pig-Butchering Platform

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T11 (Custody and Signing Infrastructure); sub-Technique of OAK-T11.005 (Operator-side Fake-Platform Fraud)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** chain-agnostic (deposit substrate is BTC / ETH / stablecoins on-chain; the fraud "platform" is off-chain operator-controlled UI/database)
**First documented:** 2018–2020 (pig-butchering model emergence); 2023 JPEX Hong Kong case as the highest-profile public enforcement action
**Aliases:** "pig-butchering platform", "fake-CEX", "romance-scam investment platform", "Sha Zhu Pan", "BeurAx-class platform", "unlicensed-offshore-exchange fraud"

## Description

A **fake centralised exchange** (or fake trading / investment platform) that has no real exchange backend. The balance display, "trade" functionality, profit/loss dashboard, and customer-support interface are entirely UI-side/database fictions generated by the operator. Users deposit real cryptocurrency (BTC / ETH / USDT / USDC) to operator-controlled wallets believing they are depositing to a legitimate exchange. Withdrawals are either blocked outright or gated behind escalating "tax" / "verification fee" / "anti-money-laundering deposit" preconditions that never result in a completed withdrawal.

The pig-butchering (Sha Zhu Pan) narrative model is the dominant user-acquisition surface: the victim is socially engineered through a romance-scam or friendship-scam feeder narrative (typically via dating apps, social media, or messaging platforms) to "invest" in the fake platform. The platform displays fictitious profits to induce escalating deposits (the "fattening" phase) until the victim attempts a large withdrawal, at which point the platform blocks it (the "slaughter").

T11.005.001 is structurally distinct from sibling sub-Techniques:

- **T11.005.001** — The platform is a fake exchange with no real backend. The fraud vehicle is the exchange/trading platform UI.
- **T11.005.002** (Fake-custodian / Fake-asset-manager) — The fraud vehicle is a fake custodial wallet or "AI-trading" yield product; the pitch is wealth management, not exchange trading.
- **T11.005.003** (Compound-operated Investment-Fraud Platforms) — The fake-CEX pattern distributed at industrial scale through forced-labour scam compounds in Southeast Asia.

The distinction from adjacent OAK Techniques:

- **T4 phishing** — phishing acquires access to a real user's holdings on a real platform. T11.005.001 users deposit voluntarily into an operator-controlled wallet they believe is a legitimate exchange.
- **T1.x rug-pull tokens** — token-side fraud. T11.005.001 has no token, no AMM pool, no on-chain protocol; the deposit destination is a single operator wallet.

## Observed indicators

- **Off-chain:** no verifiable corporate registration in the claimed jurisdiction; no audit; no proof-of-reserves; high-yield "guaranteed return" claims; no public order book; no observable on-chain trading flow consistent with claimed AUM; brand-impersonation of a legitimate exchange.
- **On-chain:** deposit-aggregation pattern into a single or small number of operator wallets without observable matched-trading outflow; large fan-in concentration.
- **User-side:** un-fillable withdrawal requests; "tax" / "verification fee" / "anti-money-laundering deposit" preconditions for withdrawal; communication-channel collapse after exit.
- **Romance-scam feeder narrative:** victim met the "investment advisor" / "romantic partner" through a dating app or social media platform and was directed to the platform.

## Detection signals

- **Regulator-warning subscription:** FCA, SEC, KNF, SFC, BaFin, ASIC published warnings on unlicensed platforms.
- **Licensed-VASP registry cross-reference:** verify the platform appears in the claimed jurisdiction's published licensed-VASP registry before depositing.
- **Small-test-withdrawal-before-deposit invariant:** any platform from which a small test withdrawal cannot be completed before depositing more is a fake-CEX.
- **On-chain forensics:** deposit-aggregation-without-trading-outflow signal; cross-cluster overlap with Southeast Asian compound laundering substrate (Huione Group).
- **Retail-bank-side debit-card monitoring:** for known scam-platform deposit destinations (FBI Operation Level Up model).

## Mitigations

- **Regulator-side:** publication and maintenance of licensed-VASP registries; cross-jurisdiction warning-feed coordination.
- **User-side:** small-test-withdrawal-before-deposit invariant; cross-reference against published licensed-VASP registries; refusal to deposit to platforms without verifiable corporate registration / audit / proof-of-reserves.
- **Retail-bank-side:** debit-card monitoring for known scam-platform deposit destinations (FBI Operation Level Up coordination model — 5,831+ victims notified, $359M+ saved by April 2025).
- **Off-chain CTI integration:** subscription to regulator-warning feeds; investigative-journalism cross-reference; corporate-registry verification automation.

**Canonical mitigations:** OAK-M18, OAK-M21, OAK-M30.

## Discussion

T11.005.001 is the **structurally largest retail-aggregate-loss sub-Technique** in the OAK taxonomy at v0.1. FBI IC3 reported $5.8B in pig-butchering-class losses in the U.S. alone in 2024. The fake-CEX / pig-butchering model aggregates as the single largest retail-attack class in crypto by aggregated dollar loss — larger than all bridge exploits, lending-protocol hacks, and custody-exchange hacks combined in retail-victim-count terms.

The sub-Technique's detection-and-mitigation surface lives primarily at the regulator / retail-bank / off-chain-OSINT layer rather than the on-chain forensics layer that anchors most other OAK Techniques. This off-chain centricity is the reason the sub-Technique was initially held in TAXONOMY-GAPS rather than promoted to canonical at v0.1: earlier OAK drafts treated "off-chain" attack surfaces as out-of-scope. The promotion to `emerging` at v0.1 reflects that the class is multi-source-attested (DOJ, SEC, FBI IC3, FinCEN, OFAC) and that the structural distinction from on-chain-centric T4 phishing and T1.x rug-pull tokens is clean.

The **romance-scam feeder narrative** is the defining user-acquisition surface for the pig-butchering era (2020–2026), distinguishing T11.005.001 from earlier fake-exchange frauds that relied on cold-call boiler-room or email-spam acquisition. The feeder narrative's conversion rate and per-victim extraction size (often six-to-seven figures) explain the industrial scale at which the pattern operates. Contributors writing future T11.005.001 worked examples should document the feeder narrative explicitly as a first-class observable.
