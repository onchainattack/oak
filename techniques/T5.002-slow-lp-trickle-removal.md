# OAK-T5.002 — Slow LP Trickle Removal

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T5 (Value Extraction), OAK-T6 (Defense Evasion)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM, Solana
**First documented:** 2024–2026 (TM-RugPull research, 2026; Tran et al. 2025 Fragmented Rug Pull)
**Aliases:** "slow rug", "trickle drain", "patient drain", "fragmented rug pull (FRP)"

## Description

A pattern in which the LP-controlling party removes liquidity gradually over an extended window rather than in a single event, defeating threshold-based real-time detectors that fire only on large single-event withdrawals. From a defender's perspective, the Technique is a deliberate counter to T5.001-style detectors: each withdrawal individually falls below per-transaction-impact thresholds, but the cumulative outflow over a calibrated window is a substantial fraction of original LP. Detection therefore requires **windowed cumulative** analysis rather than per-transaction thresholds, and **multi-actor cluster aggregation** rather than single-address watching. T5.002 is the canonical T6 (Defense Evasion) modifier on T5 (Value Extraction): the underlying extraction motive is identical to T5.001, and the technique-shift exists only to evade per-transaction detectors.

The Tran et al. 2025 Fragmented Rug Pull formalisation describes the operator recipe as: (i) "keep the lid on" — preserve LP control so on-chain extraction remains feasible; (ii) "chop thin slices" — split total exit volume into many low-impact micro-trades each below detector thresholds; (iii) "pass the ladle" — delegate sells across multiple wallets so each participant takes a small share of the extraction. The paper labels 105,434 of 303,614 deployed pools (Nov 2018 – Mar 2025) as FRP, indicating the cohort is materially large and not a long-tail edge case.

## Observed indicators

- Sustained net-outflow from LP-control cluster over a window of hours to weeks, with each individual outflow below typical alerting thresholds.
- Cumulative outflow eventually reaching a substantial fraction (>20–40%) of original liquidity over a rolling multi-day window.
- Outflow pattern that alternates with small inflows, complicating naive net-flow tracking.
- Multi-wallet "ladle" pattern: extraction routed through many non-deployer wallets that share funder-cluster lineage (OAK-M04).
- Pool-depth time series shows monotonic-ish decline against a baseline volatility envelope, distinguishable from organic LP rebalance.

## Detection signals

- Windowed cumulative net-flow per LP-control cluster over rolling windows (e.g., 24h, 7d, 30d); threshold on cumulative fraction of original LP at launch baseline.
- Trend detection on pool-depth time series independent of per-transaction sizes — fit a linear / EWMA decay against baseline volatility.
- Cluster-aggregated extraction: union all wallets in the deployer's funder-graph (OAK-M04) and apply the windowed-cumulative threshold against the union, not per-wallet.
- Cross-correlate with off-chain operator behaviour signals (social media inactivity onset, domain expiry, governance-forum disengagement) as additional features per `[fullycryptosoftrug]`.

## Real-world examples

- **Fragmented Rug Pull (FRP) cohort — multi-chain — 2018–2025** — Tran et al. 2025 (`[frp2025]`) labels 105,434 pools across 303,614 candidates as FRP, involving 34.19M transactions, 401,838 inflated-seller wallets, and 1.50M unique interacting addresses. The paper's labels are the canonical reference cohort; per-incident named cases inside the cohort are not individually called out by the authors at the per-token level. OAK v0.1 cites the cohort as the canonical FRP source; contributors with public forensic write-ups of individual FRP-class tokens are encouraged to submit named-incident PRs for v0.2.
- **SafeMoon (V1 / V2 / V3 lineage) — BNB Chain — 2021–2023** — `[secsafemoon2023]` documents the SEC's case characterising the project's tokenomics (10% transfer tax, dev wallet allocation, V1→V2 1000:1 migration) as supporting sustained deployer-side extraction over a multi-quarter window. The "reflection tax" structure means each transfer constitutes a small extraction routed to dev/marketing wallets — structurally a slow-trickle pattern even where individual events are below T5.001-class thresholds. v0.1 treats SafeMoon as a transition case between T1.005 (hidden fee-on-transfer) and T5.002 (slow accumulation of extraction at the dev-wallet); the SEC complaint is the canonical primary source.
- **Polywhale Finance (KRILL) — Polygon — 2021-06** — Loss \~\$1M+; treasury-wallet drained from \~5M KRILL to \~1.6M KRILL over the run-up to the abandonment, with \~1.04M routed to the dev wallet, with KRILL trading from \~\$1.50 to \~\$0.13. - Public detectors — limited; FRP-class detection requires windowed cumulative metrics that most v0.1 commercial vendors do not yet expose

## Mitigations

- **OAK-M11 (rate limiting / per-block caps):** at the venue layer, cap per-cluster LP-removal velocity such that even a "thin slice" strategy hits the cap once aggregated across the deployer cluster — converts T5.002 back into an observable T5.001-shaped event the detector can see.
- **OAK-M04 (funder-graph clustering):** mandatory for T5.002 detection — without cluster-level aggregation, the "pass the ladle" stage trivially evades single-address watchers.
- **OAK-M17 (time-locked governance and multi-block quorum):** for protocols with governance-controlled LP, time-lock all LP-removal proposals so any extraction is observable in advance of execution.
- **Traders / risk teams:** monitor cumulative pool-depth change relative to launch baseline rather than relying on transaction-level alerts.
- **Detection engineers:** implement windowed cumulative metrics in addition to per-transaction thresholds; aggregate at the deployer-cluster level not the address level.

## Discussion

T5.002 is documented as a **gap** in v0.1 reference implementations. Two open calibration problems remain at v0.x: (1) **window-size selection** — too short and organic LP rebalance triggers false positives; too long and the detector observes the rug only in retrospect. The Tran et al. 2025 cohort suggests 24h-to-7d windows are the most informative band, but per-chain calibration is required (memecoin Solana cycles are faster than BSC yield-farm cycles). (2) **cumulative-fraction threshold** — the natural threshold (>X% of original LP removed within window Y) is sensitive to launch-time LP baseline, which itself can be spoofed via T2.001 (single-sided liquidity plant) or T2.002 (locked-liquidity spoof). Robust calibration requires combining the cumulative-fraction signal with a baseline-quality score (i.e., is the "100% baseline" actually meaningful), which is why OAK-M04 funder-graph clustering and OAK-M05 authority-graph enumeration appear as upstream prerequisites in the mitigation stack rather than parallel options. The boundary with T1.005 (hidden fee-on-transfer) needs sharpening: tax-routed extraction (SafeMoon-style) is technically a continuous-trickle pattern but is currently scoped to T1.005 because the extraction primitive lives in transfer-mechanics rather than LP-removal-mechanics; v0.2 should clarify whether transfer-tax extraction is a third sub-Technique under T5 or remains an OAK-T1.005-with-T5-effect cross-reference.
