# OAK-T9.006.001 — DVM Vote Capture by Economically-Interested Holder

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T9 (Smart-Contract Exploit), OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation); sub-Technique of OAK-T9.006 (Subjective-Oracle Resolution Manipulation)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (Polymarket on Polygon / UMA DVM on Ethereum canonical at v0.1)
**First documented:** 2025-03 (Polymarket Ukraine mineral deal)
**Aliases:** "UMA whale vote capture", "DVM governance attack", "oracle-vote corruption"

## Description

Direct corruption of the subjective-oracle layer by a tokenholder cohort with material positions in the adjudicated market. A whale or coordinated tokenholder group votes their UMA / Kleros / Reality.eth / Augur REP holdings to drive resolution to the outcome that maximises their bettor-side payoff, against the underlying real-world signal.

The structural feature is the **voter–bettor identity overlap**: subjective-oracle protocols typically permit any tokenholder to vote on any market regardless of whether they hold positions in that market. Classical oracle-defence assumptions (voter neutrality) are structurally absent.

The **stable disequilibrium** between oracle market cap and adjudicated market volume is the load-bearing substrate. UMA's market cap was approximately \$95M during the canonical 2025–2026 dispute window; subsequent disputed markets (Zelenskyy-suit at \$237M) had volume larger than the oracle token's market cap. The economic cost of corrupting the vote is a fraction of the prize.

Distinct from sibling sub-Techniques:

- **T9.006.002 (Resolution-Spec Ambiguity Exploitation)** — attacks the spec layer rather than the oracle layer. Voters in T9.006.002 may vote consistent with one reasonable interpretation of the spec; T9.006.001 voters vote *against* the resolution criteria entirely.
- **T9.006.003 (Off-chain Resolution-Source Coercion)** — attacks the resolution-input layer (the off-chain reporter); T9.006.001 attacks the oracle layer directly.
- **T9.006.004 (Operational-Insider Trading)** — attacks the information-symmetry assumption between bettors; T9.006.001 attacks the oracle's resolution decision.

Distinct from **T9.003 (Governance Attack)** at the artefact level: T9.003 covers protocol-governance over parameters / treasury / upgrades; T9.006.001 covers oracle-resolution governance over external real-world facts.

## Observed indicators

- Sharp resolution-window probability movement (e.g., YES surge from <10% to 100%) inconsistent with the underlying real-world signal.
- Voter-position-correlation: top-N UMA / Kleros / REP voters with material positions in the adjudicated market.
- Single-whale or small-cluster tokenholder concentration capturing a meaningful fraction (>20%) of resolution vote weight.
- Public characterisation from the host platform that the resolution was "incorrect" / "unprecedented governance attack" — the platform's own admissions are first-class detection signals.

## Detection signals

- Voter-position correlation analysis across the UMA DVM (or Kleros / Reality.eth / Augur REP) vote logs against bettor-side position holdings on the adjudicated market.
- Single-whale or cluster-attribution analysis of large UMA holders during the resolution window.
- Cohort-level monitoring of disputed-market volume against UMA market cap as a structural threshold.
- Cross-reference with Polymarket / host-platform public statements regarding disputed resolutions.

## Mitigations

- **Voter-position-disclosure with conflict-of-interest slashing:** voters with positions in the adjudicated market should be excluded or slashable. UMA does not currently enforce this. Highest-leverage mitigation for T9.006.001.
- **Oracle-weight floor relative to adjudicated volume:** UMA-class oracle should refuse to adjudicate markets whose volume exceeds some fraction of the oracle token's market cap. Structural change to UMA, not Polymarket-side mitigation.
- **Machine-checkable resolution criteria** (parent-Technique mitigation): markets resolving on cryptographically-verifiable inputs are not exposed to T9.006.001.

## Discussion

T9.006.001 is the canonical *first* of the now-documented Polymarket cohort of subjective-oracle resolution-manipulation cases. The Ukraine mineral deal case is structurally significant because Polymarket itself publicly conceded the resolution was "incorrect" and described the event as an "unprecedented governance attack" — but, citing UMA's decentralised structure, refused refunds. This is the strongest available admission that the oracle layer is not mechanically defensible against economically-rational voters.

The voting-pool-vs-prize-pool disequilibrium is a **stable property** of any subjective oracle adjudicating economically-meaningful prediction markets. The larger Polymarket grows, the more profitable each individual oracle-corruption opportunity becomes, while the cost of acquiring veto-equivalent UMA voting power stays bounded by UMA's market cap. This is qualitatively different from price-oracle manipulation (T9.001), where the manipulation cost scales with pool depth and the attacker must continuously fund a position. T9.006.001 manipulation is a one-time governance vote with a long-tail bounty.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once T9.006.001 carries multiple independent worked-example anchors beyond the canonical Ukraine mineral deal and adjacent Zelenskyy-suit / UFO-declassification cohort cases. The pipeline includes any 2026+ disputed market where voter-position correlation analysis reproduces the cohort signature.
