# OAK-T1.001 — Modifiable Tax Function

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T1 (Token Genesis), OAK-T6 (Defense Evasion)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana
**First documented:** 2018 (academic), widespread by 2020
**Aliases:** "honeypot tax", "trap tax", "anti-sell tax", "tax trap"

## Description

A token whose transfer logic includes a fee or restriction parameter that an authorised role can modify after holders have entered. From a defender's perspective, the issue is the **post-launch authority surface**, not the tax itself: a static, immutable fee is observable and pricable; a fee that can be raised arbitrarily after holders enter is a concealed authority that defeats normal pre-trade risk assessment.

T1.001 is structurally distinct from T1.005 (Hidden Fee-on-Transfer) along the mutability axis: T1.005 fixes the fee in immutable bytecode but conditions it on caller / counterparty / size / time predicates; T1.001 leaves the fee parameter itself authority-mutable. The two patterns frequently co-occur on the same deployer-cluster footprint and are jointly responsible for the bulk of "honeypot" classifications in commodity vendor scoring.

## Observed indicators

- Public setter on the fee state variable (e.g. `setTaxRate(uint256)`, `setBuyFee`, `setSellFee`, `updateFees`, `setMaxTxAmount`) gated by `onlyOwner` / `Ownable` / a custom `onlyAdmin` modifier with no time-lock and no governance gate.
- Storage layout: a non-immutable fee state variable (e.g. `uint256 public _taxRate`) writable from the setter — verify with `eth_getStorageAt` against the resolved storage slot rather than relying on the source-level type.
- Owner key clustered with deployment-related funding sources (M04: funder-graph) rather than a published multisig or timelock.
- An anti-whale / max-transfer-amount parameter (`_maxTxAmount`, `maxWalletAmount`) that can be lowered to a value smaller than typical buy size, achieving sell-prevention through a quantitative gate (compare with T1.004 which uses a binary gate).
- `OwnershipTransferred(_, 0x000...0)` event absent, OR present but paired with a separate authority-bearing role (`Ownable2Step` pending owner, `AccessControl` admin role, fee-collector contract authority) that was not included in the renouncement claim — combine with T1.003.
- EIP-1967 admin slot (`0xb53127684a568b3173ae13b9f8a6016e243e63b6e8ee1178d6a717850b5d6103`) reads non-zero on a contract that also exposes a fee setter — proxy-mutable tax.
- Source-verified contract whose verified source omits a `setFee` selector that is present in the deployed bytecode (combines with T6.001).

## Detection signals

- **Static, pre-trade:** disassemble the runtime bytecode and enumerate every public/external selector that writes to the fee storage slot; flag any writer not gated by a known timelock or governance contract. A Forta / Tenderly / Slither rule can be derived directly: "function with `SSTORE` to a slot that is `SLOAD`-ed inside the `_transfer` arithmetic, callable from a non-timelock address."
- **Bytecode diff:** for source-verified contracts, diff the canonical compiled bytecode against the deployed runtime; flag any extra fee-mutating selector — this is M03 in operation.
- **Simulation matrix:** simulate `transfer` and `transferFrom` calls under (small / large) amounts × (EOA-to-EOA / EOA-to-pool / pool-to-EOA) directions; record the executed fee. Then re-simulate after fast-forwarding state with a synthetic `setFee(MAX)` call from the historical owner — if the simulation succeeds and the post-call simulated fee jumps, T1.001 is confirmed.
- **Indexer query (Dune / Allium / Goldsky):** subscribe to `OwnershipTransferred` and to any token-emitted `FeeChanged(uint256)` / `TaxUpdated(uint256)` events; alert when a fee-change event fires from an EOA that is not on a known-timelock allowlist.
- **Cross-token cluster:** group deployers via funder-graph (M04); if a deployer has previously deployed contracts with the same fee-setter signature and those tokens later experienced a fee-spike-then-LP-drain pattern, treat new deployments from the cluster as elevated-risk regardless of current fee value.

## Real-world examples

- **SQUID (Squid Game token) — BNB Chain — 2021-11-01** — Loss approximately $3.38M direct (TRM Labs attribution links the same operator cluster to two earlier scams totalling approximately 35,025 BNB / approximately $19.3M cumulative). The token's transfer logic required holders to first earn a secondary "Marbles" token, never distributed to ordinary buyers — a transfer-restriction variant of the modifiable-tax pattern (the restriction predicate being a settable allowlist rather than a numerical fee). Forensic write-up: `[trmsquid2021]`.
- **Save the Kids ($KIDS) — BNB Chain — 2021-06-05** — A Faze-Clan-promoted memecoin whose contract included a 24-hour anti-whale cap (max-sell percent of holdings) that was modified immediately before trading went live: the documented setter call lowered the 24-hour window to 1 minute, converting the cap into an effective rate-throttle that the promoter cluster could front-run while retail holders were dumped on. The KIDS post-mortem is the canonical small-cap modifiable-cap-as-tax case study; the publicly-reportable framing was "pump and dump" but the upstream primitive is T1.001. <!-- TODO: cite https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Save_the_Kids_token and Coffeezilla's contract analysis (https://protos.com/save-the-kids-faze-clan-kay-coffeezilla-proves-rug-pulls/), add bib entries `wikisavethekids` / `protossavethekids` -->
- **AnubisDAO (ANKH) — Ethereum — 2021-10-29** — Approximately 13,597 ETH (~$60M at launch) drained roughly 20 hours after the launch sale via LP-token cash-out. While the headline incident is T5.001 (Hard LP Drain), the underlying contract and the absence of a multisig or timelocked authority are the T1.001 / T1.003 conditions that made the drain trivial; AnubisDAO is canonical for the "single-developer key with mutable controls" cohort. Subsequent laundering via Tornado Cash analysed in `[cointelegraphanubismixer2022]`. Forensic context: `[decryptanubis2021]`.
- **Honeypot.is / Token Sniffer cohort, 2022 onward** — At cohort scale, modifiable-tax-with-anti-sell-spike is the highest-recall single signal across commodity vendor scanners; per-quarter cohort retrospectives in `[chainalysis2025rug]` and `[slowmist2024report]` track thousands of T1.001 deployments per quarter, with a long tail of named tokens (typically referenced only by ticker / contract address rather than a brand name).

## Mitigations

- **OAK-M02 (Static-Analysis Pre-Deployment)** — required: enumerate fee-mutating selectors and flag the contract before listing.
- **OAK-M01 (Source-Bytecode Verification)** — required: any setter present in deployed bytecode but missing from verified source is an immediate T6.001 / T1.001 combined indicator.
- **OAK-M03 (Continuous Bytecode-Diff Monitoring)** — for proxy-pattern tokens, monitor implementation upgrades for newly-introduced fee-setter selectors.
- **OAK-M05 (Authority-Graph Enumeration)** — enumerate every authority-bearing role (Ownable owner, AccessControl admins, proxy admin, fee-collector authority) before treating any single-role renouncement as conclusive.
- **OAK-M17 (Time-Locked Governance and Multi-Block Quorum)** — accept governance-gated fee mutation only when the timelock is non-trivial and quorum is multi-block.
- **OAK-M25 (Listing-Time Source-Verification + Audit-Status Gate)** — venue / launchpad gate.
- **OAK-M16 (Pre-Deployment Audit and Formal Verification)** — for tokens claiming non-mutable fees, require formal verification of the immutability claim.

Operational: traders / risk teams treat any modifiable-fee token as elevated risk regardless of advertised current rate; require immutable fee guarantees or short, hard-capped trading windows. Wallet-vendor pre-trade UX must surface a concrete numeric upper-bound on the fee, not just a present/absent flag.

## Discussion

Open question: how should the framework distinguish modifiable fees with legitimate governance gating (timelocked DAO control with non-trivial delay and multi-sig executor) from unilateral owner control? Proposal in v0.2 to introduce a `Maturity: governance-gated` sub-classification analogous to the proposal under T1.005.

Calibration gap at v0.1: the boundary between T1.001 (fee parameter is mutable) and T1.005 (fee is immutable but conditionally applied) collapses in practice when an immutable predicate-gated fee uses a *mutable* membership check (e.g. an `_isExcludedFromFee` mapping settable by owner). Such cases are filed under T1.001 in v0.1 because the operator-controllable surface is the dominant signal, but contributors should expect a sub-Technique split in v0.x.

Attribution caveat: a fee setter callable by a published, governance-controlled multisig is *not* T1.001 in spirit — it is a normal protocol-parameter authority. v0.1 detection rules will produce false positives in this regime; the pragmatic resolution is M05 (Authority-Graph Enumeration) plus an allowlist of known governance contracts, not a different static rule.
