# OAK-T14.003.001 — LST/LRT Depeg-Cascade as Constrained-Primitive Sub-class

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T14 (Validator / Staking / Restaking Attacks); sub-Technique of OAK-T14.003 (Restaking Cascading Risk)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** Ethereum L1 canonical (Lido stETH; Renzo ezETH); EVM L2s and L1s with LST / LRT collateral integration into lending markets
**First documented:** 2022-05/06 (Lido stETH cascade, pre-Shapella, chain-level redemption-absence sub-class); 2024-04 (Renzo ezETH cascade, operator-blocked redemption sub-class); 2025-07 (Lido stETH / Aave / Justin-Sun-driven cascade, withdrawal-queue-depth saturation sub-class)
**Aliases:** "stETH depeg cascade", "ezETH depeg cascade", "LRT depeg cascade", "constrained-redemption depeg", "looped-leverage liquidation cascade"

## Description

The depeg-and-liquidation cascade pattern within T14.003 (b) (Liquid-restaking-token depeg cascade) operates through **three constrained-primitive sub-classes**, each firing through a structurally-different constrained primitive but sharing the cascade structure:

1. **T14.003.001(i) — Chain-level redemption-absence.** The underlying staking primitive does not yet support direct redemption at the protocol layer. Secondary-market sell pressure has no clearing path to underlying-stake withdrawal; the LST / LRT must clear via secondary-market liquidity alone. Canonical: 2022-05/06 Lido stETH cascade pre-Shapella — direct stETH-to-ETH redemption was not yet enabled at the Beacon Chain layer, so all sell pressure had to clear via Curve / Uniswap pools. See [`examples/2022-06-lido-steth-depeg.md`](../examples/2022-06-lido-steth-depeg.md).

2. **T14.003.001(ii) — Operator-blocked redemption.** The redemption primitive exists at the protocol level but is gated by an operator-side decision (deposit cap + withdrawal disable; multi-week unbonding window with no early-exit primitive). Secondary-market sell pressure cannot exit through the operator-controlled gate; the LRT must clear via secondary-market liquidity. Canonical: 2024-04 Renzo ezETH cascade — Renzo's `restakeMint` deposit-side function was active while withdrawal-side primitive was disabled, producing a one-way valve that propagated into Morpho / Gearbox / Pendle looped-leverage liquidations. See [`examples/2024-04-renzo-ezeth-depeg.md`](../examples/2024-04-renzo-ezeth-depeg.md).

3. **T14.003.001(iii) — Withdrawal-queue-depth saturation.** The redemption primitive exists and is unblocked at the operator level but is bounded by per-epoch / per-period throughput. Sell-pressure scenarios that exceed throughput capacity produce queue-clearing-time saturation; secondary-market sell pressure clears partially through the queue and partially via secondary-market liquidity, with the secondary-market discount widening as the queue depth saturates. Canonical: 2025-07 Lido stETH / Aave validator-exit-queue / Justin-Sun-driven cascade — the Beacon Chain validator-exit-queue throughput was the binding constraint; Aave-collateralised stETH positions liquidated as the secondary-market discount widened past LTV thresholds. See [`examples/2025-07-lido-steth-aave-cascade.md`](../examples/2025-07-lido-steth-aave-cascade.md).

All three sub-classes share the **cascade structure**:

```
secondary-market sell pressure
  → bottlenecked redemption (constrained primitive saturates)
  → lending-market oracle propagation (DEX-spot-anchored oracles propagate the secondary-market discount)
  → looped-leverage liquidation cascade
  → further secondary-market sell pressure (positive-feedback amplification)
```

The cascade fires through a *different constrained primitive* in each sub-class — chain-level redemption-absence (i), operator-blocked redemption (ii), withdrawal-queue-depth saturation (iii) — but the downstream amplification path through DEX-spot-anchored lending-market oracles into looped-leverage liquidation cascades is shared.

T14.003.001 is **structurally distinct** from sibling T14.003 sub-cases:

- **T14.003 (a) AVS-design slashing-cascade** — cross-AVS slashing propagation through shared-stake operator concentration. T14.003.001 fires through redemption-primitive saturation, not through cross-AVS slashing.
- **T14.003 (c) Operator concentration risk** — operator-level compromise simultaneously failing every AVS the operator secures. T14.003.001 has no operator-compromise dimension; the failure is constrained-primitive saturation.
- **T14.003 (d) Slashing-as-economic-attack** — adversarial stake-as-weapon. T14.003.001's cascades are typically non-adversarial (operator-side capacity constraints; benign user-side sell pressure that exceeds the constrained primitive's throughput).
- **T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation)** — DEX-spot-anchored oracle manipulation. T14.003.001 *uses* DEX-spot-anchored oracles as the load-bearing amplification path but does not require any oracle manipulation; the secondary-market discount is the legitimate price-discovery output of the constrained-redemption pressure.

## Observed indicators

- **LST / LRT trading discount:** persistent secondary-market discount of the LST / LRT against its 1:1 reference asset (ETH for stETH, ezETH); discount widens as the constrained primitive saturates.
- **Constrained-primitive saturation signal:** redemption queue depth at the operator's exit-queue / unbonding window; deposit-side activity continuing while withdrawal-side primitive is disabled (Renzo ezETH operator-blocked sub-class); validator-exit-queue throughput saturation at the Beacon Chain layer (post-Shapella stETH withdrawal-queue-depth saturation sub-class); secondary-market liquidity exhaustion in the Curve / Uniswap LST / LRT / ETH pools.
- **Lending-market oracle propagation:** Aave / Compound / Morpho / Gearbox / Pendle position-value reductions tracking the secondary-market discount via DEX-spot-anchored oracle reads; LTV ratio breaches across looped-leverage positions.
- **Looped-leverage liquidation cascade:** liquidation events on lending markets accepting the LST / LRT as collateral; secondary-market sell pressure from liquidation outflows further widening the discount (positive-feedback amplification).

## Detection signals

- **Constrained-primitive depth/capacity monitoring (highest-leverage):** monitor the relevant constrained primitive's current depth/capacity — chain-level redemption-availability flag (sub-class i); operator-side withdrawal enable/disable status (sub-class ii); withdrawal-queue-depth and per-period throughput (sub-class iii).
- **Cascade-demand-saturation modelling:** for each constrained primitive, model the cascade-demand scenario that would saturate it — what secondary-market sell-pressure rate, what looped-leverage liquidation rate, what AVS-slashing-correlated sell rate would saturate the queue or exhaust secondary-market liquidity.
- **Queue-clearing-time-under-saturation:** estimate the time to clear the queue under saturation — for sub-class (iii), this is the validator-exit-queue throughput per day divided by the queued exit volume; for sub-class (ii), this is the operator's commitment to re-enable withdrawals; for sub-class (i), this is the protocol-roadmap commitment to enable redemption.
- **Lending-market LRT-collateral exposure:** for each lending market, track total LST / LRT collateral in absolute and percentage terms; estimate liquidation cascade size for a 5%, 10%, 20% LST / LRT depeg using current oracle methodology, LTV, and liquidity-fragmentation parameters.
- **Cross-cascade correlation:** monitor correlation between LST / LRT depeg traces and Beacon Chain or operator-side capacity-constraint events; a depeg-without-corresponding-capacity-saturation suggests an alternative cause (e.g., adversarial T14.003(d)) and warrants separate analysis.

## Real-world examples

- **Lido stETH depeg and Aave / Curve looped-leverage liquidation cascade — Ethereum — May–June 2022 (canonical T14.003.001(i) chain-level redemption-absence anchor)** — Pre-Shapella, direct stETH-to-ETH redemption at the Beacon Chain layer was not yet enabled. Three Arrows Capital and Celsius Network looped-leverage stETH positions on Aave produced cascade-liquidation pressure as the stETH-to-ETH peg widened. Secondary-market clearing via the Curve stETH/ETH pool was the binding constraint; the pool's liquidity exhaustion produced peg discount that propagated into Aave liquidation-thresholds via DEX-spot-anchored oracle reads. The cascade is documented as the canonical pre-Shapella T14.003 (b) anchor and is the structural-precedent for the 2024–2025 LRT / LST cascade cohort. See [`examples/2022-06-lido-steth-depeg.md`](../examples/2022-06-lido-steth-depeg.md).

- **Renzo ezETH liquid-restaking-token depeg and looped-leverage cascade — Ethereum — 2024-04-24 (canonical T14.003.001(ii) operator-blocked redemption anchor)** — ~$56M+ across 250+ borrowers liquidated. Renzo's `restakeMint` deposit-side function was active while the withdrawal-side primitive was disabled — producing a one-way valve where users could deposit ETH and mint ezETH but could not redeem ezETH for ETH at the protocol layer. The operator-blocked redemption forced all secondary-market sell pressure to clear via the Curve / Uniswap ezETH/ETH pool; the pool's depth was insufficient to absorb the liquidation-driven sell pressure and the discount widened past Morpho / Gearbox / Pendle LTV thresholds, producing the looped-leverage liquidation cascade. See [`examples/2024-04-renzo-ezeth-depeg.md`](../examples/2024-04-renzo-ezeth-depeg.md).

- **Lido stETH / Aave validator-exit-queue and looped-leverage cascade — Ethereum — 2025-07 (canonical T14.003.001(iii) withdrawal-queue-depth saturation anchor)** — Post-Shapella, direct stETH-to-ETH redemption at the Beacon Chain layer was enabled, but the validator-exit-queue throughput was the binding constraint. Justin Sun-driven sell pressure plus secondary-market liquidation outflows queued exit demand beyond the Beacon Chain's per-epoch throughput capacity. The queue-clearing-time saturation produced secondary-market discount widening past LTV thresholds; Aave-collateralised stETH positions liquidated as the secondary-market discount propagated into Aave's oracle reads. The cascade is the canonical post-Shapella T14.003 (b) anchor and demonstrates that protocol-level redemption-availability is necessary but not sufficient — per-period throughput is the binding constraint. See [`examples/2025-07-lido-steth-aave-cascade.md`](../examples/2025-07-lido-steth-aave-cascade.md).

## Mitigations

- **LRT-issuer redemption-primitive design (highest-leverage):** prefer protocol-level redemption-primitive availability with operator-side commitments to maintain withdrawal-side primitive enabled in parallel with deposit-side; publish per-epoch / per-period throughput parameters explicitly; avoid the deposit-side-active-while-withdrawal-side-disabled one-way-valve pattern (the Renzo ezETH sub-class (ii) anti-pattern).
- **Lending-market oracle methodology:** treat LST / LRT collateral as a distinct risk class from the underlying staking asset, with oracle methodology that incorporates redemption-rate / queue-depth-aware buffer rather than only DEX-spot prices. Cross-references T9.001: DEX-spot-anchored lending-market oracles propagate the secondary-market discount into liquidation triggers without redemption-rate / queue-depth-aware buffer.
- **Lending-market LST / LRT-collateral LTV caps:** separate LTV ceiling for LST / LRT collateral; isolated-pool listing; aggregate-LRT-collateral cap as a fraction of pool size; required additional buffer for tail-risk depeg scenarios.
- **Constrained-primitive depth/capacity disclosure:** LRT issuers should publish withdrawal-queue depth and per-epoch / per-period throughput explicitly; integrators should treat aggregate-stake claims as upper bounds rather than as security budgets when redemption-primitive saturation is the binding constraint.
- **Looped-leverage circuit-breakers:** lending markets accepting LST / LRT collateral should implement liquidation-cascade circuit-breakers — pause-and-review thresholds when LST / LRT secondary-market discount exceeds a configurable band, allowing manual review before automated liquidations propagate the cascade.

OAK Mitigation cross-references at v0.1 are a **gap** — no existing OAK-M* entry directly maps to constrained-primitive depth disclosure or queue-depth-aware oracle methodology. v0.x candidate Mitigation entries: "OAK-M-x LRT redemption-primitive design"; "OAK-M-x queue-depth-aware lending-market oracle methodology"; "OAK-M-x LST/LRT looped-leverage liquidation circuit-breakers".

## Discussion

T14.003.001 closes a structural gap in OAK v0.1's T14.003 coverage. The parent T14.003 file enumerates four sub-cases (a) AVS-design slashing-cascade; (b) Liquid-restaking-token depeg cascade; (c) Operator concentration risk; (d) Slashing-as-economic-attack. Sub-case (b) is the load-bearing observed-cohort dimension at v0.1 with three structurally-distinct constrained-primitive sub-classes; T14.003.001 makes the three-sub-class decomposition explicit at the canonical-Technique level.

The structural OAK lesson is that **the LST / LRT depeg-cascade pattern is not a single cascade primitive but a family of cascades parametrised by which constrained primitive saturates**. The defender / risk-team mitigation surface differs across the three sub-classes:

- **Sub-class (i)** is mitigated by protocol-level redemption-primitive enablement (post-Shapella for stETH; restaking-protocol withdrawal-feature shipping for LRTs).
- **Sub-class (ii)** is mitigated by operator-side commitments to maintain the withdrawal-side primitive enabled in parallel with deposit-side; the Renzo ezETH one-way-valve pattern is the canonical anti-pattern.
- **Sub-class (iii)** is mitigated by per-epoch / per-period throughput disclosure plus oracle-methodology hardening; protocol-level redemption-primitive availability is necessary but not sufficient.

The cross-reference to T9.001 is structurally important. DEX-spot-anchored lending-market oracles propagate the secondary-market discount into liquidation triggers without redemption-rate / queue-depth-aware buffer. The cascade firing through any of the three sub-classes is amplified by this oracle-methodology decision; lending-market integrators that adopt redemption-rate / queue-depth-aware oracle methodology bound the cascade amplification independently of which constrained primitive saturates.

The cohort's chronological progression (2022 i → 2024 ii → 2025 iii) maps onto the staking / restaking ecosystem's structural maturation:

- **2022 (sub-class i)** — pre-Shapella; chain-level redemption-absence was the binding constraint because the protocol-level primitive did not yet exist.
- **2024 (sub-class ii)** — restaking-protocol launch wave; operator-blocked redemption was the binding constraint as protocols launched deposit-side flows ahead of withdrawal-side primitive shipping.
- **2025 (sub-class iii)** — post-Shapella + EigenLayer slashing live; withdrawal-queue-depth saturation was the binding constraint as the protocol-level primitive existed but per-period throughput was the bottleneck.

Future T14.003.001 worked examples should preserve the **constrained-primitive identification** dimension explicitly — which primitive saturated in the cascade — and the **cascade-demand scenario** — what sell-pressure rate produced the saturation. Without these dimensions, the worked example collapses into a generic "depeg" framing that loses the structural distinction between the three sub-classes.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once additional cohort cases land — additional restaking-protocol operator-blocked-redemption cases beyond Renzo (sub-class ii); additional withdrawal-queue-depth-saturation cases beyond the 2025 stETH cascade (sub-class iii); and once the cross-cascade correlation analysis is operationalised across multiple LRT issuers and multiple lending markets simultaneously.
