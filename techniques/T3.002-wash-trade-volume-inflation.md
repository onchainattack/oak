# OAK-T3.002 — Wash-Trade Volume Inflation

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T3 (Holder Capture)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana, CEX (cross-venue)
**First documented:** 2019 (Bitwise SEC filing on CEX wash); 2021 (Victor & Weintraud, DEX academic cohort)
**Aliases:** "self-trading", "circular volume", "fake volume", "incentive-farming wash"

## Description

Trading activity in which a small set of related addresses repeatedly trade the same asset against each other, inflating the public volume metric without any net change in beneficial ownership. From a defender's perspective, the signal lives in the **graph of counterparties** for a given token's trades — wash patterns appear as cycles or near-cycles in that graph, and as elevated gross/net flow ratios in cluster-aggregate accounting.

T3.002 covers the fungible-token case (DEX swap and CEX-trade artefacts). The NFT-marketplace analogue is OAK-T12.001 (NFT Wash-Trade Volume Inflation); the laundering-motivated NFT-trade pattern is OAK-T7.004. T3.002 detection runs on per-pair counterparty graphs at swap-event granularity.

## Observed indicators

- High volume relative to holder count, social footprint, or external venue presence.
- A small number of addresses accounting for a disproportionate share of trade count for a given pair.
- Cyclic trade patterns where assets return to the originating cluster within a short window (A→B→A within minutes; A→B→C→A within hours).
- Repeated counterparty pairs (same buyer-seller pair accounting for >M% of pair-level trade count).
- Volume spikes correlated with marketplace-incentive snapshot windows (rewards-farming sub-pattern).
- For CEX cohorts: trade-size-histogram deformation, alignment of volume spikes across venues, and absence of the spread-pattern signature of organic retail flow.

## Detection signals

- **Cycle-detection in trade-counterparty graph:** for a given token-pair, build the buyer-seller graph weighted by volume share; flag connected components with cycle-density above threshold and gross/net ratio above threshold.
  - Dune-style skeleton: `select cluster_id, sum(amount) as gross, abs(sum(signed_amount)) as net, gross/nullif(net,0) as ratio from swaps_clustered group by cluster_id having ratio > 50`
- **Net-flow check:** compute net beneficial-ownership change for top-volume clusters over a window; flag clusters with high gross / low net.
- **Repeat-counterparty SQL pattern:** `select a, b, count(*) as trades from swaps where pair=$X group by a,b order by trades desc` — top pairs accounting for >M% of trade count are wash candidates.
- **Funder-graph link to T8.001:** trace top-cycle participants back N hops on the funder graph; common-funder match elevates wash classification confidence.
- **Forta-agent triggers:** subscribe to high-volume pair swap events; on cycle-density threshold breach, post to alert sink with cluster-id and gross/net ratio.

## Real-world examples

- **CEX volume study — Bitwise / SEC filing — March 2019.** Bitwise's filing to the U.S. SEC (in support of a Bitcoin ETF rule change) analysed 83 exchanges and concluded that ~95% of reported Bitcoin trading volume on unregulated venues was fake / non-economic wash. Real BTC-spot volume was estimated at ~$273M/day, with only 10 exchanges presenting trade-size-histogram and spread-pattern signatures consistent with organic flow. The cohort is the canonical CEX-side public reference for the T3.002 pattern. Source: `[bitwise2019fakevolumes]`.
- **LooksRare / X2Y2 marketplace-incentive wash — 2022–2023.** Per `[chainalysis2022nft]`-adjacent industry analysis, per-platform wash-trade rates reached ~94.5% on LooksRare and ~84.2% on X2Y2 in 2022–2023 — driven primarily by token-emission programs that paid traders proportional to volume. The cohort straddles T3.002 (volume-inflation goal) and the rewards-farming sub-pattern; mitigation differs because the rewards-farming pattern is fixed at the reward-formula layer rather than the metric-display layer.
- **Blur airdrop-driven trading — Feb 2023 onward.** Per CryptoSlam's contemporaneous analysis, ~$577M of trades through Blur in the airdrop-window were classified as wash; ~50% of Blur's NFT trade volume was generated by ~300 wallets; the top airdrop-claimant captured ~3.2M BLUR via repeated buy-sell of the same NFTs. The case anchors the marketplace-incentive sub-pattern in the NFT-marketplace setting (cross-references OAK-T12.001). See [`examples/2023-02-blur-airdrop-wash-cohort.md`](../examples/2023-02-blur-airdrop-wash-cohort.md) for the worked-example file.
- **DEX academic cohort — Victor & Weintraud — 2021.** Per-pair labelled cohort of wash-pattern detection across multiple DEXes with replicable methodology; the canonical academic anchor for OAK-T3.002 detection signals. Source: `[victor2021washtrade]`.
- **Aggregate market-manipulation framing — Chainalysis 2025.** Industry-aggregate volume-share attributed to suspected wash patterns by market segment; provides scale calibration. Source: `[chainalysis2025rug]`.

## Mitigations

- **Traders / risk teams:** discount published volume by an estimated wash factor; weight holder-growth and unique-counterparty metrics over raw volume.
- **Venues / aggregators:** publish wash-adjusted volume metrics alongside raw figures. See OAK-M26 (Wash-Trade Rate Metrics at Marketplace Layer).
- **Incentive-program designers:** apply cluster-level exclusion to reward-eligible volume to neutralise the marketplace-incentive sub-pattern at the source (LooksRare / X2Y2 / Blur lessons).

## Discussion

Calibration of cycle-window and net-flow thresholds is venue-specific. v0.2 should publish per-venue calibration profiles. False-positive sources: market-maker inventory flow can resemble wash on small windows; arbitrageurs across venues can produce repeated-counterparty signatures that net to zero locally. Discriminators include intra-cluster funder-graph match (true wash clusters share a funder; arbitrage clusters do not) and external-venue corroboration of the price action (true wash patterns rarely produce cross-venue follow-through).

The marketplace-incentive sub-pattern (LooksRare / X2Y2 / Blur 2022–2023) is the highest-volume historical instance of T3.002 by quite a wide margin and is the cleanest case where the *mitigation owner* (the venue) is also the entity whose product (rewards) created the incentive. v0.x may promote this sub-pattern to its own technique once a clean discriminator from generic T3.002 is published; at v0.1 it is treated as a sub-class of T3.002 because the on-chain artefact is the same.
