# OAK-T17.002 — Liquidation-Cascade Engineering

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation)
**Adjacent Techniques:** OAK-T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation — T9.001 covers oracle-side manipulation that drives the price-feed; T17.002 covers the *attacker-side liquidation-bot operation* that harvests at distressed prices), OAK-T5.004 (Sandwich / MEV Extraction — the per-liquidation harvest is operationally MEV-extraction-class but the cascade-ignition framing is T17.002), OAK-T8.001 (Operational Reuse — Cluster-Reuse Signal — load-bearing for predatory-liquidation-bot cohort attribution)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (Aave / Compound / Maker / Liquity / Morpho / Spark canonical); Solana (Solend / Marginfi / Kamino); cross-chain liquidation-engine designs broadly inherit the surface
**First documented:** Class anchored 2020-2022 (early DeFi liquidation-engine designs); systematic cascade-engineering 2022+ (Terra / UST collapse May 2022, stETH-Aave cascade June 2022 as canonical anchors)
**Aliases:** "predatory liquidation", "cascade ignition", "liquidation harvesting", "thin-liquidity liquidation farming", "depeg-cascade harvest"

## Description

Predatory bots that intentionally trigger or accelerate liquidation cascades to harvest collateral at distressed prices. The defining shape is that the attacker's *liquidation-bot operation itself* is the load-bearing component — the bot is positioned at the edge of the liquidation-eligibility threshold, the cascade is ignited by an attacker action (or by an exogenous event the attacker pre-positioned for), and the harvest accrues to the bot operator at prices that are not available to the broader market. The cascade dynamics convert a single-position liquidation into a multi-position cascade because each liquidation pushes the price further into the next position's liquidation-eligibility band.

T17.002 is **structurally distinct from T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation)** in *which side of the liquidation pipeline is load-bearing*. T9.001's load-bearing surface is the oracle / price-feed — the attacker manipulates the price-feed at the oracle layer, and the protocol's liquidation engine fires correctly against the manipulated price. T17.002's load-bearing surface is the *attacker-side liquidation-bot operation* — the price feed may be honest (oracle reading legitimate market state during a thin-liquidity / depeg / volatility window), but the bot is engineered to harvest cascading liquidations at distressed prices that the cascade itself produces. The two compose: oracle manipulation (T9.001) can be the *ignition* mechanism for a cascade that T17.002 then harvests.

T17.002 is **structurally distinct from T5.004 (Sandwich / MEV Extraction)** in *temporal scope*. T5.004 is per-block / per-transaction MEV extraction that captures slippage from a single victim swap. T17.002 is multi-block / cross-position cascade harvest that captures slippage from a multi-position liquidation chain. The per-liquidation harvest is operationally MEV-extraction-class (the bot competes for the liquidation transaction inclusion), but the cascade-ignition framing — engineering a configuration that *creates* the cascade — is the T17.002 distinction.

T17.002 covers four structurally adjacent sub-shapes:

- **Depeg-driven cascade harvest.** A pegged-asset (LSD, stablecoin, restaking-token) depegs from its redemption value; lending markets that consume the secondary-market price (rather than the redemption rate) trigger cascading liquidations against borrowers whose collateral was the now-depegged asset. The cascade harvest accrues to predatory liquidation bots positioned in advance of the depeg propagation. Anchors: Lido stETH June 2022, Renzo ezETH April 2024, Lido stETH / Aave queue-saturation cascade July 2025.
- **Thin-liquidity-window cascade harvest.** During low-volume hours (weekend / holiday / Asian-session), liquidation bots ignite a cascade by triggering a single liquidation that the venue's depth cannot absorb without significant price impact; the price impact pushes the next position into liquidation, and so on. Anchors: multiple weekend / Asian-session liquidation-cascade events documented across Aave / Compound / Solend without forensic public reporting at the named-attacker layer.
- **Collateral-asset-volatility cascade harvest.** A high-volatility collateral asset (small-cap altcoin used as collateral, NFT used as collateral via NFTfi-class lending) experiences a price drop that triggers liquidation; cascade dynamics amplify the drop and the harvest accrues to liquidation bots. Adjacent: NFT-collateral cascade events on BendDAO and similar NFT-lending protocols.
- **Stablecoin-issuer cascade harvest.** Stablecoin / algorithmic-stablecoin issuer's collateral asset experiences a death-spiral; cascading liquidations across the issuer's collateral and the algorithmic-stablecoin's bond / governance-token mechanism produce a multi-asset cascade. Anchor: Terra / Luna / UST May 2022.

## Observed indicators

- **Pre-event positioning:** liquidation-bot cohort positioned at the edge of the liquidation-eligibility threshold with capital pre-deployed for cascade harvest; bot accumulation correlated with thin-liquidity-window forecasting (weekend / holiday / cross-venue depeg event).
- **Cascade ignition signal:** a single-position liquidation that produces price impact exceeding the venue's typical depth-vs-trade-size response; the liquidation transaction is correlated with a cohort-attributable cluster.
- **Cascade propagation:** rapid sequence of liquidations across positions that were not in liquidation-eligibility before the ignition; the harvest cohort is repeatedly on the receiving side of the liquidations.
- **Forensic record:** the multi-block transaction sequence containing the cascade-ignition liquidation, the cascade-propagation liquidations, and the collateral-asset disposal; the bot cohort's net-profit calculation post-cascade.

## Detection signals

- **Liquidation-throttling at protocol layer (OAK-M11-class):** per-block caps on liquidation throughput defeat T17.002 by bounding the harvest payoff per cascade.
- **Redemption-rate-aware oracle (LSD-specific mitigation):** oracles that consume redemption rate rather than secondary-market price defeat T17.002's depeg-cascade sub-shape because the cascade-ignition signal (secondary-market depeg) does not propagate to the liquidation-eligibility check.
- **Cross-position correlation monitor:** detect rapid sequences of liquidations across positions that were not in liquidation-eligibility before the ignition, where the harvest cohort overlaps a single funder-graph cluster.
- **Liquidation-bot cohort attribution (T8.001 signal):** funder-graph clustering applied to the liquidation-bot cohort; clusters that are repeatedly on the cascade-harvest side of multi-position cascades are T17.002-suspect.
- **Thin-liquidity-window monitoring:** continuous monitoring of venue depth and protocol liquidation-eligibility-band density during low-volume windows; alerts on configurations where a single-position liquidation would produce price impact exceeding the venue's typical depth-vs-trade-size response.

- **Hyperliquid JELLY self-liquidation + cross-venue spot-pump cascade March 2025 — Hyperliquid + Solana spot — 2025-03-26** — Peak \~\$13.5M HLP unrealised loss from an attacker-engineered self-liquidation cascade composed with cross-venue spot-pump propagation. The case is structurally adjacent to T17.002 but the load-bearing failure is at the algorithmic-stablecoin-design layer; the T17.002 framing applies to the *liquidation-cascade-harvest cohort* that operated against the resulting cascading liquidations.

## Mitigations

- **Protocols (OAK-M11 — liquidation-throttling):** highest-leverage layer. Per-block caps on liquidation throughput defeat T17.002 by bounding the per-cascade harvest payoff.
- **Protocols (OAK-M09 — redemption-rate-aware oracles for LSDs / restaking-tokens):** for protocols that accept LSD / restaking-token / pegged-asset collateral, oracles that consume redemption rate rather than secondary-market DEX-spot price defeat T17.002's depeg-cascade sub-shape by construction. Lido / Renzo / EigenLayer-class assets are the canonical T17.002-exposed surface where this mitigation is load-bearing.
- **Protocols — queue-depth-aware liquidation buffer:** for protocols whose collateral assets have non-trivial withdrawal-queue dynamics (LSDs with exit queue, restaking-tokens with slashing-event delay), liquidation-eligibility-checks should account for queue depth rather than treating the secondary-market price as a clean liquidation reference.
- **Protocols — collateral-volatility-aware liquidation parameters:** for high-volatility collateral assets, liquidation parameters (close-factor, liquidation-bonus, supply-cap) should be calibrated against the asset's volatility profile and against the cohort-density of liquidation bots positioned at the liquidation-eligibility-band edge.
- **Risk teams — predatory-liquidation-bot cohort surveillance:** continuous cohort-attribution applied to the liquidation-bot ecosystem; clusters repeatedly on the cascade-harvest side of multi-position cascades are T17.002-suspect.

## Discussion

T17.002's structural lesson is that **liquidation-cascade harvest is a cohort phenomenon, not an attacker-named phenomenon**. The liquidation-bot operators are operationally indistinguishable from honest liquidation participants — both are responding to the protocol's incentive design, and both are technically following the protocol's specification. The T17.002 distinction is at the *cascade-engineering* layer: a cohort that consistently harvests multi-position cascades, rather than single-position liquidations, is operationally distinct from honest liquidation participation by virtue of the multi-position positioning that requires advance capital deployment and cohort coordination.

The cohort is structurally distinct from T9.001 (oracle manipulation) by *which side of the liquidation pipeline is load-bearing*: T9.001 manipulates the oracle to fire the liquidation; T17.002 harvests the cascade once the liquidation fires (regardless of whether the firing was T9.001-driven, exogenous-depeg-driven, or thin-liquidity-window-driven). The cohort is structurally distinct from T5.004 (sandwich-MEV) by *temporal scope*: T5.004 is per-transaction; T17.002 is cross-position multi-block.

The mitigation surfaces partition cleanly: T9.001 closed by oracle hardening (TWAP, multi-venue quorum, redemption-rate-aware oracles for LSDs); T5.004 closed by per-block / private-mempool primitives; T17.002 closed by liquidation-throttling (OAK-M11) and queue-depth-aware / redemption-rate-aware liquidation parameters at the protocol layer.

Attribution-strength notes: T17.002 attribution is typically `pseudonymous-cohort` rather than `pseudonymous-attacker` — the harvest cohort is identifiable at the funder-graph-cluster layer, but per-incident named-attacker attribution is rare. Public-record post-mortems concentrate at the protocol-risk-parameter layer (Aave Risk Council reports on stETH / ezETH cascades, Compound Risk Steward reports on cross-asset cascades) rather than at the attacker-side liquidation-bot layer.

False-positive considerations: legitimate liquidation participation is operationally essential to lending-protocol solvency; the detection signal is not "liquidation occurred during a price drop" but specifically "a cohort consistently captures multi-position cascading liquidations under thin-liquidity / depeg / volatility-window conditions, with capital pre-deployment that anticipates the cascade ignition." Cohort-level analysis is required to distinguish T17.002 from baseline-liquidation participation.

v0.x deferred items: per-protocol cascade-density metrics as a forward-looking T17.002 surface estimate; a structured rubric for distinguishing depeg-cascade / thin-liquidity-cascade / volatility-cascade / stablecoin-issuer-cascade sub-shapes; cohort-level analysis of the liquidation-bot ecosystem across major lending protocols.
