# OAK-T15.001 — Social Engineering of Operator Personnel

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T15 (Off-chain Entry-Vector / Pre-Positioning)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** chain-agnostic (the social-engineering vector is off-chain; downstream on-chain manifestation is per-incident)
**First documented:** systematically 2019 onward (DragonEx WFC Proof "trading-bot" lure as the canonical pre-2020 anchor); the LinkedIn fake-job-offer / Telegram fake-recruiter / fake-investor / fake-trading-bot variants stabilised across 2020–2026 OAK-G01 operations
**Aliases:** "TraderTraitor entry vector", "LinkedIn fake-job-offer", "fake-recruiter lure", "DPRK fake-coding-test", "fake-investor pretext", "Penpie audit-report lure", "WFC Proof", "Contagious Interview / Wagemole"

## Description

Targeted social engineering directed at operator-side personnel — developers, signers, executives, security-team staff, customer-support staff with privileged access — at exchanges, custody vendors, signing-platform vendors, bridges, DeFi protocols, and trader-tooling shops. The defining shape is **pretext + payload**: the attacker establishes a credible operator-relevant pretext (recruiter approaching a developer with a senior role; VC/investor reaching out to a founder; trading-firm-or-API partner approaching an exchange engineer; former-contractor reaching out via Telegram with an "audit report" attachment), and the pretext delivers a payload (a "coding test" repository whose dependencies trigger a backdoor; a malware-laden PDF / ZIP / DMG; a fake trading-bot or analytics application; a Calendly-class flow that drops a bin file; an `npm install` whose post-install script runs a backdoor).

T15.001 is the **vector**, not the resulting endpoint state. The endpoint state — implant established, MITM layer planted, signing-host RPC compromised, browser extension exfiltrating cookies — is OAK-T15.003 (Operator-Endpoint Compromise). The two are typically chained: a successful T15.001 produces a T15.003 endpoint state, which then enables a downstream T11.x / T9.x / T10.x event. Splitting them lets defenders independently prioritise anti-phishing controls (T15.001 mitigation: OAK-M21) and signing-host hardening (T15.003 mitigation: OAK-M19, EDR / MDM).

T15.001 is **structurally distinct** from end-user phishing (T4-class). End-user phishing's victim is the wallet-holder; T15.001's victim is an operator-side staff member whose access controls a downstream signing surface that many third-party customers depend on. The dollar-loss-prevented metric is therefore not per-victim but per-operator-customer-base: a single successful T15.001 against a Safe{Wallet} developer (Bybit Feb 2025) or a Ginco engineer (DMM May 2024) produced losses that distributed across the vendor's entire customer base.

The dominant pretext channels at v0.1 are LinkedIn (the foundational TraderTraitor / Lazarus channel from ~2019 onward), Telegram (the Radiant Capital 2024 variant — impersonation of a former contractor delivering an "audit report"), Discord / Slack DMs (fake-investor / fake-VC pretexts; fake-bug-bounty submissions), and email (recruiter pretexts plus fake-investor outreach). The channel rotates per operator's social posture; the social-engineering shape — credible operator-relevant pretext + payload that compromises the workstation — is constant.

## Observed indicators

- **Off-chain (operator-side):** unsolicited recruiter / investor / partner messages targeting personnel with privileged access (multisig signers, deployer-key holders, signing-platform engineers, customer-support staff with UI privilege); attachments or repositories whose execution requires running untrusted code (npm install, Python installation, DMG / ZIP execution, PDF with unusual permissions); credibility-laundering patterns where the pretext references a real prior project / employer / product to lower scrutiny.
- **Pretext sub-shapes:**
  - *Fake-job-offer / fake-recruiter* — most common DPRK pattern; payload is typically a "coding test" or interview repository; canonical anchors at Ronin (LinkedIn), DMM Bitcoin (LinkedIn coding test), Drift Protocol (LinkedIn six-month rapport-building); also DragonEx 2019 WFC Proof trading-bot lure as pre-2020 anchor.
  - *Fake-investor / fake-VC* — payload is typically a "deck" PDF or a Calendly-class flow that drops a bin; canonical anchor at Concentric Finance (fabricated-investor pretext to one of the multisig signers).
  - *Fake-trading-bot / fake-API-partner* — payload is a DMG / installer claiming to provide trading or analytics tooling; canonical anchor at DragonEx (2019 WFC Proof) and the broader 2024-2026 SwiftLoader / RustBucket macOS-targeted family.
  - *Fake-former-contractor / fake-audit-report* — payload is a ZIP claiming to contain a security report or post-mortem; canonical anchor at Radiant Capital (Telegram impersonation, `Penpie_Hacking_Analysis_Report.zip`).
  - *Fake-staking-partner / fake-third-party-API* — payload exploits the trust relationship between an operator and a known partner; canonical anchor at SwissBorg (Kiln staking-partner third-party API as the trust-substrate).
- **Forensic record:** mailbox / DM archive of the pretext, attachment file hashes, post-event endpoint forensics establishing the payload-to-implant chain.

## Detection signals

- **Operator-side mailbox / DM monitoring** for unsolicited contacts directed at privileged staff; flag attachments and repositories that require code execution to evaluate.
- **Anti-phishing simulation** targeted at recruiter / investor / partner pretexts (not just generic email phishing) — specifically rehearse the "LinkedIn cold-message → coding-test repository" pattern, the "Telegram former-contractor → audit-report ZIP" pattern, and the "fake-investor → deck-PDF / Calendly" pattern.
- **Zero-trust execution policy for unsolicited code** — separate, ephemeral, network-isolated environment for evaluating any unsolicited repository, attachment, or installer; never on the day-to-day developer workstation.
- **CTI feed integration** — subscribe to vendor-side advisories on active recruiter-pretext campaigns (Mandiant UNC4736 / Citrine Sleet / TraderTraitor reporting; Unit 42 BeaverTail / Contagious Interview; Microsoft Sapphire Sleet / Storm-1811 reporting); cross-reference against operator-personnel inbox traffic.

## Real-world examples

- **Bybit / Safe{Wallet} — Ethereum — 2025-02-21** — Loss \~\$1.46B in ETH equivalent. The Safe{Wallet} developer-workstation compromise that produced the on-chain T11.001 extraction at Bybit was preceded by a social-engineering vector against the Safe{Wallet} developer. The vector is the canonical recent T15.001 anchor at the largest dollar scale on the public record. See [`examples/2025-02-bybit.md`](../examples/2025-02-bybit.md).
- **Radiant Capital — Arbitrum / BNB Chain — 2024-10-16** — Loss \~\$50M. Telegram impersonation of a former contractor delivered the `Penpie_Hacking_Analysis_Report.zip` containing INLETDRIFT macOS backdoor; subsequent lateral movement compromised additional Radiant developer machines and produced the MITM signing-host layer that enabled the on-chain `transferOwnership()` extraction. Mandiant attributed the social-engineering operation to UNC4736 / Citrine Sleet. See [`examples/2024-10-radiant-capital.md`](../examples/2024-10-radiant-capital.md).
- **Ronin Bridge — Ethereum ↔ Ronin — 2022-03-23** — Loss \~\$625M. LinkedIn-delivered fake-job-offer pretext targeting a Sky Mavis engineer; malware-laden PDF "interview package" compromised the workstation and enabled lateral movement to validator-key access. The foundational TraderTraitor / Lazarus LinkedIn-vector anchor for the post-2020 cohort. See [`examples/2022-03-ronin-bridge.md`](../examples/2022-03-ronin-bridge.md).
- **DMM Bitcoin — Bitcoin — 2024-05-31** — Loss \~\$305M. LinkedIn fake-coding-test pretext compromised an employee at Ginco (DMM Bitcoin's wallet-software vendor); subsequent vendor-internal-communication compromise was used to manipulate a legitimate transaction. See [`examples/2024-05-dmm-bitcoin.md`](../examples/2024-05-dmm-bitcoin.md).
- **Concentric Finance — Arbitrum — 2024-01-22** — Loss \~\$1.7M. Fabricated-investor / fabricated-job-offer pretext targeted a multisig signer; payload compromised the developer's machine and harvested private-key material from the local wallet-software environment. The canonical small-multisig-protocol T15.001 anchor. See [`examples/2024-01-concentric.md`](../examples/2024-01-concentric.md).
- **DragonEx — multi-chain — 2019-03-24** — Loss \~\$7M. Pre-LinkedIn-canonical-pattern social-engineering using a fake cryptocurrency-trading-bot service ("WFC Proof") that DragonEx employees were socially-engineered to install. The case anchors the pre-2020 TraderTraitor pattern and is the historical antecedent for the LinkedIn / Telegram cohort that followed. See [`examples/2019-03-dragonex.md`](../examples/2019-03-dragonex.md).
- **SwissBorg — Solana — 2025-09** — Loss \~38.8M SOL (\~\$41M). Compromise vector traced to SwissBorg's third-party staking partner Kiln; the trust-substrate compromise upstream of SwissBorg's own infrastructure illustrates the partner-pretext sub-shape. See [`examples/2025-09-swissborg.md`](../examples/2025-09-swissborg.md).
- **Liquid Global / QUOINE PTE — multi-chain — 2021-08-19** — Loss \~\$91M. The cohort-class T15.001 anchor for the *DPRK exchange-side warm-wallet compromise via under-individuated off-chain entry vector* sub-shape. The public forensic record at v0.1 places the case inside the broader DPRK / TraderTraitor 2021 cohort whose pretext-and-payload pattern (LinkedIn fake-job-offer / fake-recruiter family) is well-documented at the cohort level (Chainalysis / Elliptic / FBI advisories) but not individuated for Liquid specifically. The case anchors the *T15 cohort-class mapping convention* OAK uses when the operator-cluster attribution is strong but the per-incident sub-Technique is not pinned in the public record. See [`examples/2021-08-liquid-global.md`](../examples/2021-08-liquid-global.md).

## Mitigations

- **OAK-M21 (Anti-Phishing Training for Privileged Staff):** highest-leverage layer — rehearse the recruiter / investor / former-contractor / partner-API pretexts specifically; treat any unsolicited code from a recruiter / investor / partner channel as actively hostile until proven otherwise.
- **Zero-trust evaluation environment:** evaluate any unsolicited repository / installer / attachment in a network-isolated, ephemeral environment never on the day-to-day developer workstation.
- **OAK-M22 (Rotate-on-Disclosure Discipline):** when an operator-side staff member discloses a suspected pretext interaction, treat the staff member's local credential set, signing material, and active sessions as compromised and rotate.
- **Operator-side OSINT:** monitor public references to your engineers / signers in adversary-controlled channels; treat targeted social-media outreach to identified privileged staff as elevated risk.
- **Cohort-level CTI:** maintain subscription to vendor advisories on active recruiter-pretext campaigns; cross-reference against operator-personnel inbox traffic.

## Discussion

T15.001's structural lesson is that operator-side personnel are an attack surface in their own right, not merely an extension of the vendor perimeter. The 2024–2026 OAK-G01 wave (DMM, WazirX, Radiant, Bybit, Drift Protocol) made this legible at scale: four out of the five canonical incidents in that wave landed via T15.001, the channel rotated (LinkedIn → Telegram → fake-API-partner) but the social-engineering shape was constant, and the dollar-aggregate exceeded \$2B. For OAK's purposes, T15.001 is the **highest-leverage mitigation point** in the supply-chain attack class: every successful pretext-recognition event prevents an entire downstream T11 / T9 / T10 chain.

The DPRK-attribution share is high but not exclusive. The pretext-and-payload pattern generalises: any sufficiently motivated adversary can rent the kit (BeaverTail / InvisibleFerret are open-source-adjacent at this point), and OAK contributors writing future T15.001 examples should not assume DPRK attribution by default. Attribution belongs at the OAK-Gnn layer based on the cluster-forensic record, not at the Technique layer.

False-positive considerations: legitimate recruiter / investor / partner outreach is genuinely common in operator-side workflows. The detection signal is not "the pretext exists" but "the pretext requires executing unsolicited code." Operator-side training should focus on recognising the **payload-vector** step, not on rejecting all unsolicited contact.

v0.x deferred items: a clean boundary between T15.001 and adversarial-insider patterns (a hostile insider whose access was originally legitimate is structurally distinct from a pretext-induced grant of access; OAK does not yet have a Technique covering insider-threat surfaces and may need a separate sub-Technique once the public record matures); cohort-level metrics on time-from-first-contact-to-payload-execution and time-from-payload-to-on-chain-extraction (Drift Protocol's six-month rapport-building was unusually long; the median is under 30 days).
