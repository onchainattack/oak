# OAK-T5.004 — Sandwich / MEV Extraction

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T5 (Value Extraction), OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM
**First documented:** 2019 (Daian et al., "Flash Boys 2.0")
**Aliases:** "sandwich", "MEV sandwich", "front-run + back-run"

## Description

A value-extraction pattern in which an actor with mempool or block-ordering visibility surrounds a victim's pending swap with their own ordering-adjacent transactions, capturing the price difference. From a defender's perspective, the pattern is well-characterized in the academic and public-research literature, and detection is concerned primarily with **post-hoc attribution** (who was sandwiched, by whom, for how much) rather than prevention at the protocol layer.

## Observed indicators

- Three-transaction patterns ordered such that two transactions from a related actor bracket a victim swap, producing a net favorable outcome for the bracketing actor.
- Concentration of such patterns at specific block builders / relayers.
- Stable per-block / per-slot extraction profile against a known set of pools.

## Detection signals

- Block-structure analysis: scan ordered transactions for the canonical three-transaction shape and compute realized extraction value per pattern.
- Counterparty: cluster brackets by submitting actor; flag actors with sustained extraction volume.

## Real-world examples

- **`jaredfromsubway.eth` — Ethereum — operating since 2023-02-27** — Industry-tracked sandwich-MEV operator. EigenPhi's 2023 appraisal characterised the bot's first ~3 months as ~254K transactions of which ~98.31% were identified as front/back-runs, with ~106K victim swaps, ~\$40.6M revenue, and ~\$6.3M profit; cumulative metrics later reported include ~82,679 ETH MEV revenue against ~76,850 ETH gas spent. The operator is repeatedly cited as a canonical reference for how sandwich extraction looks at scale on a public chain. Analytics source: `[eigenphijared2023]`.
- **MEV-Boost equivocation attack — Ethereum — 2023-04-02** — Two MIT-affiliated brothers, Anton and James Peraire-Bueno, exploited a flaw in MEV-Boost's relay protocol to extract ~\$25M from sandwich bots in a single ~12-second window. They deliberately broke the trust assumptions between relays and validators by submitting a malformed proposal that revealed the contents of a private MEV bundle, then front-ran the disclosed transactions. The DOJ unsealed indictments in May 2024 (5 counts including wire fraud and money laundering); the case is the first criminal prosecution of an MEV-boost-layer exploit. The attack inverts the canonical sandwich pattern — sandwich operators were themselves the victims. See worked example at `examples/2023-mev-boost-equivocation.md`. <!-- TODO: cite https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny/pr/two-brothers-arrested-attacking-ethereum-blockchain-and-stealing-25-million -->
- **High-value single-block sandwich cohort — Ethereum — 2022-2024** — EigenPhi maintains rolling per-incident dashboards for high-value sandwiches; documented cases include multi-million-dollar sandwiches against retail swaps that lacked slippage-tolerance configuration. The 2022 \$24M Wintermute-related sandwich-class incidents (where the victim was an algorithmic market-maker, not retail) bracket the upper bound of public single-incident extraction. Analytics: `[eigenphijared2023]` plus per-incident EigenPhi / Flashbots dashboards.
- **Solana Jito-bundle MEV extraction cohort — Solana — 2023 onward** — Jito bundle infrastructure created a Solana-side MEV-extraction surface analogous to MEV-Boost but with different primitives (no public mempool; bundle-auction-priority-based ordering). Documented at the cohort level by Jito's own reporting and by independent dashboards (Helius, Triton). Specific high-value Solana sandwich incidents are routinely surfaced by ZachXBT and ScamSniffer on a rolling basis. v0.1 scopes T5.004 to EVM (see Solana scope note in Discussion); a Solana-MEV sub-Technique is a v0.x candidate.
- **Foundational academic characterisation — 2019** — Daian et al., "Flash Boys 2.0" (`[daian2019flashboys]`), is the foundational empirical and theoretical characterisation of priority-gas-auction-based MEV extraction on Ethereum, including sandwich attacks. The paper introduced the framing that subsequent industry analytics (EigenPhi, Flashbots) operationalised.

## Discussion

T5.004 is the most cleanly characterized Technique in v0.1 on EVM — it has well-established literature, public datasets, and stable detection methodology. It serves as the "gold standard" template for how OAK Techniques should be written.

**Solana scope note (v0.1):** Solana sandwich and MEV extraction is a distinct surface (no global mempool; Jito bundle inspection, Helius / Triton MEV-tracking signature shapes, leader-schedule timing, validator-to-builder priority-auction fills are the relevant primitives). v0.1 indicators in this Technique are EVM-mempool-shaped and would not detect Solana sandwich behaviour cleanly; rather than carry an EVM-only signal under a "Solana" Chains label, T5.004 is scoped to EVM at v0.1. A separate Solana-MEV Technique (or a Solana sub-Technique under T5.004) is a v0.x candidate once a calibrated detector with Jito-bundle-aware signals has reference-implementation status.
