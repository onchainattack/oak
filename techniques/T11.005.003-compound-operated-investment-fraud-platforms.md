# OAK-T11.005.003 — Compound-Operated Investment-Fraud Platforms

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T11 (Custody and Signing Infrastructure); sub-Technique of OAK-T11.005 (Operator-side Fake-Platform Fraud)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** chain-agnostic; deposit substrate is BTC / ETH / stablecoins (USDT on Tron dominant); industrial-scale victim-acquisition infrastructure predominantly in Southeast Asia
**First documented:** 2020 (emergence of industrial-scale scam compounds); 2024 OFAC Ly Yong Phat / O-Smach Resort designation; 2025 DOJ Chen Zhi / Prince Group $15B Bitcoin forfeiture
**Aliases:** "Southeast Asia scam compound", "forced-labour investment fraud", "industrial pig-butchering", "compound-operated fraud", "Cambodia/Myanmar scam compound"

## Description

The fake-CEX / fake-investment-platform pattern of T11.005.001 distributed at **industrial scale** through forced-labour scam compounds in Southeast Asia (Cambodia, Burma / Myanmar, Laos). Multiple operator-controlled entities work in concert across layers — fake platform, fake custodian, fake recovery service, compound feeder-narrative infrastructure — to appear as independent operators, while in reality being co-located, co-managed, and co-financed from a single compound or operator group.

The scale is the defining feature: individual compounds house thousands of trafficked workers operating romance-scam and friendship-scam feeder narratives at industrial throughput, targeting victims globally. The compound structure enables:

- **Labour-force scale:** trafficked workers operating feeder narratives across dating apps, social media, and messaging platforms in multiple languages.
- **Multi-layer fraud stacking:** the same compound operates fake-CEX platforms (T11.005.001), fake-custodian / asset-manager frauds (T11.005.002), and fake-recovery services that target previous victims claiming to recover their losses.
- **Laundering integration:** compound operators control or are adjacent to laundering infrastructure (Huione Group, FinCEN section 311 final rule October 2025) that processes victim deposits at industrial throughput.
- **Jurisdictional arbitrage:** compounds operate in jurisdictions with weak or compromised law-enforcement cooperation, exploiting extradition gaps with victim-residence jurisdictions (U.S., EU, OECD).

The structural distinction from sibling sub-Techniques:

- **T11.005.003** is the **industrial-scale distribution** variant: the fake-platform pattern of T11.005.001 replicated across a compound's infrastructure with trafficked labour as the operational workforce.
- **T11.005.001** (Fake-CEX / Pig-Butchering Platform) is the per-platform fraud shape. T11.005.003 is the cohort-level industrial-organisational shape that produces T11.005.001 at scale.
- **T11.005.002** (Fake-custodian / Fake-asset-manager) is the per-product fraud shape.

## Observed indicators

- Multi-platform victim-destination wallet overlap with known compound-associated laundering infrastructure (Huione Group wallets; OFAC-designated entities).
- Victim feeder-narrative commonality: victims from diverse jurisdictions report structurally identical romance-scam / investment-fraud narratives with per-language localisation.
- Multi-layer fraud stacking: victims of the initial platform fraud are subsequently targeted by "recovery service" scams operated by the same compound.
- Operator-mobility: compound operators relocate across jurisdictions (Cambodia → Myanmar → Laos → Philippines) in response to law-enforcement pressure.
- Federal-record attribution: OFAC designations (Ly Yong Phat September 2024); DOJ forfeiture filings (Chen Zhi / Prince Group October 2025); FinCEN section 311 designations (Huione Group October 2025).

## Detection signals

- **On-chain forensics:** cluster-attribution of victim-deposit wallets to known compound-associated entities; cross-platform wallet-address overlap with OFAC-designated entities and FinCEN-identified laundering infrastructure.
- **Off-chain OSINT:** investigative-journalism coverage (BBC, Reuters, UNODC, UN OHCHR reporting on Southeast Asian scam compounds); OFAC / FinCEN / DOJ enforcement actions; UNODC transnational organised crime threat assessments.
- **Victim-narrative clustering:** cross-jurisdiction victim reports with structurally identical feeder narratives and platform UI patterns — the industrial replication of the fraud template is itself a detection signal.
- **Regulator-warning-feed cross-reference:** FCA, SEC, ASIC, SFC warnings referencing Southeast Asian compound-operated platforms.

## Discussion

T11.005.003 is the **industrial-organisational shape** that produces T11.005.001 at scale. The compound model is the defining operational innovation of the 2020–2026 pig-butchering era: forced-labour compounds in Southeast Asia operate fake-platform fraud at industrial throughput — thousands of trafficked workers per compound, romance-scam feeder narratives in dozens of languages, multi-layer fraud stacking, and integrated laundering infrastructure.

The compound structure's jurisdictional arbitrage (operating in jurisdictions with weak or compromised law-enforcement cooperation) makes traditional per-incident law-enforcement response structurally insufficient. The primary recovery surface is DOJ civil forfeiture at cohort scale (Chen Zhi / Prince Group $15B Bitcoin seizure) and FinCEN section 311 actions against the laundering substrate (Huione Group), rather than per-victim restitution.

The sub-Technique's detection surface at v0.1 is almost entirely at the federal-record / OSINT layer rather than the on-chain forensics layer. The on-chain signals (victim-deposit-wallet cluster overlap with OFAC-designated entities and FinCEN-identified laundering infrastructure) are observable but are downstream of the compound's operational structure and require off-chain attribution context to interpret.
