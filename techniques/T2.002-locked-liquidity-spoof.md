# OAK-T2.002 â€” Locked-Liquidity Spoof

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T2 (Liquidity Establishment), OAK-T6 (Defense Evasion)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, BNB Chain, Solana
**First documented:** 2021 (industry reports)
**Aliases:** "fake lock", "soft lock", "LP lock theatre", "partial lock", "lock receipt forgery"

## Description

A claim of "locked liquidity" that, on inspection, does not constrain the deployer's ability to remove liquidity in practice. From a defender's perspective, the issue is a mismatch between the off-chain claim ("LP locked for X months") and the on-chain authority structure (lock duration, locker contract identity, lock coverage of total LP supply, presence of administrative override).

OAK v0.1 distinguishes four operational sub-patterns under T2.002. They share the marketing-vs-on-chain mismatch signature but differ in which specific authority gap is exploited:

- **Partial-coverage lock** â€” lock receipt covers only a fraction of total LP supply (e.g., 30% locked, 70% deployer-controlled); the marketing claim implies full lock.
- **Short-duration lock** â€” lock duration is materially shorter than the implied investor time horizon (e.g., 30-day lock marketed as "long-term commitment").
- **Lookalike locker** â€” LP is held in a locker contract that visually resembles a known legitimate locker (Unicrypt, Team Finance, PinkLock, Mudra Locker) but is in fact a deployer-deployed lookalike with administrative override.
- **Authority-override lock** â€” lock receipt is genuine but the locker contract retains administrative roles (e.g., `transferOwnership` callable, early-release admin function) that allow the deployer to retrieve LP before the claimed unlock date.

## Observed indicators

- Lock receipt referenced in marketing covers only a fraction of total LP supply (compare `balanceOf(locker)` against total LP supply minted at pool-creation).
- Lock duration shorter than the implied investor time horizon (read locker contract storage for `unlockTime` or equivalent).
- Locker contract has administrative roles that allow early release (presence of `onlyOwner` modifier on a `withdraw`, `unlock`, or `transferOwnership` function in locker bytecode).
- LP held in a locker contract whose deployment transaction trace originates from the same funder cluster as the token deployer (T8.001 link).
- Locker contract address is one character / one byte different from a known canonical locker (typo-squat lookalike).
- Lock-receipt URL points to a website hosted on the deployer's domain rather than the canonical locker's domain (Unicrypt, Team Finance, etc., all publish receipts at their own canonical domains).

## Detection signals

- **Static lock-coverage check:** for every memecoin / new token launch, parse the lock receipt referenced by the deployer; verify covered LP fraction, duration, and locker authority structure. Dune-style: `select pool, total_supply, locked_amount, locked_amount/total_supply as coverage_ratio from pool_summary where coverage_ratio < 0.95`.
- **Locker-allowlist match:** locker contract address must match a known list of legitimate lockers (Unicrypt, Team Finance, PinkLock, Mudra Locker, UNCX). Deployer-deployed lookalikes fail this check.
- **Locker-authority enumeration:** decompile the locker contract; enumerate `onlyOwner`-gated functions; flag if any include `withdraw`, `transferOwnership`, `setUnlockTime`, or `emergency*` patterns.
- **Pre-listing gate (venue layer):** exchanges and aggregators (DEX Screener, GeckoTerminal, CoinMarketCap) can require a verified locker-allowlist match as a listing precondition; this neutralises the lookalike-locker sub-pattern at the source.

## Mitigations

- **Traders / risk teams:** verify lock receipts directly against known locker contracts; treat "locked" claims as unverified by default. See OAK-M02 (Static Analysis Pre-Deployment) and OAK-M01 (Source-Bytecode Verification) for the underlying methodology.
- **Locker services (highest-leverage):** publish a canonical authority-structure schema and signed listing of legitimate locker contract addresses (Unicrypt, Team Finance, PinkLock have made progress here but adoption is uneven).
- **Venues / aggregators:** apply locker-allowlist match as a listing-time gate; surface the lock-coverage ratio (`locked / total`) prominently rather than a binary "locked: yes/no". See OAK-M25 (Listing-Time Source-Verification + Audit-Status Gate).

## Discussion

T2.002 is the canonical OAK-T2 + OAK-T6 modifier case: the on-chain authority structure is genuinely permissive while the off-chain marketing claim implies it is constrained. The defender-side gap at v0.1 is that locker-allowlist coverage is uneven across chains â€” Unicrypt and Team Finance are well-known on EVM, but BNB Chain has many minor lockers and Solana's locker landscape (Streamflow, Bonkbot-locked, others) is still consolidating. v0.x will publish a canonical multi-chain locker registry as the underlying calibration anchor.

The four sub-pattern split (partial-coverage / short-duration / lookalike / authority-override) is a v0.x candidate for separate sub-Techniques. At v0.1 the unified framing is justified because the detection methodology (parse receipt â†’ verify locker allowlist â†’ enumerate locker authority) is shared.

Coverage gap: cross-chain locked liquidity, where the lock receipt is on a chain different from the LP itself, is intentionally split out as T2.003 because the detection methodology (cross-chain lock-receipt-to-pool resolution; multi-chain locker registry with per-chain bytecode hashes) is materially different.
