# OAK-T7.001 — Mixer-Routed Hop

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T7 (Laundering)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Bitcoin, cross-chain
**First documented:** 2017 (academic interest), enforcement attention 2022+
**Aliases:** "mixer hop", "obfuscation hop", "anonymity-set hop"

## Description

The routing of post-extraction assets through a privacy-oriented service whose explicit purpose is to break the linkage between deposit and withdrawal addresses. From a defender's perspective, T7.001 is identified less by the deposit event itself (which is publicly observable) and more by the **inferential linkage** between deposit and withdrawal sides via timing, amount, and counterparty heuristics — and by the residual on-chain artifacts on either side of the hop.

## Observed indicators

- Deposit transactions from previously T5-flagged clusters into known privacy-service deposit addresses (Tornado Cash 0.1 / 1 / 10 / 100 ETH pool contracts on Ethereum mainnet are the canonical EVM-side reference list; Tornado-Nova on Gnosis Chain and the Tornado Cash router contracts are documented variants).
- Withdrawal transactions from the same service whose timing, amount, and downstream-counterparty patterns are statistically associable with the upstream deposits.
- Continuation of activity on the withdrawal side that resumes patterns characteristic of the upstream operator (for example, T1 deployment-funding patterns).
- Anomalous use of post-OFAC-designated mixer infrastructure (Sinbad bitcoin deposit addresses pre-Nov-2023 seizure; eXch deposit addresses pre-2025 shutdown) by clusters that previously routed through Tornado Cash.

## Detection signals

- Deposit-side: per-cluster deposit volume into known privacy-service contracts; live-updated against the OFAC SDN list rather than frozen at a single 2022 snapshot.
- Linkage heuristics (probabilistic): timing-window, amount-class, and counterparty-graph heuristics that produce candidate withdrawal-side matches with stated confidence.
- Cluster continuation: T8.001-style operator-cluster continuity across the hop.
- Mitigation cross-reference: see OAK-M07 (cross-chain attribution graph) and OAK-M27 (Travel Rule / KYC at the privacy-chain boundary) for the operational mitigation surface.

## Real-world examples

- **AnubisDAO post-rug laundering — Ethereum -> Tornado Cash — 2021-10-29** — Following the OAK-T5.001 hard rug of ~13,556 ETH (see the T5.001 page), the drainer routed funds through Tornado Cash in four portions over the following 24 hours; an intermediary address subsequently laundered ~1,018 ETH through the same service. This is a clean public example of the T5 -> T7 chain on which most early laundering-route detection methodology was developed. Forensic write-up: `[cointelegraphanubismixer2022]`.
- **Tornado Cash sanctions designation — Ethereum — 2022-08-08** — The U.S. Treasury (OFAC) added Tornado Cash to the SDN list under Executive Order 13694, citing approximately $7B in alleged laundering volume routed through the protocol. The designation was a regulatory artefact rather than an incident; for OAK's purposes it bounds the time period during which "Tornado Cash deposit" became a per-transaction sanctions consideration for downstream venues. Source: `[ofac2022tornado]`.
- **Ronin Bridge -> Tornado Cash — Ethereum — 2022-04 onwards** — Following the March 2022 Ronin Bridge theft (~$625M, OAK-G01 / Lazarus attribution per `[ellipticronin2022]`), the operator routed extracted ETH through Tornado Cash in the months preceding the August 2022 sanctions designation; this is the canonical "pre-OFAC Lazarus -> Tornado" reference flow that the post-2022 substrate substituted away from.
- **Harmony Horizon -> Railgun continuation — Ethereum — 2023-01-13** — Per the FBI public statement of Jan 23 2023 (`[fbiharmony2023]`), the June 2022 Harmony Horizon theft proceeds (>$60M ETH) were moved through Railgun in January 2023 as continuation laundering. This is the canonical post-Tornado-Cash-sanctions privacy-substrate substitution case in the public record.
- **Sinbad mixer designation — Bitcoin — 2023-11-29** — OFAC designated Sinbad.io as a key Lazarus laundering tool that had processed flows from the Horizon Bridge and Axie Infinity / Ronin heists; the FBI / Dutch FIOD / Finnish KRP simultaneously seized the Sinbad infrastructure. Treasury and Elliptic characterised Sinbad as a successor to the May-2022-sanctioned Blender.io. Source: `<!-- TODO: cite URL -->` (Treasury press release JY1933).
- **eXch shutdown post-Bybit — multi-chain — 2025-05-01** — Per `[coindeskthorchainlazarus2025]` and Elliptic / TRM coverage, ~$200M of the $1.46B Bybit-extracted funds (Feb 2025, OAK-G01) were routed through the no-KYC eXch service before eXch announced May 1 2025 shutdown under DOJ / international scrutiny; eXch resumed limited API operations subsequently per TRM. Source: `<!-- TODO: cite URL -->` (Elliptic eXch retrospective; TRM eXch ongoing-operations post).
- **YoMix mixer — Bitcoin — 2023-2024** — Per Chainalysis, YoMix absorbed a substantial share of the Sinbad-equivalent flow after the Sinbad seizure; cited under `[chainalysis2024laundering]` as part of the post-Sinbad mixer-substrate evolution.

## Mitigations

- **Risk teams / venues:** screen incoming deposits against post-mix-withdrawal flags published by major compliance providers; subscribe to a live-updated SDN-anchored mixer watchlist rather than a frozen 2022 snapshot.
- **Protocols / venues:** integrate compliance-provider screening at deposit time. See OAK-M27 (Travel Rule + KYC at the privacy-chain boundary) and OAK-M07 (cross-chain attribution graph) for the canonical mitigation surfaces; OAK-M22 (rotate-on-disclosure) applies for venues that discover historical exposure to a now-designated mixer counterparty.

## Discussion

T7.001 detection is fundamentally probabilistic and jurisdiction-sensitive. v0.1 documents the Technique; vendor-specific linkage methodologies are out of scope for the open framework but are linked from Reference implementations.

**Post-OFAC substrate (2022 -> present).** The Tornado Cash sanctions designation (Aug 2022) materially changed the mixer-routing landscape. Pre-OFAC: a small number of high-volume EVM-native mixers (Tornado Cash dominant; Blender.io for BTC, sanctioned May 2022) drew the bulk of post-extraction routing; cohort detection was near-binary (deposit-address match against a short SDN-anchored list). Post-OFAC: the substrate fragmented across (a) **privacy-preserving substrates** with broader feature surfaces — most prominently Railgun, used at scale in 2023+ DPRK laundering chains (FBI Jan 2023 PSA flagged the >$60M Harmony continuation through Railgun); (b) **successor mixers** that absorbed Tornado-class flow before themselves being designated — Sinbad (sanctioned and seized Nov 29 2023 as a Blender successor and OAK-G01 staple), eXch (post-Bybit-2025 shutdown after ~$200M routed; years of explicit no-KYC operation), YoMix (post-Sinbad flow absorption documented by Chainalysis); (c) **cross-chain mixer-equivalents** — THORChain in particular acted as a high-volume privacy-equivalent for the post-Bybit-2025 laundering chain (~$1.46B routed in ~10 days, classified under T7.003 but exercising mixer-equivalent obfuscation properties).

**Privacy-chain substitute risk.** Beyond mixers, sustained laundering pressure on transparent-chain substrates is pushing flows toward natively-private chains (Monero, Zcash shielded pool, Penumbra, Aztec Connect successors, Namada). Per `[chainalysisprivacychain2024]`, instant-exchanger-mediated XMR conversion is the documented bridge from EVM-extracted assets into Monero. The compliance posture of major venues (per `[binancexmrdelist2024]` Monero delistings) constrains the off-ramp for privacy-chain laundering, but the mid-flow obfuscation surface remains.

**False-positive reasoning.** Privacy-service deposits by individual users acting for legitimate confidentiality reasons are non-zero and not by themselves attributable; the false-positive cost of treating mixer-deposit alone as evidence is high enough that defender methodology consistently requires additional cluster-attribution signal (T5 origin, OAK-G* group attribution) before treating a mixer-routed flow as illicit. Detection consequence: a defender's mixer-routing watchlist must (i) be live-updated against the SDN list, (ii) include privacy-preserving substrates as candidate hops even where unsanctioned, (iii) treat cross-chain native-asset swap protocols (THORChain, Maya) as mixer-equivalents for cohort-tracking. The pre/post-OFAC routing-pattern divergence is now itself a forensic signal: a 2024+ extraction-to-laundering chain that routes only through Tornado Cash is anomalous, whereas a chain that fans out across Railgun / eXch / Sinbad-or-YoMix / THORChain matches the modern OAK-G01 cohort tradecraft. v0.x deferred work: a per-mixer cohort-attribution table linking each post-2022 sanctioned mixer to its dominant operator clusters.
