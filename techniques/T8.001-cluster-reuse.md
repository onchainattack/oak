# OAK-T8.001 — Common-Funder Cluster Reuse

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T8 (Operator Continuity / Attribution Signals)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana
**First documented:** 2021 (TRM SQUID cross-incident attribution); systematic 2024–2025
**Aliases:** "operator cluster", "funder reuse", "deployer fingerprint", "operator-graph reuse"

## Description

The reuse of common funding-source addresses across the launch of multiple adversarial tokens or operations, allowing investigators to attribute distinct incidents to a single operator cluster. From a defender's perspective, T8.001 is what turns one-off alerts into longitudinal operator profiles — the Technique itself is structural, not behavioral, and is identified through cross-incident graph analysis. T8.001 covers the *single-chain* case; OAK-T8.002 covers the cross-chain case where per-chain graphs become jointly attributable only via cross-chain analysis.

## Observed indicators

- Single funding source appears upstream of deployment-related addresses across multiple tokens (depth-2 same-funder-ancestor ratio above threshold across N flagged tokens).
- Shared deployment patterns: identical compiler metadata, constructor parameter shapes, role mappings, salt patterns for CREATE2 deployment.
- Tooling fingerprints: identical bundler-tool signatures (Jito-bundle structure on Solana; private-mempool batching on EVM), identical multisend / disperse contract usage signatures.
- Persistent counterparty pattern: downstream beneficiaries (deposit addresses, off-ramp clusters) continue receiving from successor operations after a brand-or-domain rotation (Garantex → Grinex; Inferno → Angel).
- Off-chain reuse signals (where available): shared landing-page templates, shared social handles, shared hosting-fingerprints across nominally-unrelated tokens.

## Detection signals

- **Cross-token funder graph:** build a graph rooted at deployer-and-early-buyer addresses across all flagged tokens in a window; identify connected components with multiple distinct token deployments (Dune-style: `select component_id, count(distinct token) as tokens from cluster_components group by component_id having tokens > 3`).
- **Deployment-fingerprint clustering:** index contracts by `(creation-bytecode-hash, constructor-arg-shape, deployer-EOA, compiler-metadata)`; flag fingerprints recurring across multiple incident-flagged tokens.
- **Persistent-counterparty propagation:** when an operator brand is sanctioned or shut down, monitor downstream deposit clusters for resumption-of-flow from a new upstream cluster — the resumption signature is the highest-signal indicator of cluster continuity (Garantex → Grinex worked example per `[treasury2025garantexnetwork]` / `[trmlabs2025grinex]`).
- **Forta / on-chain-trace queries:** subscribe to deployment events; on emission, compute deployment fingerprint and funder-ancestor signature, match against known operator-cluster index.
- **Off-chain CTI integration:** cross-reference shared landing-page hashes / social-handle reuse against on-chain attribution clusters.

## Real-world examples

- **TRM Labs SQUID-cluster attribution — BNB Chain — 2021.** TRM's investigation of the SQUID hard-rug operator (see OAK-T1.001 / T5.001 example) traced the same operator cluster across two earlier scams, with three-incident cumulative recovery estimated at ~35,025 BNB (~$19.3M at the time). One of the cleanest public examples of T8.001 because the cross-incident funder-graph and deployment-fingerprint linkage was published with the original forensic write-up rather than reconstructed years later. Source: `[trmsquid2021]`.
- **Drainer-as-a-Service operator-graph handover — multi-chain — 2024.** Per `[slowmist2024report]`, SlowMist's tracking of Inferno Drainer through Q1–Q3 2024 (rising market share to ~43%) followed by the publicly-announced October 2024 handover of operations to Angel Drainer is T8.001 at the *service* layer — same operational infrastructure persists across operators. Defenders attributing post-Oct-2024 drainer activity should treat continuity-of-infrastructure as a stronger signal than continuity-of-actor. The lineage continues through Pink / Vanilla / Chick per `[scamsniffer2024lineage]`.
- **Garantex → Grinex laundering-cluster reuse — 2025.** Per `[treasury2025garantexnetwork]` and `[trmlabs2025grinex]`, the March 2025 Garantex takedown was followed within days by Grinex emerging as a successor exchange under the same operator network, with persistent counterparty patterns linking pre-takedown and post-takedown deposit clusters. The August 14, 2025 OFAC designation of Grinex and the A7A5 ruble-stablecoin network treats the brand discontinuity as operator continuity. The canonical T8.001 case at the laundering-infrastructure layer.
- **Lazarus / TraderTraitor cluster fingerprints — multi-incident — 2017 onward.** Per `[chainalysis2024dprk]` and `[chainalysisdprktradertraitor]`, OAK-G01 wallet clusters are tracked across the Ronin (Mar 2022), Harmony Horizon (Jun 2022), Atomic Wallet (Jun 2023), WazirX (Jul 2024), and Bybit (Feb 2025) incidents through funder-graph and deployment-fingerprint reuse; the cross-incident T8.001 coverage is the load-bearing attribution surface for Lazarus at modern multi-chain scale. The single-chain framing here covers the per-chain instances; OAK-T8.002 covers the cross-chain joint attribution.
- **Pink Drainer cluster reuse — 2024.** Per `[scamsniffer2024pink]`, Pink Drainer's $3M+ extractions (including the OpenAI CTO and Orbiter Finance founder cases) are attributable through funder-graph reuse across nominally-independent victim incidents — a per-affiliate T8.001 instance within the broader G02 service lineage.

## Mitigations

- **Risk teams / investigators:** maintain operator-cluster watchlists and re-flag any new token whose genesis maps into a known cluster. See OAK-M04 (Funder-Graph Clustering), OAK-M07 (Cross-Chain Attribution Graph).
- **Launchpads:** screen new deployments against publicly known operator-cluster lists before listing. See OAK-M25 (Listing-Time Source Verification and Audit-Status Gate).
- **Receiving venues (CEXes, bridges):** integrate cluster-attribution-provider screening at deposit time; treat persistent-counterparty propagation post-rotation as elevated-risk signal.

## Discussion

Operator-cluster definitions decay over time as operators rotate funding paths; v0.2 should publish a freshness/decay convention for cluster watchlists (recommended: 90-day rolling re-validation of cluster boundaries against new deployments). False-positive sources: shared CEX hot wallets, shared bridge-deposit wallets, and shared MEV-bot funding wallets all produce common-funder signatures across unrelated operators — depth-2 ancestor matching alone is insufficient. Discriminators include intra-cluster funding-pattern variance (true operator clusters fund within tight time windows; coincidental shared-CEX-funded clusters do not) and downstream behaviour correlation.

The infrastructure-vs-actor framing matters operationally: in the Inferno → Angel and Garantex → Grinex cases, brand discontinuity under operator continuity is the persistent pattern, and watchlists keyed on brand or specific named-actor degrade rapidly. T8.001-aware controls should target persistent-counterparty propagation and deployment-fingerprint reuse rather than named-actor labels.

Cross-chain extension is OAK-T8.002. The single-chain case here is the more mature detection surface at v0.1; the cross-chain case requires additional graph-merging methodology and is currently coverage-gap territory for single-chain detectors.
