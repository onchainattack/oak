# OAK-T4.008 — Fake-DEX Clone-Frontend Phishing

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T4 (Access Acquisition)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM dominant (Uniswap / PancakeSwap / Lido / Curve / Stargate / Orbiter / Radiant / Zapper / DefiLlama clone-frontends); Solana (Raydium clone-frontends); cross-chain
**First documented:** 2023-03 (MS Drainer kit cohort initial deployment); class-level industrial cohort observable across 2023–2026 with continuing distribution surface
**Aliases:** "fake DEX phishing", "clone-frontend phishing", "typosquat DEX UI", "paid-ad fake-DEX cohort", "Inferno-Drainer fake-frontend"

## Description

Phishing campaigns whose distribution surface is **paid-for inventory on a legitimate ad platform** (Google Search ads, X / Twitter promoted posts, Telegram bot traffic, Apple App Store / Google Play Store listings) and whose lure is a **counterfeit deployment of a popular DEX UI** — Uniswap, PancakeSwap, Curve, Raydium, Lido, Stargate, Orbiter, DefiLlama, Zapper, Radiant. The attacker-controlled UI is hosted on a typosquatted / near-miss / homoglyph domain (e.g., `unlswap.org` vs `uniswap.org`, `pancake-swap.app` vs `pancakeswap.finance`) controlled by the attacker from the moment of deployment, and connects to a wallet-drainer backend (Inferno → Angel / AngelFerno; MS Drainer; PhishLab kits).

T4.008 is **structurally distinct** from sibling T4 Techniques at the *substrate* level:

- **T4.002 (Compromised Front-End Permit Solicitation)** — compromises a *real platform's* user-facing surface (DNS, registrar, supply-chain JS, BGP). T4.008's fake-DEX **never had a real platform to compromise**; the entire UI is a counterfeit deployment hosted on a typosquatted / near-miss domain.
- **T4.001 / T4.004 / T4.005** — define the on-chain extraction primitive (Permit2 misuse, allowance-drainer, setApprovalForAll-drainer). T4.008 is a *distribution-surface* sub-Technique; the on-chain extraction primitive chains into T4.001 / T4.004 / T4.005 via the same drainer-kit substrate (Inferno / Angel / AngelFerno; MS Drainer; PhishLab) that the legitimate-frontend-compromise class uses.
- **T4.007 (Native-app Social Phishing on Engagement-Weighted Platforms)** — distribution surface is a target platform's *own* engagement mechanic. T4.008's distribution surface is paid-for inventory on a *separate* legitimate ad platform (search engine, social media, app store, messaging platform).

The novel structural feature is the **distribution surface** (paid ad placement on a legitimate ad platform) — not the on-chain extraction primitive. The detection-and-mitigation surface lives at the *ad-platform-side enforcement layer* (Google Ads tracking-template-misuse review; X promoted-post review; App Store / Play Store binary review; Telegram bot / channel takedown) rather than at the legitimate-platform infrastructure-hardening layer that resolves T4.002.

Four distinguishable distribution sub-surfaces:

- **T4.008.001 — Google Search paid ads.** Highest-volume sub-class. Attacker buys keyword inventory matching the legitimate platform's name; the paid result ranks above the legitimate platform's organic result. Tracking-template misuse displays the legitimate domain to Google Ads' review surface while the click resolves to the phishing URL. Calibration anchor: MS Drainer December 2023 ($59M / 63,210 victims; 10,000+ phishing websites; targeting Zapper / Lido / Stargate / DefiLlama / Orbiter / Radiant).
- **T4.008.002 — X (Twitter) paid promoted posts.** Second-highest-volume sub-class. ScamSniffer December 2023 reports six of nine paid phishing ads on their feed in late 2023 were MS Drainer instances. Calibration anchor: 2025-07-21 Polymarket-using DeFi user $1.23M loss to fake Uniswap clone reached through paid X / Google ad.
- **T4.008.003 — Telegram bot-driven traffic.** Attacker operates a Telegram channel or bot ("trade $TOKEN now," "snipe new tokens," "verify your wallet") that funnels users to the fake-DEX clone-frontend at signing time. Lure is community-amplified rather than search-amplified. Calibration anchor: 2024–2025 Solidus Labs PumpCell (~$800K/month October 2025) and Kaspersky / Group-IB observations of 2,000% surge in Telegram phishing-bot volume late 2024.
- **T4.008.004 — App Store fake-DEX wrapper apps.** Counterfeit Android / iOS applications listed under previously-benign or compromised developer accounts that wrap a phishing UI inside a WebView. The fake apps prompt users to enter their 12-word mnemonic phrase under the guise of "importing your wallet to PancakeSwap / SushiSwap / Raydium / Hyperliquid." PancakeSwap notably has no legitimate mobile application (web-only at v0.1 cutoff), so any mobile app under that brand is counterfeit. Calibration anchor: Cyble Research and Intelligence Labs 2024 cohort (22+ apps, 50+ phishing-domain infrastructure, Median-framework WebView wrapping); 2025 Google Play removal sweep (22+ apps).

## Observed indicators

- **Domain-side fingerprints:** typosquat / near-miss / homoglyph domains (`unlswap.org` vs `uniswap.org`; `pancake-swap.app` vs `pancakeswap.finance`); recent registration dates relative to claim of legitimate-platform-affiliation; absence of ENS / handle-equivalent integration that legitimate platforms typically have.
- **Ad-platform-side fingerprints:** paid Google Search ad ranking above the organic result; tracking-template alias displaying the legitimate domain to ad-review while resolving the click to a phishing URL; X / Twitter promoted post with a brand-impersonation account.
- **Mobile-app-side fingerprints:** WebView-wrapped phishing UIs (Median framework usage in the Cyble cohort); mnemonic-phrase input prompts during "wallet import" flows; embedded malicious URLs in privacy-policy text; app published under a developer account with sparse history.
- **On-chain backend fingerprints:** drainer-kit substrate (Inferno / Angel / AngelFerno; MS Drainer; PhishLab) at the on-chain extraction layer; spender-contract addresses observable in the user's signed transaction.

## Detection signals

- **Ad-platform-side enforcement (highest-leverage):** Google Ads tracking-template-misuse review; X promoted-post brand-verification at the post-creation stage; App Store / Play Store binary review with WebView-wrapper detection; Telegram bot / channel takedown coordination.
- **Domain-reputation services:** ScamSniffer / Web3 Antivirus / SEAL ISAC drainer-website blocklists; community-curated phishing-domain feeds (PhishFort, Chainabuse).
- **Wallet-side pre-trade simulation:** MetaMask / Rabby / Phantom / OKX Wallet pre-trade simulation surfaces drainer-spender-contract patterns and known phishing-domain warnings inline with the swap-confirmation prompt.
- **Independent-vendor cohort-tracking:** ScamSniffer's drainer-campaign tracking and December 2023 MS Drainer publication; SlowMist's 2024 Mid-year and Annual Blockchain Security Report; BleepingComputer / Infosecurity Magazine reporting; Group-IB Inferno Drainer technical analysis; per-victim public reporting via ZachXBT, Lookonchain, and SEAL Threat Intel.

## Real-world examples

- **Fake DEX / clone-frontend distribution cohort — multi-chain — 2023 onward (canonical T4.008 multi-year cohort)** — ~$59M stolen by the **MS Drainer** kit alone across **63,210 victims** between March 2023 and December 2023, primarily distributed through Google Search ads and X (Twitter) ads spoofing fake-DEX clone-frontends of Zapper, Lido, Stargate, DefiLlama, Orbiter Finance, and Radiant (per ScamSniffer's December 2023 cohort write-up). ~$1.23M drained from a single Polymarket-using DeFi user on 2025-07-21 via a fake Uniswap clone reached through a Google Ads paid result; the bonded wallet-drainer used was the AngelFerno descendant of Inferno. ~$494M aggregate 2024 wallet-drainer ecosystem total per ScamSniffer (67% YoY increase, 332,000 unique victim addresses), of which fake-DEX-distribution-class campaigns are the dominant per-incident shape; the named-incident calibration is the calibration anchor. 22+ counterfeit Android applications impersonating PancakeSwap, SushiSwap, Raydium, Hyperliquid, and similar DEXes removed from the Google Play Store across 2024–2025 per Cyble Research and Intelligence Labs (CRIL); the campaign infrastructure spanned 50+ phishing domains and was embedded in WebView-wrapped phishing sites. Attribution: `pseudonymous` at cohort scale; partially clustered in named cases. MS Drainer is attributed to a Telegram-handle-vendor "Pakulichev" / "PhishLab" who sold the kit for $1,500 plus a 20% revenue share. Inferno Drainer (and its post-November-2023 affiliate-base continuity into Angel Drainer) is the dominant kit substrate behind 2024 cases. Per-victim attribution typically lands on the *affiliate*, not the kit-vendor. See [`examples/2023-2026-fake-dex-clone-frontend-cohort.md`](../examples/2023-2026-fake-dex-clone-frontend-cohort.md).

## Mitigations

- **Ad-platform-side enforcement (highest-leverage):** Google Ads should harden tracking-template-misuse review; X / Twitter promoted-post review should require brand-verification at the post-creation stage for promoted posts targeting trademark keywords; App Store / Play Store should require binary review with WebView-wrapper detection. The cohort persists not because detection is unsolved but because the *ad-platform-side enforcement* lags the per-campaign click-through window.
- **Domain-reputation feeds:** drainer-website blocklists (ScamSniffer, Web3 Antivirus, SEAL ISAC) should be integrated into wallet pre-trade simulation surfaces and into ad-platform review pipelines.
- **Wallet-side pre-trade simulation:** MetaMask / Rabby / Phantom / OKX Wallet pre-trade simulation should surface drainer-spender-contract patterns and known phishing-domain warnings inline with the swap-confirmation prompt, with high-friction confirmation flow when the simulation surface fails.
- **Legitimate-DEX-side defensive registration:** legitimate DEX operators should defensively register typosquat / near-miss / homoglyph domains; publish authoritative-frontend lists; integrate ENS / handle-equivalent identity systems that wallet UIs can verify.
- **App-store-side WebView-wrapper detection:** App Store / Play Store binary review should detect WebView-wrapped phishing UIs as a discrete risk class; require enhanced review for apps that embed external URLs in privacy-policy text or that brand-impersonate established DEX operators.
- **User-side hygiene:** prefer typing the legitimate-DEX URL directly into the address bar rather than clicking ad results; verify the domain matches the canonical legitimate domain before connecting a wallet; treat any unsolicited "verify your wallet" / "claim airdrop" prompt as suspicious by default.

OAK Mitigation cross-references at v0.1 are a **gap** — no existing OAK-M* entry directly maps to ad-platform-side enforcement. v0.x candidate Mitigation entries: "OAK-M-x ad-platform tracking-template-misuse hardening"; "OAK-M-x app-store WebView-wrapper detection"; "OAK-M-x wallet-side pre-trade phishing-domain simulation".

## Discussion

T4.008 is the **highest-volume paid-traffic-distributed phishing class in the wallet-drainer category 2023–2026**. The distribution surface is **legitimately purchased ad inventory** rather than compromised infrastructure. The cohort persists not because detection is unsolved but because the *ad-platform-side enforcement* lags the per-campaign click-through window.

The structural distinction from T4.002 (Compromised Front-End Permit Solicitation) is the *substrate* of the user-facing UI. T4.002 compromises a real platform's user-facing surface (DNS, registrar, supply-chain JS, BGP); the fake-DEX class **never had a real platform to compromise**. The structural OAK lesson is that the user-side mental model "I'm interacting with the legitimate Uniswap because the UI looks identical to Uniswap" is structurally false because the substrate of the UI is attacker-controlled from the moment of deployment.

The four distribution sub-surfaces operate at structurally different ad-platform enforcement layers:

- **T4.008.001 (Google Search paid ads)** — search-engine-ad-review enforcement; tracking-template-misuse is the canonical evasion vector.
- **T4.008.002 (X promoted posts)** — social-media-ad-review enforcement; brand-impersonation is the canonical evasion vector.
- **T4.008.003 (Telegram bot-driven traffic)** — messaging-platform takedown enforcement; community-amplification is the lure rather than search-engine ranking.
- **T4.008.004 (App Store fake-DEX apps)** — app-store binary-review enforcement; WebView-wrapper is the canonical evasion vector.

The on-chain extraction primitive is shared across all four sub-surfaces: drainer-kit substrate (Inferno / Angel / AngelFerno; MS Drainer; PhishLab) chains into T4.001 / T4.004 / T4.005 at the on-chain layer. The kit-vendor / affiliate operator-cluster split is structural — the kit-vendor (Pakulichev / PhishLab for MS Drainer; OAK-G02 cohort for Inferno / Angel) sells the kit for a fee + revenue share; the affiliate operator runs the per-campaign distribution and captures the bulk of the realised loss.

The 2024 wallet-drainer ecosystem aggregate (~$494M per ScamSniffer; 67% YoY increase; 332,000 unique victim addresses) anchors T4.008 as the dominant per-incident shape across the cohort. The class is included at v0.1 with `emerging` maturity rather than `stable` because (a) the multi-year cohort anchor is composite rather than per-incident, (b) the ad-platform-side enforcement maturation is ongoing, and (c) the kit-vendor-vs-affiliate operator-cluster attribution depth is partial across the cohort.

The structural cross-reference to T11.007.003 (Brand-trust-leveraged active phishing for seed-phrase exfiltration) is informative: T11.007.003 targets hardware-wallet vendor brand-trust to solicit *seed phrases* via email / physical mail / companion-app prompts; T4.008 targets DEX brand-trust to solicit *on-chain authority grants* via paid-ad-distributed clone frontends. Both classes exploit brand-impersonation; the substrate-of-extraction differs (seed phrase vs. on-chain authority grant). Future T4.008 worked examples should preserve the distinction and avoid conflating the two classes.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once per-incident worked-example anchors land beyond the multi-year cohort file (per-incident anchors for MS Drainer December 2023, the 2025-07-21 Uniswap-clone Polymarket-user case, the Cyble Play Store cohort, and Telegram-bot-traffic-specific anchors). The current cohort file is sufficient to anchor T4.008 at `emerging` per the v0.1 promotion criteria but is structurally a single composite anchor rather than the multi-anchor independent-confirmation pattern that warrants `stable`.
