# OAK-T17.001 — Cross-Venue Arbitrage-Driven Price-Discovery Distortion

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation)
**Adjacent Techniques:** OAK-T5.004 (Sandwich / MEV Extraction — single-block / single-venue case of the same family; T17.001 is the cross-venue / cross-block case), OAK-T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation — T9.001 manipulates the oracle's input venue; T17.001 manipulates the cross-venue spread that produces market-wide price-discovery distortion as the consequence), OAK-T8.001 (Operational Reuse — Cluster-Reuse Signal — load-bearing for cross-venue arbitrage-bot cohort attribution)
**Maturity:** draft
**Chains:** chain-agnostic (cross-venue arbitrage is a continuous phenomenon across CEX / DEX venues on every chain with non-trivial trading activity; the load-bearing surface is the inter-venue spread, not the chain-level state)
**First documented:** Class anchored conceptually 2017-2020 (CEX / DEX arbitrage as a continuous phenomenon throughout the period); no canonical extraction-scale OAK anchor at v0.4
**Aliases:** "cross-venue arbitrage manipulation", "spread-driven price-discovery distortion", "lagging-venue victim cohort", "cross-CEX/DEX arbitrage exploit"

## Description

Multi-venue spread exploitation in which the attacker's actions on one venue produce a cross-venue price-discovery distortion that creates a victim cohort on a lagging venue. The defining shape is **legitimate cross-venue arbitrage *as cover* for or *as consequence of* a primary action on the lead venue** — the arbitrage-bot leg of the kill-chain is operationally indistinguishable from honest arbitrage activity, but the lead venue's price move was either (a) caused by the attacker's primary trading action on that venue, or (b) caused by an upstream event the attacker had pre-positioned for, and the consequence is that the lagging venue's price discovery is shaped into a configuration that produces an extraction outcome.

T17.001 is **structurally distinct from T5.004 (Sandwich / MEV Extraction)** in *atomicity scope*. T5.004 is the per-block / per-transaction sub-class — the attacker frontruns and backruns a victim swap inside a single venue's block, capturing the slippage difference. T17.001 is the cross-venue / cross-block case — the price-discovery distortion produced on venue A propagates to venue B over a multi-block window (CEX-DEX latency, oracle-update latency, settlement-layer latency on different chains), and the victim cohort settles on venue B against the distorted reference price. The mitigation surface differs: T5.004 is closed at the per-block layer (private mempools, MEV-Boost relays, venue-side commit-reveal); T17.001 is closed at the venue-architecture / oracle-architecture layer (multi-venue oracle quorum, deeper liquidity on the lagging venue, randomised cross-venue settlement windows).

T17.001 is **structurally distinct from T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation)** in *load-bearing surface*. T9.001's load-bearing surface is the *oracle's input venue* — the attacker manipulates a thin-liquidity AMM pool that the oracle reads from, and the oracle reports the manipulated price to the protocol. T17.001's load-bearing surface is the *cross-venue spread* itself — there is no specific protocol whose oracle is the load-bearing surface; the manipulation produces price-discovery distortion that is consumed by every downstream contract / venue / market participant that references the lagging venue's price during the propagation window. T9.001 produces a single-protocol-victim outcome; T17.001 produces a multi-victim cohort outcome.

T17.001 is **structurally distinct from generic arbitrage**. Generic cross-venue arbitrage closes spreads and improves price discovery — the social value is positive. T17.001 covers the sub-class where the spread itself was *manufactured* by an attacker action on the lead venue, or where the attacker pre-positioned for an exogenous spread-opening event that other market participants did not have advance knowledge of. The boundary is whether the attacker's action *opened* the spread (T17.001) or *closed* a spread that already existed (generic arbitrage). The detection signal is at the cohort-attribution layer: the same cluster repeatedly being on the profitable side of cross-venue spreads correlated with one party's lead-venue actions is T17.001-suspect.

## Why this Technique is `draft` at v0.4

T17.001 is introduced as `draft` rather than `emerging` because OAK does not have a clean dollar-loss anchor for the class at v0.4. Cross-venue arbitrage is a continuous phenomenon in crypto markets and is documented at the academic / market-microstructure layer (multiple papers on CEX / DEX latency arbitrage and on cross-venue spread persistence) and at the regulatory layer (CFTC commentary on cross-venue manipulation). The public record concentrates at the market-microstructure / continuous-cohort layer rather than at the discrete-extraction-event layer that the OAK worked-example template is shaped around.

Specifically, the v0.4 documentation gap is:

- **No canonical attacker-named cross-venue manipulation case with documented victim-cohort dollar-loss.** Cross-venue spread manipulation is documented at the academic and CTI levels, but the canonical case where the attacker is named, the victim cohort is identified, and the extraction is quantified at extraction-scale has not yet landed.
- **Boundary-against-generic-arbitrage is editorially difficult.** Cross-venue arbitrage that closes a spread is socially-positive; cross-venue arbitrage that *manufactures* a spread on the lead venue is socially-negative. Distinguishing the two operationally requires multi-venue coordinated forensics that few public investigators have the operational capability to produce.
- **Adjacent OAK incidents are mostly T9.001-class.** Mango Markets October 2022 used cross-venue thin-liquidity moves on FTX / AscendEX / Serum to drive Mango's oracle, but the load-bearing surface for the loss was Mango's oracle (T9.001), not the cross-venue spread itself.

Future contributors should anchor T17.001 once a public-record case lands at the dollar-loss scale comparable to T17.002 / T16.001-003 anchors. Promotion to `emerging` requires at least one canonical worked example with a named attacker (or pseudonymous with strong forensic attribution), an identified victim cohort, and a quantified extraction.

## Observed indicators

- **Cross-venue (lead venue):** large directional trade or order-flow on venue A produces a price move that exceeds venue A's typical depth-vs-trade-size response; the cohort that placed the lead-venue trade is correlated with the cohort that captured the cross-venue arbitrage profit.
- **Cross-venue (lagging venue):** spread between venue A and venue B widens during the propagation window; victim cohort on venue B settles against the lagging price (oracle update, AMM swap, perpetual settlement) before the spread closes.
- **Cohort-attribution (T8.001 signal):** the arbitrage-bot cohort that captures the spread is repeatedly on the profitable side of similar cross-venue events correlated with one identifiable lead-venue actor; funder-graph clustering connects the lead-venue actor and the arbitrage-bot cohort.
- **Pre-event signal:** absence of multi-venue oracle quorum on the protocol consuming the lagging-venue price; thin liquidity on the lagging venue relative to the lead venue; predictable cross-venue-settlement window (e.g., scheduled oracle update time, scheduled DAO treasury swap window).

## Detection signals

- **Multi-venue oracle quorum check:** the canonical mitigation against T17.001 is a price reference that requires quorum across N independent venues; protocols that consume a single-venue or two-venue thin-quorum price feed are T17.001-exposed by construction.
- **Cross-venue spread monitor:** continuous monitoring of the inter-venue spread for a given asset pair; alerts on spread excursions that exceed the asset's typical inter-venue spread distribution and that are correlated with a single lead-venue actor's order-flow.
- **Cohort-correlation analysis at the bot layer:** funder-graph clustering applied to the arbitrage-bot cohort that captures cross-venue spreads; a cluster that is repeatedly correlated with a single lead-venue actor's trades is T17.001-suspect.
- **Lagging-venue depth analysis:** for each protocol whose price reference is potentially T17.001-exposed, characterise the cross-venue depth ratio at the price-reference layer; thin-lagging-venue is a structural T17.001 risk factor.

## Reference implementations

- `mg-detectors-rs` — coverage **gap** at v0.4. The on-chain-only signal is the lagging-venue settlement against the distorted reference; the upstream cross-venue manipulation signal lives at the venue-data layer.
- Vendor coverage: market-microstructure analytics firms (Kaiko, CCData, Amberdata) report cross-venue spread metrics that are load-bearing for T17.001 detection; specialist MEV / market-quality researchers (Flashbots, Eigenphi, EigenLabs) increasingly surface cross-venue MEV signals; CFTC / SEC market-surveillance desks have the cross-venue forensic capability but discrete public reports at extraction-scale are sparse.

## Mitigations

- **Protocols (OAK-M09 — multi-venue oracle quorum):** highest-leverage layer. A price reference that requires quorum across N independent venues defeats T17.001 by construction — manipulation on a single lead venue does not propagate to the protocol's settlement math because the quorum requires multiple-venue confirmation.
- **Protocols — settlement-window randomisation:** for protocols with predictable settlement windows (oracle update, DAO treasury swap, vesting unlock, perpetual settlement), randomising the window reduces the attacker's ability to pre-position on the lagging venue.
- **Venues — depth incentives on lagging venues:** market-maker incentive programmes that improve depth on the lagging venue narrow the cross-venue spread and reduce the T17.001 attack surface.
- **Risk teams — cross-venue spread monitoring + cohort-attribution:** continuous monitoring of cross-venue spreads correlated with cohort-attribution analysis at the bot layer.
- **Regulators — cross-venue forensic capability:** the CFTC / SEC / FCA market-surveillance desks have the cross-venue forensic capability that is the most credible deterrent against named-attacker T17.001 cases; the operational gap is that crypto venue data is fragmented across multiple jurisdictions and venue cooperation is uneven.

## Discussion

T17.001's structural lesson is that **multi-venue oracle quorum is the load-bearing mitigation** against cross-venue manipulation, regardless of which specific cross-venue mechanism the attacker uses. Closing T9.001 (oracle-feed manipulation) by adding a TWAP window does not close T17.001 — the attacker can still distort the cross-venue spread on the underlying venues; closing T17.001 requires that the consuming protocol's price reference cannot be moved by manipulation on any single venue.

The cohort is structurally distinct from T5.004 (sandwich-MEV) by *atomicity scope*: T5.004 is per-block / per-transaction; T17.001 is cross-venue / cross-block. The cohort is structurally distinct from T9.001 by *load-bearing surface*: T9.001's load-bearing surface is the oracle; T17.001's load-bearing surface is the cross-venue spread. The cohort is structurally distinct from generic arbitrage by *spread origin*: generic arbitrage closes pre-existing spreads; T17.001 manufactures the spread on the lead venue.

T17.001's `draft` status reflects the v0.4 documentation gap honestly. The class is real and operationally documented at the academic / market-microstructure / regulatory layer, but the public record concentrates at the continuous-cohort axis rather than at the discrete-extraction-event axis. Promotion to `emerging` requires a canonical worked example with a named attacker, an identified victim cohort, and a quantified extraction.

False-positive considerations: legitimate cross-venue arbitrage is a continuous and socially-positive phenomenon; the detection signal is not "cross-venue arbitrage occurred" but specifically "the same arbitrage cohort is repeatedly on the profitable side of cross-venue spreads correlated with one lead-venue actor's order-flow." Cohort-level attribution is required to distinguish the load-bearing T17.001 surface from the non-load-bearing legitimate-arbitrage cohort.

v0.x deferred items: a canonical worked example at extraction-scale (the v0.4 documentation gap that promotes T17.001 from `draft` to `emerging`); a structured rubric for distinguishing T17.001 from T9.001 (load-bearing-surface test) and from T5.004 (atomicity-scope test); cohort-level metrics on cross-venue spread distribution as a forward-looking T17.001 surface estimate.
