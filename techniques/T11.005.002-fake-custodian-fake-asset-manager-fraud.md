# OAK-T11.005.002 — Fake-Custodian / Fake-Asset-Manager Fraud

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T11 (Custody and Signing Infrastructure); sub-Technique of OAK-T11.005 (Operator-side Fake-Platform Fraud)
**Maturity:** observed
**Chains:** chain-agnostic (deposit substrate is BTC / ETH / stablecoins on-chain; the fraud "platform" is off-chain operator-controlled UI/database)
**First documented:** 2011 (Bitcoin Savings & Trust as the earliest structurally characterised case); 2018–2019 (PlusToken as the largest-value multi-asset wallet Ponzi); 2020-onward (HyperVerse / CryptoFX / Forsage / CoinDeal as the modern enforcement-record cohort)
**Aliases:** "fake custodian", "fake asset manager", "MLM crypto Ponzi", "AI-trading Ponzi", "fake yield fund", "fake wealth-management platform", "multi-asset wallet Ponzi"

## Description

The operator presents a fake custodial wallet, "wealth management", "AI-trading" yield product, or Bitcoin-denominated investment vehicle backed by a custodial wallet they control. The on-chain receivables (investor deposits in BTC / ETH / stablecoins) are real — victims send real cryptocurrency to operator-controlled wallets. The trading / yield generation / arbitrage operation claimed as the return source is entirely fictitious. Payouts to early entrants (the "returns" that build credibility and attract new deposits) are funded entirely from later entrants' deposits — the classic Ponzi structure applied to cryptocurrency-denominated receivables.

T11.005.002 is structurally distinct from sibling sub-Techniques and adjacent custody-class Techniques:

- **T11.005.001 (Fake-CEX / Pig-Butchering Platform)** — The fraud vehicle is a fake exchange with no real backend. The pitch is "deposit to trade." T11.005.002's pitch is "deposit and we generate yield for you" — the operator is a fake asset manager, not a fake exchange.
- **T11.005.003 (Compound-Operated Investment-Fraud Platforms)** — Industrial-scale fake-platform fraud operated through forced-labour scam compounds. T11.005.002's operator may be a single individual or small group without compound infrastructure.
- **T11.001 (Third-Party Signing-Vendor Compromise)** — A real vendor's infrastructure is breached. T11.005.002 inverts this: the "custodian" is the fraud; there is no real vendor.
- **T5.005 (Treasury-Management Exit)** — A real DeFi protocol operator exits with user funds. T11.005.002's operator never operated a real DeFi protocol — the entire platform and yield narrative was fiction from inception.

The technique has three historical eras:

- **2011–2017 (Bitcoin forum era):** Forum-distributed Ponzi solicitation on Bitcoin Talk and early crypto forums. The canonical anchor is Bitcoin Savings & Trust (2011–2012, ~700K BTC), the first SEC case establishing Bitcoin-denominated investments as securities.
- **2018–2019 (Multi-asset wallet Ponzi era):** Telegram-recruited multi-asset Ponzi schemes accepting BTC / ETH / multiple altcoins. The canonical anchor is PlusToken (2018–2019, ~$2–5.7B), the largest-value Ponzi in crypto history.
- **2020–2026 (Modern enforcement-record era):** MLM-structured "AI-trading" / "wealth management" yield products distributed globally, often with affinity-fraud targeting. Anchored by HyperVerse/HyperFund (DOJ + SEC 2024, ~$1.7–1.9B), CryptoFX (SEC 2024, ~$300M Latino-targeted), Forsage (SEC 2022), and CoinDeal (SEC + DOJ 2023, ~$45M).

## Observed indicators

- **Operator-side:** no verifiable trading history; no audit; no proof-of-reserves; guaranteed high-yield claims (e.g., 7% weekly / ~3,641% annualised); MLM-style referral commissions; the operator controls the custodial wallet.
- **On-chain:** deposit-aggregation pattern into a small number of operator-controlled wallets; Ponzi payout structure observable on-chain (early addresses receive deposits from later addresses rather than from trading-counterparty inflows); no observable on-chain trading flow consistent with the claimed yield-generation method.
- **User-side:** early payouts build credibility and encourage escalating deposits; eventual default on promised repayments (Ponzi collapse); communication-channel collapse post-default.
- **Victim-acquisition:** forum-based (Bitcoin Talk era); Telegram / Discord / WeChat / KakaoTalk (multi-asset wallet era); MLM affinity-fraud targeting with per-language localisation (modern era).

## Detection signals

- **Regulator-warning subscription:** SEC, CFTC, DOJ, FCA, KNF, SFC, BaFin enforcement actions and investor alerts.
- **On-chain forensics:** Ponzi payout structure detection — the ratio of new-deposit inflow to existing-investor payout outflow; addresses receiving "yield" payments that are traceable to new depositor inflows rather than trading-counterparty profits.
- **Off-chain OSINT:** corporate-registry verification in the claimed jurisdiction; absence of a verifiable trading desk or asset-management registration; investigative-journalism cross-reference (BehindMLM, ICAC, BBC).
- **User-side defensive signal:** small test-withdrawal before any meaningful deposit; the invariant "if the claimed yield exceeds the risk-free rate by an order of magnitude, the operator is a Ponzi" is the canonical user-side mitigation.

## Mitigations

- **Regulator-side:** enforcement actions (SEC civil, DOJ criminal, CFTC); investor-alert publication; cross-jurisdiction coordination on extradition and asset recovery.
- **User-side:** small-test-withdrawal-before-deposit invariant; refusal to deposit to platforms without verifiable trading history / audit / proof-of-reserves; awareness that guaranteed-yield claims above the risk-free rate by an order of magnitude are structurally Ponzi.
- **Off-chain CTI integration:** subscription to regulator-warning feeds; investigative-journalism cross-reference; corporate-registry verification automation for emerging "yield funds."

## Discussion

T11.005.002 is the **structurally oldest sub-Technique** in the T11.005 family, with the 2011 Bitcoin Savings & Trust case predating the pig-butchering era (T11.005.001, 2020–2026) by nearly a decade. The three-era lineage — Bitcoin forum Ponzi (2011–2017) → multi-asset wallet Ponzi (2018–2019) → global MLM "AI-trading" Ponzi (2020–2026) — demonstrates that the fake-custodian / fake-asset-manager structural class has been continuous and adaptive across crypto's infrastructure evolution, changing the victim-acquisition surface (forum → Telegram → MLM affinity-fraud) and the deposit substrate (BTC-only → multi-asset → stablecoin-dominant) while preserving the core Ponzi structure.

Bitcoin Savings & Trust (2011–2012) is the earliest structurally complete T11.005.002 case: forum-distributed fake-investment solicitation, fictitious yield-generation narrative (Bitcoin arbitrage), Ponzi payout structure, and operator-side diversion of deposits for personal use. The SEC v. Shavers precedent (2013) — the first judicial ruling that Bitcoin-denominated investments are securities under the Howey test — established the legal framework that underpins all subsequent federal enforcement against T11.005.002 operators.

The PlusToken case (2018–2019, ~$2–5.7B) is the largest-value single instance of T11.005.002 and possibly the largest Ponzi scheme in history by aggregate receipts. The case's scale — spanning Bitcoin, Ethereum, EOS, and other chains — demonstrated that the fake-custodian structural class scales to sovereign-wealth-fund-order extraction, and that the laundering surface for a T11.005.002 exit is a first-class forensic problem in its own right.

The modern enforcement-record cohort (HyperVerse, CryptoFX, Forsage, CoinDeal) demonstrates that the T11.005.002 structure is resilient across acquisition surfaces — HyperVerse operated globally through YouTube/TikTok/MLM channels; CryptoFX targeted the U.S. Latino community with Spanish-language affinity-fraud; Forsage used smart-contract MLM mechanics on Ethereum — but the underlying Ponzi structure (depositor-funded payouts, fictitious yield source, operator-controlled custodial wallet) is invariant.
