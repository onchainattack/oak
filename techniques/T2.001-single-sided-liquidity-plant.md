# OAK-T2.001 — Single-Sided Liquidity Plant

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T2 (Liquidity Establishment)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana
**First documented:** empirical (no canonical academic citation)
**Aliases:** "thin LP", "shallow pool", "single-side seed", "asymmetric seed"

## Description

A pool whose initial liquidity is provided overwhelmingly on one side, leaving the price highly sensitive to a small amount of counter-side trading. From a defender's perspective, the indicator is the depth-vs-FDV ratio at launch: a pool whose total quote-side depth is a small fraction of the token's claimed market cap is set up such that natural price discovery is impossible — the first few buy transactions move price by orders of magnitude, the deployer's pre-allocated holdings are revalued upward against thin counter-side liquidity, and the deployer extracts value via T5.001 (Hard LP Drain), T5.002 (Slow LP Trickle), or T1.001-style fee mutation against trapped buyers.

Distinction from legitimate bonding-curve launches (e.g. Pump.fun on Solana from January 2024 onward, Friend.tech, Bondi-curve memecoin venues): bonding-curve launchpads are *intentionally* single-sided as a venue design — the bonding curve is the price oracle, and the venue's published curve provides the predictable counter-side liquidity. T2.001 is about *concealed* single-sided seeding on AMM pools where market-cap framing implies two-sided depth that does not exist.

## Observed indicators

- LP `Mint` event on the AMM (Uniswap V2: `Mint(sender, amount0, amount1)`; Uniswap V3 / V4: `Mint(owner, tickLower, tickUpper, amount, amount0, amount1)`) with one side near-zero or zero relative to the claimed market cap.
- Initial pool reserves at first observable block such that quote-side depth (in WETH / USDC / SOL / native token) is orders of magnitude smaller than circulating-supply nominal value at the bonding-curve-implied price.
- LP token mint going to deployer-clustered addresses (M04 funder-graph) rather than to a public locker (M02 / T2.002 verification).
- Concentrated-liquidity range (Uniswap V3 / V4): position `tickLower` and `tickUpper` set in a way that constrains realistic trading bands to a narrow window around the expected dump price; e.g. a deployer setting a tight upper-tick window leaves no liquidity above it, so buyer-side pressure rapidly clears the range and price runs to the next tick gap.
- Asymmetric LP burn / lock — the deployer burns or locks only the smaller side of the pair, keeping authority over the dominant side.
- Cross-token cluster: deployer cluster previously seeded pools with the same depth-to-FDV ratio that subsequently underwent T5 extraction events.

## Detection signals

- **Static, at pool creation:** for every new V2 / V3 / V4 pool, compute depth/FDV ratio from the `Mint` event reserves and the token's total supply; flag any ratio below a chain-and-venue-calibrated threshold (e.g. quote-side depth < 0.5% of FDV at launch). Implementable as a Forta agent / The Graph subgraph subscribed to factory `PoolCreated` events.
- **Indexer query (Dune / Goldsky):** join `PoolCreated` events with the first `Mint` event per pool; output (pool, token, quote-token, depth-USD, FDV-USD, ratio); alert on ratio below threshold.
- **Behavioural, first-hour:** for newly-launched pools, sample swap events for the first N blocks; compute price-impact-per-swap-USD; if price-impact-per-USD exceeds threshold T (calibrated per chain), the pool is operationally single-sided regardless of static reserves.
- **Range-coverage check (V3 / V4):** for concentrated-liquidity positions, compute the price range covered by liquidity vs. the implied trading band from the launch price; flag positions whose range coverage is narrower than the venue baseline.
- **Cross-token cluster (M04):** if a deployer's prior pools matched the depth-to-FDV signature and subsequently extracted via T5, treat new pools from the same deployer as elevated-risk regardless of current pool's depth.

## Real-world examples

- **SQUID — BNB Chain — 2021-11-01** — Beyond the T1.001 transfer-restriction primitive, SQUID's PancakeSwap pool was seeded with thin counter-side liquidity that allowed the deployer cluster to dump pre-allocated supply against retail buyer pressure for several hours; the post-extraction LP residual was effectively single-sided (token-side) once the deployer had cycled out. Loss approximately $3.38M direct. Source: `[trmsquid2021]`.
- **AnubisDAO — Ethereum — 2021-10-29** — The launch LP setup combined T2.002 (no locked LP) with T2.001 conditions (initial pool depth was minimal relative to the claimed sale total of approximately 13,597 ETH). The drain occurred via deployer-controlled LP-token cash-out approximately 20 hours after launch. Source: `[decryptanubis2021]`, `[cointelegraphanubismixer2022]`.
- **Pump.fun bonding-curve cohort — Solana — 2024 onward** — Negative-control reference. Pump.fun's bonding-curve venue intentionally exposes single-sided "liquidity" via a venue-managed curve; this is *not* T2.001 because the curve itself is the publicly-disclosed price oracle and is not deployer-controlled. Cited here to anchor the discriminator: T2.001 is about *concealed* single-sided seeding on a market-cap-framing AMM pool, not about disclosed bonding-curve launchpads. Per-pool incidents within the broader memecoin-launchpad cohort that *graduate* from the bonding curve into a thin two-sided AMM pool routinely match T2.001 once on the AMM. <!-- TODO: cite https://www.solflare.com/ecosystem/pump-fun-where-memes-meet-markets-on-solana/ for the bonding-curve negative-control framing, add bib entry `solflarepumpfun2024` -->

## Mitigations

- **OAK-M04 (Funder-Graph Clustering)** — required: cluster deployers and surface prior T2.001 + T5 extraction history.
- **OAK-M02 (Static-Analysis Pre-Deployment)** — depth-to-FDV check at pool-creation time as a venue / launchpad listing gate.
- **OAK-M25 (Listing-Time Source-Verification + Audit-Status Gate)** — venue / launchpad listing precondition (publish minimum-liquidity-depth requirements).
- **OAK-M28 (Token-Unlock Calendar Integration)** — for projects with vesting / unlock schedules, ensure the unlock cadence is matched against the depth available to absorb sells without T2.001 re-emergence.

Operational: traders / risk teams require minimum quote-depth thresholds before considering entry; treat depth as a hard gate, not a heuristic. Launchpads publish minimum-liquidity-depth requirements as a listing prerequisite. Aggregators / wallet pre-trade UX should surface depth-USD and depth-to-FDV ratio as first-class fields, not just price.

## Discussion

Threshold calibration for "single-sided" varies by chain and venue; v0.2 may introduce per-venue calibration guidance. At v0.1 the baseline is depth-to-FDV ratio under approximately 0.5% on EVM-side V2 pools, with V3 / V4 concentrated-liquidity positions evaluated against range-coverage rather than headline depth. Solana-side calibration is materially different because Pump.fun-class bonding-curve venues have made single-sided launches the *default* — depth-to-FDV is uninformative without a venue-class discriminator.

Calibration gap at v0.1: distinguishing T2.001 from legitimate low-FDV early-stage launches with intentionally-thin LP requires venue-class context (was the launch on a bonding-curve venue? is the team publicly seeding with a vesting schedule?) that is not always machine-parseable from on-chain data alone. The pragmatic v0.1 resolution is to combine the static signal with the deployer-cluster signal (M04): T2.001 alone is "elevated risk", T2.001 + prior T5 extraction in the cluster is "high confidence malicious".

Attribution-strength caveat: per-incident T2.001 attribution rarely surfaces as the canonical post-mortem framing — the publicly-reportable narrative is typically the downstream T5.001 / T5.002 extraction event. Cohort-scale prevalence per the SolRPDS / TM-RugPull datasets is the more reliable cross-incident signal.

Sub-pattern split deferred to v0.x: V3 / V4 concentrated-liquidity range manipulation (narrow tick range, single-tick gaps) may warrant a separate T2.001.NN sub-Technique because the static-rule shape (range coverage vs. depth coverage) differs from the headline-depth signal.
