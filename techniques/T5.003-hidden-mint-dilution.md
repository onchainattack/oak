# OAK-T5.003 — Hidden-Mint Dilution

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T5 (Value Extraction)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana
**First documented:** 2021 (Xia et al. canonical Uniswap scam-token cohort)
**Aliases:** "stealth mint", "hidden inflation", "post-launch mint"

## Description

A token whose contract retains an authorised minting role that is exercised after holders have entered, increasing the supply held by deployer-controlled addresses while diluting existing holders. The Technique decomposes into a T1-level pre-event signal (mint authority exists in the deployed contract) and a T5-level event (mint is actually exercised against deployer-cluster recipients) — Technique pages cover both legs because both are required for end-to-end detection. T5.003 is distinct from T1.005 (hidden fee-on-transfer) in that the extraction primitive lives in **supply expansion**, not in transfer mechanics; it is distinct from T5.001 / T5.002 because the extraction does not require LP removal — the operator's freshly-minted tokens are sold into the pool against existing holder demand, transferring value directly via dilution rather than via LP-side withdrawal.

T5.003 also captures the variant where a smart-contract-platform-level vulnerability (rather than an explicit mint-role) lets an attacker inflate a token balance — e.g., reentrancy-into-mint or balance-tracking bugs in lending markets where the protocol's bookkeeping treats borrow + collateral as independent of the underlying ledger. These cases are scoped here because the defender-observable effect (post-event total-supply / circulating-supply diverging from documented schedule) and the mitigation surface (continuous on-chain supply reconciliation against expected schedule) are identical to the explicit-mint-role case.

## Observed indicators

- Contract retains a privileged mint role at deployment without an immutable cap or governance gating.
- Post-launch mint events to deployer-clustered addresses (OAK-M04 funder-graph attribution).
- Total supply trajectory inconsistent with the published tokenomics — pre/post block-balance diff exceeds cumulative `Mint` event sum (suggesting a non-standard mint path).
- Solana-specific: SPL `MintTo` instructions emitted by a non-renounced mint authority post-listing; SPL Token-2022 `permanent-delegate` extension exercised to materialise tokens at deployer-controlled accounts (pairs with OAK-T1.002).
- Lending-market variant: pool-level totalSupply / totalBorrow diverges from underlying-asset reserve in a single block following a flagged transaction (reentrancy-into-mint signal).

## Detection signals

- Static (pre-event): presence of mint authority unbounded by hard cap or governance; non-zero `mintAuthority` on Solana SPL mints; ERC-20 `mint(address,uint256)` callable by a non-multisig EOA without time-lock (OAK-M02 static-analysis).
- Real-time: mint events to clusters flagged in earlier Tactics; alert on any `Transfer(0x0, deployer-cluster, X)` event where `X` is non-trivial relative to circulating supply.
- Reconciliation: compare on-chain `totalSupply` against published tokenomics on a recurring cadence (per-block on EVM, per-slot on Solana); divergence beyond declared emission schedule is the canonical T5.003 signal.
- Cross-event balance-diff: in a single block, sum of all `Transfer` `from=0x0` events against the change in `totalSupply` should match exactly; mismatches indicate a non-standard supply-mutation path (e.g., direct storage write, balance-tracking bug, reentrancy-into-mint).
- OAK-M03 continuous bytecode-diff monitor: if mint-authority assignment is patched in via proxy upgrade or storage write after deployment, the bytecode-diff (or storage-slot-diff) is the upstream signal.

## Real-world examples

- **Uniswap scam-token cohort (Xia et al. 2021)** — `[xia2021mintdump]` provides the canonical labelled cohort: a large-scale Uniswap scam-token study including hidden-mint cases as a documented sub-class with on-chain artefacts and per-token dilution traces. This is the academic-grade primary reference for T5.003.
- **Rari Capital / Fei Fuse pools — Ethereum — 2022-04-30** — Loss \~\$80M. The exploit was a reentrancy on the Fuse-pool `exitMarket()` path that let the attacker effectively duplicate borrow-side balances against single-side collateral — a balance-inflation primitive functionally equivalent to an unauthorised mint against the lending market's bookkeeping. Borderline classification: the primary attack surface is reentrancy (OAK-T9 family), but the defender-observable effect is unauthorised supply expansion of the pool's debt-token / cToken-equivalent, which is the T5.003 signature. ## Mitigations

- **OAK-M02 (static-analysis pre-deployment):** flag retained mint authority on listing intake; require either renounced authority or governance-gated authority for clean-listing status.
- **OAK-M17 (time-locked governance and multi-block quorum):** if mint authority is governance-held, require time-lock on all mint-execution paths so any minting is observable in advance.
- **OAK-M03 (continuous bytecode-diff monitoring):** detect post-deployment introduction of mint-paths via proxy upgrade.
- **OAK-M25 (listing-time source-verification and audit-status gate):** venues require T1.002-equivalent SPL-permanent-delegate disclosure and ERC-20 mint-authority disclosure as pre-listing data.
- **Traders / risk teams:** filter out tokens with active mint authority unless authority is on a published trusted-authority allowlist; treat tokenomics-vs-on-chain-supply reconciliation as a recurring (not one-time) check.
- **Protocols / launchpads:** require either renounced mint authority or explicit governance-gated mint authority before listing.

## Discussion

Token standards differ significantly in how mint authority is represented (EVM ERC-20 vs Solana SPL vs SPL Token-2022 with `permanent-delegate`). v0.2 should split T5.003 into chain-specific sub-techniques if the detection methodology diverges materially: the EVM signal is contract-source / role-grant-event analysis; the Solana SPL signal is mint-authority-account state plus extension flags; SPL Token-2022 adds permanent-delegate and confidential-transfer surfaces that have no EVM analogue. Calibration gap (v0.x): the lending-market / smart-contract-bug variant of T5.003 (Rari-class) is currently scoped here based on defender-observable effect (supply expansion), but the underlying primitive is distinct from "explicit retained mint role" cases. A v0.2 split could promote the lending-market variant to its own sub-Technique under T9 (smart-contract exploit) with T5.003 cross-reference, mirroring how T6.001 modifies T1 / T9 across Tactics. Attribution caveats: where the mint-recipient is a protocol-level address (e.g., a treasury or staking contract) rather than a deployer-cluster address, classification depends on whether the destination is a transparent governance-controlled venue or a deployer-aliased address; OAK-M05 authority-graph enumeration is the load-bearing distinguisher.
