# OAK-T7.009 — Sanctioned-Entity and Illicit-Purpose Financing

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T7 (Laundering), OAK-T8 (Attribution)
**Maturity:** observed
**Chains:** Bitcoin (primary for OFAC-designated addresses), Ethereum, TRON (increasing share of illicit-purpose transaction volume post-2022)
**First documented:** 2013 (Silk Road-era designated-entity tracking); formalised 2018–2020 with OFAC crypto-designation framework and FATF Travel Rule
**Aliases:** "designated-entity financing", "sanctions-evasion crypto", "illicit-content monetisation", "terrorism-financing crypto", "KYT red-flag screening"

## Description

A class of OAK-T7 patterns in which cryptocurrency is used to finance entities, activities, or content categories that are subject to international sanctions, law-enforcement designation, or treaty-obligation prohibition — including OFAC SDN-designated entities, UN-sanctioned organisations, terrorism-financing networks, and CSAM (child sexual abuse material) commercial operations. The on-chain laundering primitive is structurally identical to other T7 sub-Techniques (mixer routing, CEX layering, cross-chain hopping, privacy-chain migration), and T7.009 does not name a distinct laundering *method*. Rather, T7.009 names the **designated-destination and illicit-purpose** layer that triggers regulatory-screening obligations at the compliance infrastructure layer (KYT/AML screening, suspicious-activity reporting, law-enforcement referral) and that distinguishes these flows from economically-motivated laundering (T7.001–T7.008).

From a defender's perspective, T7.009 is the canonical case of **purpose-based rather than method-based detection**: the detection signal is not the laundering pattern (which may be indistinguishable from ransomware, darknet-market, or exchange-hack laundering) but the **destination-address attribution** — whether the receiving address is on a published designation list (OFAC SDN, UN sanctions, INTERPOL warrant, national law-enforcement referral) or is linked via blockchain-intelligence clustering to a designated entity or illicit-content commercial network. Detection is therefore a function of the **address-labelling coverage and freshness** maintained by blockchain-intelligence providers (Chainalysis, TRM Labs, Elliptic, CipherTrace), not of a novel on-chain pattern.

T7.009 is structurally distinct from T7.001–T7.008 because those Techniques name laundering *methods* (mixer, CEX, bridge, NFT, privacy chain, DeFi yield, DEX aggregator, stablecoin issuer) whereas T7.009 names the *purpose-and-destination* layer. An address receiving CSAM-commercial proceeds may use any of T7.001–T7.008 for the laundering leg; the T7.009 signal is in the address labelling, not in the laundering method.

## Observed indicators

- A deposit, withdrawal, or transfer to or from a cryptocurrency address listed on the U.S. Treasury OFAC SDN List, the EU Consolidated Sanctions List, the UN Security Council Sanctions List, or a national law-enforcement designation list — the canonical T7.009 signal at the compliance-screening layer.
- A deposit to a CEX from an address that Chainalysis, TRM Labs, Elliptic, or another blockchain-intelligence provider has labelled with a CSAM, terrorism-financing, or sanctions-evasion category tag — the KYT/AML screening-level detection.
- A transaction pattern consistent with Hawala-style value transfer using cryptocurrency as the settlement rail: round-number transfers between addresses in different jurisdictions without a corresponding on-chain commercial transaction, often involving high-risk-jurisdiction CEXs as intermediary nodes.
- Recurring small-value payments (sub-$100) to a cluster of addresses with high address-reuse rates — the structural on-chain pattern for CSAM commercial content payment models (subscription, pay-per-view) that distinguishes CSAM-related flows from larger, less-frequent ransomware or hack-related flows.
- Fundraising-campaign addresses promoted on social media or messaging platforms by designated entities or their affiliates, with donation inflows from geographically dispersed addresses — the structural T7.009 signal for terrorism-financing fundraising.

## Detection signals

- **Regulatory screening (primary, highest-precision):** screen all inbound and outbound cryptocurrency transactions against OFAC SDN, EU, UN, and national designation lists. A positive match is a definitive T7.009 signal regardless of laundering method.
- **Blockchain-intelligence labelling (secondary):** integrate Chainalysis, TRM Labs, Elliptic, or equivalent labelling feeds into transaction screening; alert on any transaction involving an address with a CSAM, terrorism-financing, or sanctions-evasion category label.
- **Behavioural-pattern screening (tertiary, lower-precision):** flag recurring small-value payments to previously-uninteracted addresses in jurisdictions with limited law-enforcement cryptocurrency capability; flag fundraising-campaign address patterns (many small inbound transfers, single consolidation outbound transfer); flag round-number cross-border transfers consistent with Hawala-style settlement.
- **CEX KYC/AML integration:** require KYC-verified identity for all deposit and withdrawal addresses; screen deposit-address provenance against designation lists; file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) with FinCEN (or equivalent national FIU) for any T7.009-positive transaction.
- **Cross-institutional coordination:** maintain information-sharing agreements between blockchain-intelligence providers, NGOs (International Centre for Missing & Exploited Children, Internet Watch Foundation), and law-enforcement agencies for CSAM-related address labelling and rapid referral.

## Reference implementations

- OFAC SDN List — the definitive regulatory-designation source for sanctioned cryptocurrency addresses. The SDN List has included cryptocurrency addresses since November 2018.
- Chainalysis, TRM Labs, Elliptic — blockchain-intelligence providers maintaining labelled-address datasets for CSAM, terrorism-financing, and sanctions-evasion categories.
- FATF Recommendation 15 (Virtual Assets) and Recommendation 16 (Wire Transfers / Travel Rule) — the international regulatory framework mandating KYT/AML screening for virtual-asset transactions.
- Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) and International Centre for Missing & Exploited Children (ICMEC) — NGO partners for CSAM-related address labelling and referral.

## Mitigations

- **CEXs and virtual-asset service providers (VASPs) — regulatory-compliance layer (OAK-M26):** implement real-time transaction screening against OFAC SDN, EU, UN, and national designation lists; maintain a blockchain-intelligence provider integration for labelled-address screening; file SARs for all T7.009-positive transactions; maintain a compliance-officer workflow for rapid escalation of T7.009-positive matches to law enforcement.
- **Blockchain-intelligence providers:** maintain and refresh labelled-address datasets for CSAM, terrorism-financing, and sanctions-evasion categories; provide API-level integration for CEX and VASP screening; coordinate with law enforcement and NGOs for address-labelling referrals.
- **Law enforcement:** maintain cryptocurrency-address designation lists; coordinate cross-jurisdiction seizure and forfeiture operations for designated-entity cryptocurrency assets; participate in the international Counter-Ransomware Initiative (CRI), the Global Coalition to Address Synthetic Drug Threats, and the international CSAM-crypto working groups for address-labelling coordination.
- **NGOs and civil society:** maintain referral mechanisms for CSAM-related cryptocurrency address reporting; coordinate with blockchain-intelligence providers for address labelling and with law enforcement for follow-on investigation.

## Discussion

T7.009 is the OAK technique that most clearly illustrates the **purpose-vs-method distinction** in the T7 family. T7.001–T7.008 each name a distinct on-chain method for moving value while obscuring provenance; T7.009 names the legal-and-regulatory destination layer that triggers compliance obligations independently of the method used. An investigator tracing a CSAM-commercial payment through a mixer (T7.001), a CEX (T7.002), a cross-chain bridge (T7.003), and a privacy chain (T7.005) is observing T7.009 at the destination-attribution layer and T7.001/T7.002/T7.003/T7.005 at the method layer simultaneously — the T7.009 detection is in the destination labelling, not in any specific laundering pattern.

A calibration caveat (v0.1+): CSAM-related and terrorism-financing cryptocurrency transaction data is systematically undercounted in public reporting. Law enforcement and blockchain-intelligence providers treat operational address-labelling data as sensitive and do not publish comprehensive labelled-address datasets. OAK's T7.009 coverage is therefore a **methodology-and-compliance-framework** reference rather than an incident-enumeration reference, consistent with the sensitive-law-enforcement-data constraint that applies to these categories. Future OAK versions may split T7.009 into sub-Techniques along the purpose axis (T7.009.001 — Sanctions Evasion; T7.009.002 — Terrorism Financing; T7.009.003 — CSAM Commercial Monetisation) if the public forensic record becomes rich enough to characterise per-sub-category detection methodology distinctly.
