# OAK-T16.003 — Delegation-Cluster Vote Takeover

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T16 (Governance / Voting Manipulation)
**Adjacent Techniques:** OAK-T9.003 (generic Governance Attack class — preserved on every T16.003 worked example), OAK-T8.001 (Operational Reuse — Cluster-Reuse Signal — load-bearing for delegation-graph attribution), OAK-T16.002 (Hostile-Vote Treasury Drain — frequently co-occurs when the delegation cluster also holds direct positions)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (canonical anchors at Compound, Balancer, SushiSwap); cross-chain governance contracts inherit the surface where delegation-graph mechanics exist
**First documented:** 2022-2024 (Humpy pattern-of-conduct at Balancer / SushiSwap as antecedents; Compound Proposal 289 July 2024 as the canonical anchor)
**Aliases:** "delegation pull attack", "delegate-takeover", "delegation cluster", "delegate-coordination governance attack", "cohort-coordinated vote", "Humpy-class governance accumulation"

## Description

Acquisition of governance voting power through **delegation pull** from a cluster of compromised, controlled, or coordinating wallets, used to push a malicious proposal through a protocol's governance. The defining shape is **multi-wallet → single-delegate concentration** of voting weight: the underlying token holdings remain distributed across the cluster, but the delegation graph collapses the voting weight to a single delegate (or a tightly-coordinated set of delegates) sufficient to clear quorum. The accumulation window is typically multi-week to multi-month, distinguishing this from the single-block T16.001 (flash-loan) and from T16.002 (direct accumulation against thin governance token).

T16.003 is the canonical Compound Proposal 289 pattern. The July 2024 case is the textbook chain: the "Golden Boys" cohort (including the wallet associated with the on-chain pseudonymous identity "Humpy") accumulated COMP across multiple addresses and pulled delegation across those addresses to a coordinating delegate, reaching the COMP governance quorum threshold. The cohort then submitted Compound Proposal 289 directing \~499,000 COMP (\~\$24M) from the Compound DAO treasury to a "goldCOMP" yield-bearing vault effectively controlled by the cohort. The proposal *passed the on-chain vote* — but was *prevented* in the pre-execution timelock review window through community recognition of the self-dealing pattern, counter-proposal deployment, and direct cohort-side participation in the cancellation. The case is the canonical T16.003 anchor and the canonical worked example for the **prevented-attempt outcome** in the T16 corpus.

T16.003 is **structurally distinct from T16.001 (Vote Takeover via Flash-Loan)** in *acquisition mechanism* and *atomicity window*. T16.001 acquires voting power inside a single flash-loan-bracketed block via stablecoin-to-governance-token conversion; T16.003 acquires voting power across a multi-week accumulation window via direct purchases plus delegation pull, and holds the position throughout the vote and into the post-vote timelock window. Mitigation differs: snapshot-based voting power (T16.001 mitigation) does *not* close T16.003 because the attacker holds the position at every snapshot; closing T16.003 requires governance-process-layer mitigation (cohort-level surveillance of delegation flows, counter-proposal operational capability, paired with the timelock review window).

T16.003 is **structurally distinct from T16.002 (Hostile-Vote Treasury Drain)** in *holder topology*. T16.002 attackers hold voting weight directly (or have it inflated via T9.001 against a directly-held position); voting weight tracks token holdings 1:1. T16.003 attackers concentrate voting weight via delegation graph from a multi-wallet cluster; the underlying token holdings remain distributed. The mitigation surfaces overlap (cohort-surveillance, counter-proposal capability) but the detection signals differ: T16.002 surfaces in the holder-distribution dashboard; T16.003 surfaces in the delegation-graph dashboard. Many T16.003 cases co-occur with T16.002 (the Compound 289 cohort had both direct holdings and delegation-pulled weight); contributors should map both when both mechanisms are load-bearing.

T16.003 covers two structurally adjacent sub-shapes:

- **Coordinated multi-wallet delegation pull from a publicly-known cohort.** A cohort of coordinating actors (publicly identified or pseudonymous-but-traceable via T8.001 cluster-reuse signals) accumulates governance tokens across multiple addresses and pulls delegation to a single delegate to reach quorum. Canonical anchor: Compound Proposal 289 (Golden Boys / Humpy).
- **Compromised-wallet delegation pull (cluster takeover via key compromise).** Attacker compromises a set of wallets via T11.x / T15.x / T4.x and pulls delegation from each compromised wallet to an attacker-controlled delegate. Distinct from the cohort-coordination sub-shape because the underlying wallets are not voluntary participants; the attribution and mitigation surfaces are different (T11.x / T15.x mitigation upstream, T8.001 cluster-attribution downstream). v0.1 has the class but no canonical anchor at scale; future contributors should anchor it once a public-record case lands.

The T16.003 surface is gated by two design conditions: (a) the protocol's governance contract supports delegation as a primary voting-weight aggregation primitive (Compound Governor Bravo's `delegate` pattern, OpenZeppelin Governor's checkpoint-based delegation), and (b) the protocol's quorum threshold can be cleared by the cohort's accumulated delegation-pulled voting weight. Both conditions hold across the modern Governor-Bravo-pattern DAO ecosystem; T16.003 is therefore a standing surface across most top-tier DeFi DAOs from the day the governance contract is deployed.

## Observed indicators

- **Pre-event (governance-design layer):** delegation as primary voting-weight aggregation primitive; quorum threshold below the cohort's accumulated delegation-pulled voting weight; absence of conflict-of-interest disclosure enforcement at proposal-submission time.
- **At-event (delegation-graph signal):** multi-wallet delegation pull to a single delegate (or tightly-coordinated delegate set) within a narrow window prior to a contested proposal; delegation graph topology that concentrates voting weight from a cluster whose underlying funding-graph signal overlaps T8.001 cluster-reuse.
- **At-event (cohort-coordination signal):** the wallets contributing delegation share funding-graph or operational-reuse signals (T8.001) — common funder ancestor, common withdrawal-from-CEX cluster, common deployer / signer cluster; the publicly-known cohort identification (Humpy / Golden Boys at Compound) is the canonical example of this signal at the governance-forum layer.
- **At-event (proposal-text-vs-payload divergence):** proposal text frames the action in routine-governance language but the on-chain calldata directs treasury, voting power, or upgrade authority to the proposing cohort or to a vehicle controlled by the cohort.
- **Post-vote (timelock-window signal):** the post-vote review window is where T16.003 is detected and prevented (Compound 2024) or realised (when the timelock is short or absent). Absence of operational counter-proposal capability is the load-bearing failure mode.
- **Forensic record:** the delegation-graph snapshot at proposal-creation time; the proposing cohort's on-chain accumulation pattern (typically visible in Tally / Boardroom / DAO-analytics dashboards); the cohort's prior pattern-of-conduct at adjacent protocols (Humpy at Balancer / SushiSwap as the canonical pre-Compound-289 antecedent record).

## Detection signals

- **Cohort-level surveillance of delegation flows:** monitor governance-token delegation events across publicly-known coordinating cohorts and across delegation-graph topology that concentrates voting weight from a multi-wallet cluster; alert on patterns that approach quorum-relevant threshold.
- **T8.001 cluster-attribution applied to delegation graph:** the cluster-reuse signal at the funding / operational layer is the leading indicator for T16.003 risk. Cohorts with prior pattern-of-conduct at adjacent protocols (Humpy at Balancer → SushiSwap → Compound 289) are particularly load-bearing.
- **DAO-analytics dashboard integration (Tally / Boardroom / DeepDAO):** these tools surface delegation-graph topology, voting-history-by-actor, and cohort-coordination signals at the governance-process layer; the Compound 289 case validates this defender instrumentation.
- **Proposal-payload-vs-beneficiary correlation check:** static analysis of proposal calldata at submission time, with explicit check that the proposal beneficiary does not overlap the proposing cohort or the cohort's delegation-pulled wallets.
- **Counter-proposal operational capability:** rehearsed mechanism for deploying a counter-proposal during the post-vote timelock review window; the Compound 2024 case validates this primitive.
- **CTI integration on cross-protocol cohort actors:** maintain watchlists of publicly-named governance-coordination cohorts (Humpy / Golden Boys and similar pattern-of-conduct actors) across DAO governance forums.

## Real-world examples

- **Compound DAO Proposal 289 — Ethereum — 2024-07-28** — Loss \$0 (attempted but prevented). Canonical T16.003 case. The "Golden Boys" cohort (including the wallet associated with the pseudonymous identity "Humpy") accumulated COMP voting power across multiple addresses through direct on-chain purchases and delegation pull over the months preceding July 2024, then submitted Proposal 289 directing \~499,000 COMP (\~\$24M) from the Compound DAO treasury to a "goldCOMP" yield-bearing vault effectively controlled by the cohort. The proposal *passed the on-chain vote* — the cohort's accumulated delegation-pulled voting power, plus delegations from supporting holders, cleared quorum and the simple-majority threshold. Between vote-passage and the timelock-gated execution window, the broader Compound community recognised the proposal as self-dealing and deployed an emergency counter-proposal, cancelling the malicious treasury action before the timelock expired. The case is the canonical T16.003 anchor and the canonical OAK example of a *prevented* T16-class attack. See [`examples/2024-07-compound-vote-takeover.md`](../examples/2024-07-compound-vote-takeover.md).
- **Cohort context — Humpy pattern-of-conduct at Balancer and SushiSwap** — Pre-Compound-289 antecedent record. Humpy's pattern of accumulating governance tokens for self-dealing or rent-extracting proposals was previously documented at Balancer and SushiSwap. The cohort's coordination was publicly visible for months before Proposal 289; the Compound community's ability to recognise Proposal 289 as self-dealing depended on this prior visibility. The cross-protocol pattern-of-conduct is the canonical T8.001 + T16.003 cohort-surveillance signal.

## Mitigations

- **Protocols (OAK-M17):** time-locked governance + multi-block quorum is a baseline requirement; the timelock window is load-bearing as a *review window* paired with operational counter-proposal capability. Compound's Governor Bravo design retains the timelock as a mandatory review window; the 2024 case validates this primitive.
- **Protocols — counter-proposal operational capability:** rehearsed mechanism for deploying a counter-proposal during the timelock review window. The Compound 2024 case is the canonical example: a passed-vote governance attack on a top-tier protocol was prevented in the timelock window through community action. The defender lesson is that the timelock window is operationally useful only when paired with active counter-proposal capability.
- **Protocols — conflict-of-interest disclosure:** governance-process-layer requirement that proposers disclose any beneficiary relationship; particularly load-bearing when the proposing cohort's delegation-graph signal indicates a coordinating multi-wallet structure.
- **Cohort-level surveillance:** track publicly-identified governance-coordination cohorts across protocols; treat their accumulation patterns at any new protocol as elevated-risk for T16.003. T8.001 cluster-attribution applied to delegation flows is the operational shape.
- **Protocols (OAK-M02):** proposal-payload static analysis with explicit check that the proposal beneficiary does not overlap the proposing cohort or its delegation-pulled wallet cluster.
- **DAO-process layer — pre-vote review cadence:** discoverable governance-process discipline that any treasury-affecting proposal opens a multi-day pre-vote discussion window where the proposer's cohort attribution is publicly surfaced before voting begins.
- **Risk teams:** for governance-token holdings, monitor delegation flows for any pattern that concentrates voting weight from a multi-wallet cluster; maintain per-protocol delegation-graph + cohort-attribution inventories.

## Discussion

T16.003's structural lesson is that the **delegation graph itself is an attack surface** distinct from the underlying token-holding distribution. A protocol whose holder distribution looks decentralised at the token-balance layer can be governance-takeover-vulnerable at the delegation-graph layer if a coordinating cohort can pull delegation from a multi-wallet cluster to a single delegate. Defender-side instrumentation that monitors only the holder-distribution dashboard misses the delegation-graph signal entirely; the Compound 2024 case validates the load-bearing role of Tally / Boardroom / DAO-analytics dashboards that surface the delegation graph as a first-class signal.

The cohort is structurally distinct from T16.001 by acquisition-mechanism (T16.001 = flash-loan; T16.003 = delegation pull from multi-wallet cluster), and from T16.002 by holder-topology (T16.002 = direct holdings or T9.001-inflated; T16.003 = delegation-graph concentration with distributed underlying holdings). Many T16.003 cases co-occur with T16.002 (the Compound 289 cohort had both direct holdings and delegation-pulled weight); contributors should map both when both mechanisms are load-bearing.

Attribution-strength notes: Compound Proposal 289 is `pseudonymous` with `publicly-named cohort` attribution status. The Golden Boys cohort and the wallet associated with the pseudonymous identity Humpy were publicly identified at the governance-forum / on-chain-analytics layer; individual cohort members are pseudonymous-to-identified at varying levels. The publicly-named-cohort attribution status is the load-bearing T16.003 detection primitive: the Compound community's ability to recognise Proposal 289 as self-dealing within the pre-execution review window depended on the prior visibility of Humpy's pattern-of-conduct at Balancer and SushiSwap. Cross-protocol cohort surveillance (T8.001 + T16.003) is therefore the canonical defender practice, not single-protocol monitoring in isolation.

False-positive considerations: legitimate delegation flows are routine in modern DAO governance — large holders delegate to subject-matter-expert delegates as a routine governance-process pattern. The detection signal is not "delegation flow" but specifically "delegation flow from a cohort whose funding-graph / operational-reuse signature overlaps T8.001 cluster-reuse, where the cohort's accumulated voting weight reaches quorum-relevant threshold and the resulting proposal benefits the cohort itself." Cohort-level analysis confirms that the legitimate use-case for "coordinating cohort delegates to itself and proposes a self-dealing payload" essentially does not exist outside attacker / rent-extractor behaviour.

v0.x deferred items: the compromised-wallet delegation-pull sub-shape (where the cluster wallets are not voluntary participants but are compromised via T11.x / T15.x / T4.x) lacks a canonical anchor at v0.1 and should be anchored once a public-record case lands; treatment of cross-DAO operator continuity (a single cohort accumulating governance positions across multiple DAOs concurrently) as a distinct sub-shape that may deserve its own sub-Technique once the cohort-record matures; the boundary against legitimate-but-aggressive governance-coordination (delegate consortia operating openly with disclosed beneficiary relationships) which is structurally adjacent to T16.003 but mitigation-wise belongs at the governance-process / disclosure layer rather than at the contract-enforcement layer.
