# OAK-T11.004 — Insufficient-Entropy Key Generation

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T11 (Custody and Signing Infrastructure)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** chain-agnostic (any ECDSA / EdDSA-curve chain whose end-user keys are produced by an off-chain generator); canonical anchors on Ethereum
**First documented:** 2022-09 (Profanity vanity-address generator public disclosure by 1inch); the structural class is older (Bitcoin "RNG bug" cohorts predate the canonical Ethereum case but are not the v0.1 anchor)
**Aliases:** "weak-RNG key generation", "vanity-address entropy collapse", "Profanity-class private-key recovery", "32-bit-seed key recovery"

## Description

A class of custody-side vulnerabilities in which the **off-chain key-generation tool** seeds its CSPRNG with materially less entropy than the curve floor (typically a 32-bit seed against a 256-bit curve), reducing the effective private-key search space from `~2^256` to a brute-forceable bound (`~2^32` for the canonical case). Once the entropy-collapse is publicly disclosed, the affected keys can be recovered offline by any sufficiently-resourced attacker — typically GPU-feasible in hours-to-days for the 32-bit-seed shape — and the on-chain assets controlled by those keys are extracted in a sweep. From a defender's perspective, T11.004 is structurally distinct from other T11.x Techniques: there is no UI compromise (T11.001), no malicious wallet binary (T11.002), no on-chain multisig modification (T11.003); the keys themselves are cryptographically defective at the moment of generation, and the on-chain artefact is indistinguishable from a normal signed transaction at the consensus layer.

T11.004's load-bearing operational property is the **half-life-of-known-vulnerability-after-disclosure**: even after a high-profile cryptographic disclosure with explicit rotation guidance, a non-trivial fraction of affected addresses remain un-rotated and continue to be drained over a long tail. The Profanity / Wintermute cohort case (`examples/2022-09-wintermute-profanity-cohort.md`) is the v0.1 evidence that this metric is non-trivial — extractions from un-rotated Profanity-generated addresses continued for months after the September 2022 disclosure. Defender intuition that "publicly disclosed = quickly rotated" does not match the field record; the cohort tail is the operational lesson contributors should preserve when documenting future T11.004-class incidents.

## Observed indicators

- A public disclosure by a security researcher or vendor (1inch, Halborn, an academic group) of an entropy-collapse flaw in a named key-generation tool, with proof-of-concept private-key recovery and explicit rotation guidance.
- Coordinated burst of extraction events across distinct end-user wallet addresses whose **address-generation provenance is traceable to the affected tool** (vanity-pattern fingerprint, deployer-tooling fingerprint, leading-zeros pattern characteristic of the gas-saving rationale that drove tool adoption).
- Cohort-tail extractions: small-scale, sporadic drains from un-rotated affected addresses continuing weeks or months after the disclosure window.
- Funding-cluster overlap: the destination wallets of multiple cohort extractions share funding sources, consistent with one-or-more attackers running the recovery campaign at scale rather than individual-per-victim opportunism (note: the Profanity cohort spans both single-attacker sweep activity and multi-attacker opportunistic exploitation; defenders should not collapse cohort attribution to a single operator).

## Detection signals

- **Address-generation-pattern enumeration.** Once an entropy-collapse disclosure lands, the affected address space is computable: enumerate the addresses with non-trivial balances that match the affected tool's generation fingerprint and treat them as a cohort-level alert population. The pre-disclosure signal a third-party defender could read is the cohort *size*; the post-disclosure signal is the cohort *exploitation rate*.
- **Rotation-discipline monitoring.** For known cohort populations, monitor on-chain activity for rotation events (transfers from the affected address to a fresh, generator-clean destination) versus extraction events. The rotation-vs-extraction ratio in the post-disclosure window is a first-class operational metric for the cohort.
- **Deployer / admin authority chase.** Where an affected address holds admin / deployer authority over downstream contracts, the attacker reaches contract-layer assets in addition to the EOA-layer balance. Defenders monitoring affected addresses must track downstream contract authority, not only direct balances.
- **Cohort-tail surveillance.** Months after the disclosure, sporadic extractions from un-rotated cohort members continue; persistent watchlists on the affected address population produce a long-tail signal that single-window monitoring misses.

## Real-world examples

- **Wintermute — Ethereum — 2022-09-20** — Loss \~\$160M (canonical large-institutional anchor). Wintermute had used the Profanity vanity-address generator to produce a gas-saving leading-zeros vault admin address; after the 2022-09-15 1inch disclosure, Wintermute partially responded (moved ETH out of the directly-exposed hot wallet) but did not revoke admin authority, and the attacker extracted via the un-rotated admin path. Halborn published the canonical post-mortem. See worked example at [`examples/2022-09-wintermute.md`](../examples/2022-09-wintermute.md).
- **Profanity vanity-address cohort — Ethereum — 2022-09-15 to 2022-12 (cohort case)** — Loss \~\$3.3M+ extracted from non-Wintermute Profanity-affected wallets in the September 2022 window plus a sporadic cohort tail through Q4 2022 / 2023. Multiple pseudonymous attackers across the cohort window (some larger extractions show G01-laundering-pattern similarities; smaller extractions consistent with opportunistic exploitation by independent actors). The cohort is OAK's canonical worked example for the *publicly-known-vulnerability-not-rotated* operational pattern in its small-and-mid-tier-victim regime. See worked example at [`examples/2022-09-wintermute-profanity-cohort.md`](../examples/2022-09-wintermute-profanity-cohort.md).
- **Cohort context** — `[1inchprofanity2022]` documents the 32-bit-seed CPRNG flaw with proof-of-concept private-key recovery and explicit rotation guidance. `[halbornwintermute2022]` is the function-level walk-through of the un-rotated-admin-authority failure mode. The Profanity cohort tail is the v0.1 evidence that the half-life of known cryptographic vulnerabilities after disclosure is materially longer than defender intuition suggests.

## Reference implementations

- `mg-detectors-rs` — coverage **gap** at v0.1. T11.004's primary detection surface is *post-disclosure cohort enumeration* (computing the affected address space from the tool's generation fingerprint) plus rotation-vs-extraction monitoring; both are batch-analytics rather than per-transaction risk-engine surfaces.
- Vendor coverage outside MG: cryptographic-disclosure-feed monitoring (CERT, vendor security blogs, audit-firm advisories); on-chain analytics providers (Chainalysis, TRM, Elliptic) for cohort cluster-attribution forensics; wallet-provider rotation-prompt UX (Metamask / Trust Wallet / Rabby etc. did not surface a Profanity-rotation prompt at scale; the cohort tail is partly a UX failure).

## Mitigations

- **Tool-side:** key-generation tools must seed CSPRNGs with full curve-floor entropy from a vetted source (`/dev/urandom`, hardware RNG, OS crypto API). Rolling your own RNG seeding is the canonical anti-pattern; the Profanity case demonstrates that "32-bit seed against 256-bit curve" can ship in widely-adopted open-source tooling without being challenged for years.
- **User-side:** treat any address generated by a vanity / custom key-generation tool as elevated risk; rotate any address whose generation tool has not been independently audited for entropy seeding; immediate rotation on any disclosure of CSPRNG flaws in the generation tool, including admin / deployer authority on downstream contracts, not only EOA balances.
- **Wallet-vendor / venue-side:** post-disclosure rotation prompts surfaced to affected user populations; cohort-level alerts to identifiable affected users where the wallet provider has the address-generation provenance signal; institutional custody operators should maintain an inventory of generation-tool provenance for their key-set and apply post-disclosure rotation as a first-class operational runbook.
- **Cohort-tail monitoring:** post-disclosure persistent watchlists on the affected address population, not single-window monitoring. The half-life-of-known-vulnerability-after-disclosure is non-trivial; the operational rotation-discipline metric is what makes the cohort tail actionable.

OAK Mitigation cross-references at v0.1 are a partial gap — no existing OAK-M* entry directly maps to the post-disclosure cohort-enumeration / rotation-discipline-monitoring mitigation surface. Candidate v0.x entries: "OAK-M-x post-disclosure cohort rotation monitoring", "OAK-M-x generation-tool-provenance custody inventory".

## Discussion

T11.004 is structurally distinct from the rest of the T11 family in a way contributors should preserve: the failure mode is **purely cryptographic at the generation moment** and is not detectable by any on-chain or runtime signal until the disclosure lands. T11.001 (vendor UI compromise), T11.002 (wallet-binary distribution compromise), T11.003 (in-use multisig manipulation), T11.005 (operator-side fake platform), T11.006 (cold-storage seed-phrase exfiltration), T11.007 (hardware-wallet supply-chain), T11.008 (embedded-wallet IDP compromise), T11.009 (trader-tooling supply-chain) all involve *some* observable artefact at compromise time — a build-pipeline event, a UI substitution, a runtime contract change, a credential-flow anomaly. T11.004 has none: the keys are valid at the protocol layer, and the only signal is the public cryptographic disclosure that converts the cohort population into an exploitable target.

The Profanity cohort case (`examples/2022-09-wintermute-profanity-cohort.md`) and the Wintermute canonical-incident case (`examples/2022-09-wintermute.md`) form a paired worked-example structure (cohort + canonical-incident) that captures the same failure mode at two scales: institutional (Wintermute, \~\$160M, large-institutional response timeline) and population (cohort, \~\$3.3M+ across many small-and-mid-tier victims, operational-rotation-discipline timeline). Future T11.004 worked examples should preserve both scales where the public record supports them, rather than collapsing the institutional-scale anchor and the cohort-tail evidence into a single incident frame. The half-life-of-known-vulnerability-after-disclosure is a load-bearing operational metric for the class and is materially different from the canonical-incident-response-timeline metric that anchors single-incident T11.x cases.

The class is `stable` at v0.1 because the forensic chain — Profanity's open-source 32-bit-seed CPRNG → 1inch's disclosure with proof-of-concept private-key recovery → Halborn's function-level post-mortem → Wintermute's institutional-scale extraction → cohort-tail extractions across the Profanity-affected population — is multi-source-attested and the structural definition is unambiguous. Future entropy-collapse disclosures (against new vanity tooling, embedded-wallet SDKs, browser-extension PRNGs, deployer-tool custom signers) will map to T11.004 cleanly; the v0.1 anchor establishes the worked-example shape contributors should follow.
