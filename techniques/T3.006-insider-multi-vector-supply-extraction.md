# OAK-T3.006 — Insider Multi-Vector Supply Extraction

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T3 (Pump-and-Dump and Market Manipulation), OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (Ethereum, Solana via Pump.fun); cross-chain
**First documented:** 2026-05 (LABtrade — ZachXBT investigation)
**Aliases:** "multi-vector insider extraction", "coordinated insider supply dump", "insider multi-mechanism rug"

## Description

The most sophisticated form of insider extraction: the project team deploys multiple simultaneous extraction mechanisms — private OTC/loan deals, unilateral vesting changes, market maker coordination, CEX deposit dumping, buyback-wallet commingling — that individually appear as separate legitimate activities but collectively form a coordinated retail extraction strategy. No single vector is obviously malicious in isolation; the extraction is only visible when all vectors are cross-referenced.

The structural feature is **vector fragmentation as evasion**: by distributing extraction across four or more independent mechanisms, the team ensures that (a) no single on-chain pattern triggers a detector tuned to a single vector, (b) off-chain governance actions (vesting changes) are invisible to on-chain-only monitoring, and (c) the commingling of treasury, buyback, and insider wallets makes insider outflows indistinguishable from operational treasury management.

T3.006 is structurally distinct from sibling T3 sub-Techniques:

- **T3.003 (Pump-and-Dump Coordination)** — parent class; single-vector or dual-vector extraction (coordinated sell-off, bundler launch). T3.006's defining feature is the four-or-more-vector simultaneous extraction architecture.
- **T3.004 (Influencer-Amplified Promotion-and-Dump)** — distribution surface is the influencer's audience; T3.006's distribution surface is the project's own tokenomics opacity and governance processes.
- **T3.005 (Vesting Schedule Manipulation)** — covers the vesting-change vector in isolation. T3.006 covers the coordinated use of vesting changes *alongside* OTC, MM, and CEX vectors.
- **T5.005 (Treasury Management Exit)** — single-vector treasury drain. T3.006's treasury extraction is distributed across multiple vectors, making treasury outflows indistinguishable from operational flows.

## Observed indicators

- **Multi-vector extraction architecture.** At least four independent extraction vectors operating simultaneously: private OTC/loan deals, unilateral vesting changes, MM coordination, and CEX deposit dumping.
- **Borrower wallet = buyback wallet.** The address receiving OTC/loan proceeds is the same address executing public buybacks — treasury and insider flows are commingled by design.
- **Supply opacity.** No clear token distribution across data aggregators (CoinGecko, CoinMarketCap, RootData report different supply figures). The team controls >95% of supply.
- **Unilateral governance changes without community process.** Vesting terms changed (e.g., 3-month → 9-month cliff) without governance vote or community discussion. Discovered via leaked documents, not public disclosure.
- **CEX deposit dormancy + withdrawal + social amplification → dump.** Large CEX deposits sit dormant for weeks, then a portion is withdrawn with significant social media coverage, creating a bullish narrative while remaining deposits are dumped.
- **Cross-project insider pattern.** Founders' prior projects show the same extraction pattern — the repeat-offender signal.

## Detection signals

- **Cross-vector correlation (highest-leverage).** Cross-reference off-chain governance actions (vesting changes, loan contracts, corporate filings) with on-chain insider wallet clustering and CEX deposit timing. No single vector triggers individually; the correlation across vectors is the detection signal.
- **Borrower-buyback wallet identity check.** Flag addresses that both receive loan/OTC proceeds from the protocol treasury AND send market-buy transactions as public buybacks. Treasury and insider flow commingling is detectable on-chain.
- **CEX deposit dormancy + social-amplified withdrawal pattern.** Monitor for large CEX deposits from insider-linked addresses that sit dormant, followed by partial withdrawal with social media amplification, followed by dump of remaining deposits.
- **Supply-aggregator divergence.** Flag tokens where CoinGecko, CoinMarketCap, and RootData report materially different circulating supply, total supply, or FDV figures — the opacity is the signal.
- **Cross-project founder tracking.** Track founders across projects; flag founders whose prior project left investors with losses and who subsequently launch a new token with the same extraction architecture.

## Mitigations

- **Cross-vector monitoring (highest-leverage).** Monitor for correlation across: governance actions (vesting changes, loan contracts), on-chain insider wallet clustering, CEX deposit timing, and supply-aggregator divergence. No single vector triggers individually; the correlation is the detection signal.
- **Supply transparency enforcement.** Data aggregators should require token projects to publish a standardised token distribution schedule before listing; tokens with materially divergent supply figures across aggregators should be flagged.
- **Governance-change disclosure requirements.** Vesting schedule changes, token allocation changes, and insider loan/OTC deals should require advance public disclosure and community governance vote. Unilateral changes without community process should trigger elevated risk classification.
- **Borrower-buyback wallet separation.** Protocol treasuries should maintain separate wallets for OTC/loan proceeds, buyback execution, and operational treasury management. Commingling of these functions in a single address should be flagged as an elevated-risk pattern.
- **Cross-project founder registry.** Community-maintained registry of founders and their project history; founders whose prior projects resulted in investor losses should be flagged when launching new tokens.

**Canonical mitigations:** OAK-M04, OAK-M11, OAK-M25.

## Discussion

T3.006 captures the most sophisticated form of T3 insider extraction: the coordinated use of four or more independent extraction vectors that individually appear benign but collectively form a retail extraction architecture. The technique is structurally important because it defeats single-vector detectors — each vector on its own (OTC deal, vesting change, CEX deposit) is a legitimate operational activity. The extraction is only visible when all vectors are cross-referenced.

The LABtrade May 2026 case is the canonical T3.006 anchor because it demonstrates all four vectors simultaneously: (1) OTC/loan deals structured to benefit insiders regardless of token performance, (2) unilateral vesting changes without community process, (3) CEX deposit dormancy + withdrawal + social amplification → dump, and (4) borrower-buyback wallet commingling. The $6B FDV at peak makes this one of the largest T3 extraction events in the OAK reference period, and the ZachXBT investigation provides the cross-vector correlation that makes T3.006 classification possible.

The structural distinction from T3.004 is important. T3.004's extraction is influencer-driven — the promoter's audience is the demand-side amplifier. T3.006's extraction is tokenomics-and-governance-driven — the project's own structural opacity (supply opacity, vesting opacity, governance opacity) is the extraction architecture. The two can compose: a project with T3.006 multi-vector extraction architecture that also uses T3.004 influencer amplification is a compound T3 attack. But the extraction mechanisms are distinct and the detection surfaces are different.

The class is marked `emerging` rather than `observed` because only one field-confirmed incident (LABtrade May 2026) anchors the technique at v0.1. Additional multi-vector insider extraction incidents are expected as the T3 detection surface matures and cross-vector correlation monitoring becomes operational.
