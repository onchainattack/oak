# OAK-T6.006 — Counterfeit Token Impersonation

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T6 (Defense Evasion); chains downstream into OAK-T4 (Access Acquisition) for dust-attack lures and OAK-T5 (Value Extraction) for fake-LP-deposit drains
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (primary); cross-chain bridge variants (Polkadot ⇄ EVM canonical at v0.1)
**First documented:** dust-attack-lure / fake-LP cohort widespread from approximately 2022; canonical bridge-internal-mint sub-class 2026-04 (Hyperbridge)
**Aliases:** "fake-symbol-matching token", "bridge-token impersonation", "counterfeit-mint", "dust-attack lure", "fake LP-token"

## Description

Contracts that share the symbol (or other surface-identifier) of a legitimate token (USDC / USDT / WETH / WBTC / DOT) but are unrelated at the contract identity layer. Used in dust-attack lures, fake LP-token deposit drains, bridge-token impersonation on non-mainnet chains, and counterfeit-mint via compromised bridge admin authority.

T6.006 has **three structurally-distinct sub-classes**, ranked by severity and identity-layer-overlap with the legitimate token:

- **(a) Fake-symbol-matching deployment** — separate, unrelated contract sharing the symbol. Canonical for dust-attack lures (small-amount transfers of fake-token to a victim address with the goal of inducing the victim to investigate / interact) and fake LP-token deposit drains (a fake LP token of a popular pool is offered to vault / yield protocols that accept LP collateral by symbol). ScamSniffer 2023–2024 cohort coverage tracks the dust-attack lure variant at scale.
- **(b) Bridge-impersonation via fake-bridge-token deployment** — counterfeit deployment masquerading as a legitimate bridged token. The counterfeit is a separate contract from the legitimate bridge's token but is named / branded identically and is offered as the "bridged DOT" / "bridged BTC" / "bridged ETH" on a non-mainnet chain.
- **(c) Bridge-internal-mint** — the *legitimate* bridge contract mints counterfeit tokens via attacker-acquired admin authority, indistinguishable from legitimate at the contract identity layer. **Worst-case shape**; canonical Hyperbridge April 2026.

T6.006 is **structurally distinct** from adjacent Techniques:

- **T1.006 (Honeypot-by-Design)** — covers immutable-bytecode-at-deployment hostile-transfer-logic. T6.006's transfer-logic may be entirely benign; the attack is at the *identity* layer (symbol / branding) rather than the transfer-logic layer.
- **T6.005 (Proxy-Upgrade Malicious Switching)** — chains with T6.006 in the bridge-internal-mint sub-class where the upgrade-authority transfer is the mechanism by which the impersonation surface is opened.
- **T4.003 (Address Poisoning)** — adjacent attack class targeting transaction-history UX confusion. T6.006 targets symbol / branding confusion at the token identity layer.
- **T2.x rug-pull tokens** — token-side liquidity-establishment fraud where the *token itself* is the asset of fraud. T6.006's failure mode is impersonation of a *legitimate* asset, not the launch of a new fraudulent asset.

The notional-vs-realised loss gap is structurally informative across the cohort and should be recorded explicitly. Counterfeit mints scale infinitely on the supply side but are bounded on the cash-out side by destination-pool liquidity (in the bridge-internal-mint sub-class) or by victim-discovery latency (in the dust-attack-lure sub-class).

## Observed indicators

- **Sub-class (a) — fake-symbol-matching:** new token deployment with symbol matching a major asset (USDC / USDT / WETH / WBTC) but contract address divergent from the canonical deployment; small-amount inbound transfers to victim addresses; presence in fake LP-pool deployments offered to yield / vault protocols.
- **Sub-class (b) — fake-bridge-token:** branding consistent with a legitimate bridge (Wormhole / Hyperbridge / LayerZero / Synapse) but contract address not in the legitimate bridge's official deployment registry; offered for trading / bridging on chain-explorer surfaces but with no upstream reservation in the legitimate bridge's source-chain.
- **Sub-class (c) — bridge-internal-mint:** legitimate bridge contract emits mint events without corresponding deposit-correlation events on the source chain; admin-authority change events on the destination-chain token contract from a non-deployer / non-governance EOA. Chains with T6.005 — the upgrade itself is the precondition for the counterfeit-mint.
- **Pattern continuity:** T6.006 typically chains downstream into T5 outflow (fake-LP-token deposit drain; bridge-internal-mint cash-out via DEX into ETH / stablecoins) or T4 (dust-attack lure as prelude to wallet-drainer phishing).

## Detection signals

- **Pre-event (sub-classes a + b):** wallet / aggregator / venue-side token-list curation; refusal to honour symbol-only token resolution where contract-address verification against canonical registries fails; SAFE-list / known-good-token registry for asset-matching by contract address rather than symbol.
- **At-event (sub-class c):** real-time bridge monitoring for mint-without-deposit-correlation; bridge admin-authority-change events without governance correlation. Same detection-signal locus as T6.005's on-chain-forgery sub-class because the cases chain.
- **Cohort-level:** ScamSniffer / Etherscan / BscScan token-list curation; chain-analytics provider tagged-token registries; centralised-venue listing review.
- **Forensic:** post-event cluster-attribution of dust-attack-lure deployments to commodity drainer-kit infrastructure (Inferno / Angel / AngelFerno; MS Drainer; PhishLab) — the deployment-substrate is shared with broader T4 phishing-kit infrastructure.

## Real-world examples

- **Hyperbridge — Polkadot ⇄ Ethereum / Base / BNB / Arbitrum — 2026-04-13 (canonical bridge-internal-mint sub-class)** — Loss ~\$2.5M realised / ~\$1.2B notional. The counterfeit bridged-DOT was minted by *the legitimate bridge contract* via attacker-acquired admin authority, making it indistinguishable from legitimate bridged-DOT at the contract identity layer. Uniswap pools quoted the counterfeit identically to legitimate bridged-DOT — the impersonation succeeded at the protocol-identity layer, not just the symbol layer. Worst-case shape of T6.006, chained with T6.005's on-chain-forgery sub-class. See [`examples/2026-04-hyperbridge-merkle-proof-counterfeit-mint.md`](../examples/2026-04-hyperbridge-merkle-proof-counterfeit-mint.md).

## Mitigations

- **Wallet UX:** asset resolution by contract address rather than symbol; SAFE-list of known-good token contract addresses; explicit warning when a token symbol resolves to multiple contract deployments.
- **Aggregator / venue:** token-list curation against canonical registries; refusal to route through sub-class-(a) / sub-class-(b) deployments; centralised-venue listing-review for bridge-token claims.
- **Bridge designers:** per-message commitment binding for cryptographic proofs that authorise admin-authority changes; mint-cap-and-deposit-correlation enforcement at the destination-chain token contract; bridge-side telemetry for mint-without-deposit-correlation events.
- **Risk teams:** maintain per-asset inventories of (a) canonical contract addresses across chains, (b) legitimate bridge-token contract addresses, (c) admin-authority pathway for each bridge integration. Treat tokens whose symbol matches a major asset but whose contract address is not in the canonical registry as elevated risk.

## Discussion

T6.006 chains naturally with T6.005 in the bridge-internal-mint sub-class (sub-class c), where the upgrade-authority transfer is the mechanism by which the impersonation surface is opened. The Hyperbridge case is the canonical chained-Technique anchor for both — and is the worst-case shape because the counterfeit is minted by the legitimate bridge contract, making it indistinguishable from legitimate bridged-DOT at the contract identity layer. Uniswap pools quoting bridged-DOT priced the counterfeit identically; the impersonation succeeded at the protocol-identity layer, not just the symbol layer.

The three-sub-class split (fake-symbol-matching / fake-bridge-token / bridge-internal-mint) is the analytically distinctive feature of the Technique. Severity scales with identity-layer-overlap: sub-class (a) is detectable at the wallet / aggregator / venue token-list-curation layer; sub-class (b) is detectable at the centralised-venue listing-review layer; sub-class (c) is detectable only at the bridge-side telemetry layer because the contract identity *is* the legitimate one.

The notional-vs-realised loss gap is structurally informative. Counterfeit mints scale infinitely on the supply side; the operator can only convert as much to ETH / stablecoins as the destination pools can absorb without crashing the price. T6.006 contributors should record both notional and realised loss; the ratio reflects destination-pool-liquidity constraints (for sub-class c) or victim-discovery-latency constraints (for sub-classes a / b).

The sub-class (a) cohort is detectable at scale by commodity scanners (ScamSniffer, Etherscan token-list curation) — the failure mode is the wallet / aggregator UX layer rather than detection-signal availability. Cohort-level mitigation lives at the asset-resolution-by-contract-address-not-symbol invariant; flagged contracts are blocked at the token-list-curation layer.

The sub-class (c) cohort is the structurally novel surface at v0.1 and the dominant `emerging` framing for T6.006. The Hyperbridge case is the canonical 2026 anchor; pipeline targets for v0.x include any future cross-chain-proof-verification incident where the bridge mints counterfeit-but-protocol-identity-legitimate tokens. The detection surface is *bridge-side telemetry* (admin-authority-change events without governance correlation; mint events without deposit-correlation) rather than contract-layer rewrite.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once the worked-example anchor set expands to per-incident files for each sub-class. Currently sub-class (c) is anchored at Hyperbridge alone; sub-classes (a) and (b) are anchored only at the cohort level via ScamSniffer 2023–2024 coverage. Per-incident anchors for each sub-class are pipeline targets for v0.x.
