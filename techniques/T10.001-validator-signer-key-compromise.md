# OAK-T10.001 — Validator / Signer Key Compromise

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T10 (Bridge and Cross-Chain)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, cross-chain
**First documented:** 2022 (Ronin canonical case)
**Aliases:** "validator key theft", "multisig compromise", "MPC key loss"

## Description

A class of bridge incidents in which the attacker obtains the threshold of validator or signer keys required to authorise an asset movement on the bridge — without compromising the bridge contracts themselves. The attack surface is the **off-chain custody** of the keys that the bridge's on-chain authorisation function requires. From a defender's perspective, T10.001 is identifiable through two complementary signals: an *off-chain* signal (validator-operator security posture indicators, including phishing campaigns, supply-chain compromise, and operational-security failures) and an *on-chain* signal (authorisation events whose key-set composition diverges from the operator's published practice).

T10.001 is the canonical entry vector for OAK-G01 (Lazarus Group) bridge targeting through 2022–2024. Both the Ronin and Harmony Horizon incidents fit this Technique cleanly; the Multichain July 2023 incident is also a T10.001 case but with a different proximate cause (CEO arrest leading to MPC-key loss rather than active phishing-led compromise) and is included to demonstrate the breadth of the underlying class.

## Observed indicators

- Off-chain reporting of phishing campaigns, fake-job-offer payloads, or supply-chain compromise targeting validator-operator personnel at known bridge organisations.
- On-chain authorisation events for large asset movements whose signing-key composition is unusual (e.g., a 2-of-5 multisig where the same two specific keys sign).
- Discrepancy between the bridge operator's published key-management practice (geographic distribution, hardware-security-module use, multi-party-computation thresholds) and the observable signing pattern.

## Detection signals

- Real-time per-bridge: monitor the threshold-signing event for any bridge against the bridge operator's published key-rotation cadence and geographic distribution; alert on threshold-signing events whose key composition has not appeared in normal operations recently.
- Off-chain telemetry coordination: integrate phishing-campaign and supply-chain compromise reporting from CTI feeds; flag bridges whose validator-operator personnel appear in such reporting.
- Pre-incident: inventory bridges whose validator-set is small (e.g., 5/9 or 2/5 thresholds) versus large; treat low-threshold bridges as elevated risk regardless of operator reputation.

## Real-world examples

- **Ronin Bridge — Ethereum ↔ Ronin sidechain — 2022-03-23** — Loss \~\$625M (\~173,600 ETH and \~25.5M USDC). Five of nine Ronin validator keys were compromised via social-engineering: a Sky Mavis employee was targeted via a LinkedIn-delivered fake-job-offer payload (PDF containing malware) which gave the attackers infrastructure access leading to validator key theft. The hack remained undetected for six days. FBI/Treasury attribution to Lazarus Group / OAK-G01 published April 2022. See worked example at `examples/2022-03-ronin-bridge.md`.
- **Harmony Horizon Bridge — Ethereum ↔ Harmony — 2022-06-23/24** — Loss \~\$100M. The bridge used a 2-of-5 multisig; attackers compromised two of the five signing addresses. FBI confirmed Lazarus Group / OAK-G01 attribution. \~\$96M of the proceeds was subsequently laundered through Tornado Cash. Sources: `[ellipticronin2022]` (companion analysis), [Halborn — Explained: The Harmony Horizon Bridge Hack](https://www.halborn.com/blog/post/explained-the-harmony-horizon-bridge-hack).
- **Multichain — Multi-chain MPC bridge — 2023-07-06** — Loss \~\$126M. Multichain's MPC ("multi-party computation") key shares were lost following the arrest of CEO Zhaojun by Chinese police; hardware containing key shares was confiscated. Industry analyses are split on whether this is a T10.001 *external* compromise or an *insider* / wind-down event; Multichain ceased operations within a week. The case is included as a canonical example of T10.001 broadly construed: regardless of the attribution, the bridge's authorisation function ceased to be controlled by its publicly-claimed key-management posture. Sources: [Halborn — Explained: The Multichain Hack (July 2023)](https://www.halborn.com/blog/post/explained-the-multichain-hack-july-2023), [Chainalysis — Multichain Exploit: Possible hack or rug pull](https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/multichain-exploit-july-2023/).
- **Shibarium PoS-bridge — Ethereum ↔ Shibarium — 2025-09-12** — Loss \~\$2.3M-\$4.1M across 17 token positions; 4.6M BONE *frozen* by the team's emergency-pause path (partial-recovery channel via owner-side staking-contract authority). Combined-vector T10.001 + T16.001 sub-shape: attacker controlled or compromised signing authority for some subset of 12 validators (AWS-KMS-stored signing keys; team enumerated four candidate intrusion vectors — developer-machine compromise, AWS-KMS compromise, npm-supply-chain compromise, insider — without confirming any single one), then flash-borrowed 4.6M BONE in the same execution context as the malicious checkpoint to amplify staked-BONE-derived voting weight past the 2/3 super-majority threshold. The case is the cleanest 2025 anchor for T10.001 + T16.001 composing in the PoS-bridge setting and demonstrates that the Beanstalk April-2022 lesson (snapshot-based stake at proposal-creation time) did not propagate from the governance-contract sub-domain into bridge-validator-set-eligibility designs even 41 months later. Pseudonymous-unattributed; PeckShield, Hexens, Seal 911 forensic engagement. See [`examples/2025-09-shibarium-bridge.md`](../examples/2025-09-shibarium-bridge.md).

## Mitigations

- **Bridge operators:** raise validator thresholds and broaden geographic / organisational distribution; require hardware-security-module enforcement; rotate keys on a published cadence; integrate off-chain phishing-detection telemetry (especially for engineering personnel routinely contacted via LinkedIn / messaging platforms).
- **Risk teams:** maintain per-bridge validator-set inventories; treat low-threshold (≤3-of-N for small N) bridges as elevated risk regardless of operator reputation.
- **Off-chain (operator personnel):** anti-phishing training specifically aimed at fake-job-offer LinkedIn vectors; out-of-band verification for any payload received through external recruiting channels.

## Discussion

T10.001 is the single-most-impactful Technique in modern bridge-attack literature by aggregate dollar value: Ronin alone accounts for more than \$600M, Harmony adds another \$100M, and Multichain adds another \$126M. The defensive lessons converge on **off-chain operational security for validator-operator personnel** more than on **on-chain detection** — the on-chain manifestation is typically a single transaction that is final by the time it lands. The Bybit case (`examples/2025-02-bybit.md`) is structurally similar (third-party-vendor supply-chain compromise of signing infrastructure) but is not a bridge incident; OAK catalogues it under OAK-G01 attribution with cross-references rather than as a T10.001 case.
