# OAK-T16.002 — Hostile-Vote Treasury Drain

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T16 (Governance / Voting Manipulation)
**Adjacent Techniques:** OAK-T9.003 (generic Governance Attack class — preserved on every T16.002 worked example), OAK-T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation — the upstream value-inflation step that produces the voting-position the attacker then uses), OAK-T5.005 (Treasury-Management Exit — the on-chain framing of the resulting treasury egress)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM, Solana, cross-chain (any DAO with on-chain governance over a treasury whose voting-token can be acquired or inflated by the attacker)
**First documented:** 2022 (Mango Markets October 2022 as the canonical anchor)
**Aliases:** "DAO settlement vote", "treasury self-pay vote", "post-exploit governance settlement", "hostile DAO vote", "negotiated extraction vote"

## Description

Acquisition (or upstream inflation) of governance-token voting power through legitimate market means — direct on-chain purchase, exchange-routed accumulation, oracle-inflation of an existing position into voting eligibility — followed by submission and passage of a governance proposal whose payload directs the DAO treasury into the attacker's pocket as a "settlement," "bug bounty," "compensation," or routine treasury-management action that the attacker themselves benefits from. The defining shape is **acquire / inflate voting power → submit treasury-affecting proposal → vote the proposal through using the acquired voting weight → treasury egress to attacker-controlled addresses**, with the entire flow happening at the on-chain governance layer rather than as a single-transaction flash-loan event.

T16.002 is the canonical Mango Markets second-step pattern. The October 2022 case is the textbook chain: Avraham Eisenberg used T9.001 (oracle manipulation) to inflate an MNGO position into a notional-collateral position large enough to borrow ~\$110M from Mango lending pools (the realised T9.001 extraction), and then — *separately* — used the inflated MNGO holdings to vote a "settlement" proposal through Mango DAO that allowed Eisenberg to retain ~\$47M of the borrowed funds in exchange for "returning" ~\$67M (which had originally come from the same lending pools) and a non-pursuit commitment from the DAO. The second step — the settlement vote — is T16.002. The first step — the oracle-inflation that produced the voting position — is T9.001. The two are causally chained but operationally distinct: T9.001 is mitigated at the oracle-design layer (TWAP, multi-venue, deviation breakers); T16.002 is mitigated at the governance-process layer (cohort-surveillance of proposing actors, conflict-of-interest disclosure, counter-proposal capability when the voting cohort and the beneficiary cohort overlap).

T16.002 is **structurally distinct from T16.001 (Vote Takeover via Flash-Loan)**. T16.001 attackers acquire voting power inside a single flash-loan-bracketed block and never hold the position outside that window; T16.002 attackers hold the voting position across the entire proposal lifecycle (submission, voting period, timelock, execution) and remain economically exposed to the underlying token throughout. The mitigation surface differs: snapshot-based voting power (T16.001 mitigation) does *not* close T16.002 because the attacker holds the position at every snapshot. Closing T16.002 requires either upstream mitigation (preventing the value-inflation that produced the voting position — T9.001 oracle hardening) or governance-process mitigation (cohort-level surveillance, mandatory conflict-of-interest disclosure on proposals whose beneficiary overlaps the proposing cohort, counter-proposal capability with sufficient time before timelock-execution).

T16.002 is **structurally distinct from T16.003 (Delegation-Cluster Vote Takeover)** in *holder topology*. T16.003 attackers acquire voting power via delegation pull from a multi-wallet cluster — voting power is concentrated through delegation graph mechanics; the underlying token holdings may remain distributed across the cluster. T16.002 attackers hold the voting weight directly (or have it inflated via T9.001 against a directly-held position). Both can co-occur (the Compound Proposal 289 cohort had both direct holdings and delegation-pulled weight); contributors should map both T16.002 and T16.003 when both mechanisms are load-bearing.

T16.002 covers three structurally adjacent sub-shapes:

- **Post-T9.001 settlement vote.** Attacker uses oracle-inflation to extract from a lending or perpetual-futures protocol, then uses the inflated governance-token holdings to vote a settlement / non-pursuit / compensation proposal that allows them to retain the extraction. Canonical anchor: Mango Markets October 2022 (Eisenberg / MNGO settlement).
- **Direct market accumulation against a thin governance token.** Attacker accumulates voting weight against a governance token whose circulating-supply-vs-quorum-threshold ratio is favourable (the protocol's quorum requires only a small fraction of circulating supply); attacker submits a proposal with a treasury-affecting payload that benefits them. Distinct from flash-loan-funded variant by the multi-week / multi-block accumulation window. The Compound Proposal 289 cohort overlaps this sub-shape (`examples/2024-07-compound-vote-takeover.md`) although the dominant load-bearing mechanism for that case was delegation-pull (T16.003).
- **Self-dealing routine treasury operation.** Proposal text frames the action as a routine treasury-management operation (yield-deployment, vault-rebalance, partner-incentive program) that on inspection turns out to direct treasury funds into a vehicle controlled by the proposing cohort. Cohort context: smaller-protocol DAOs with concentrated holdings see this pattern recurringly through 2023-2026; the public-record anchors at the Compound Proposal 289 case (which was prevented in the timelock review window) and at smaller-DAO cases that are typically not published forensically.

## Observed indicators

- **Pre-event (governance-design layer):** governance-token circulating-supply-vs-quorum-threshold ratio favourable to a single-actor or small-cluster acquisition; proposing-actor eligibility threshold low or absent; conflict-of-interest disclosure not enforced at proposal-submission time; counter-proposal mechanism either absent or operationally unrehearsed.
- **At-event (proposing-actor signal):** proposing actor (or proposing-actor's wallet cluster) is the explicit beneficiary of the proposal payload — treasury direction lands in an address controlled by the proposing cohort, or in a vehicle (vault, multisig, "yield" contract) effectively controlled by the cohort.
- **At-event (vote-distribution signal):** voting-weight share at proposal-passage concentrated in a small set of addresses with on-chain coordination signal (T8.001 cluster-reuse signature on the voting wallets); voted weight in favour of the proposal correlates with the proposing cohort's accumulated holdings; counter-vote weight concentrated in the broader holder base.
- **At-event (proposal-text-vs-payload divergence):** proposal text frames the action in routine-treasury-management language but the on-chain calldata directs treasury to the proposing cohort. The Compound 289 case is canonical here: "yield-deployment to goldCOMP vault" framing concealed self-dealing transfer.
- **On-chain:** treasury egress event from the DAO contract to attacker-controlled addresses or attacker-controlled vehicle; egress sized at the proposal's payload amount; timing aligned with the post-vote timelock-execution window.
- **Forensic record:** the proposal record (text, payload, voting outcome); the proposing cohort's on-chain accumulation pattern (typically visible in DAO-analytics dashboards — Tally, Boardroom, Boardroom analytics); the upstream value-inflation event when present (T9.001 oracle-inflation precondition).

## Detection signals

- **Cohort-level surveillance of governance-token accumulation patterns:** monitor governance-token holdings across publicly-known coordinating cohorts (Humpy / Golden Boys, similar pattern-of-conduct actors) over multi-week windows; alert on accumulation patterns that approach quorum-relevant threshold.
- **Proposal-payload-vs-text divergence detection:** static analysis of proposal calldata at submission time, with explicit check that the proposal beneficiary does not overlap the proposing cohort. Boardroom / Tally / DAO-analytics dashboards surface this signal at the governance-process layer.
- **Conflict-of-interest disclosure enforcement:** governance-process-layer requirement that proposers disclose any beneficiary relationship; failure to disclose surfaces as a defender-actionable signal in the pre-execution review window.
- **Per-proposal simulation against treasury egress paths:** replay-style simulation of any proposal whose execution payload touches treasury; particular attention to proposals that direct funds to addresses or vehicles whose on-chain history overlaps the proposing cohort.
- **Counter-proposal operational capability:** rehearsed mechanism for deploying a counter-proposal during the post-vote timelock review window; the Compound 2024 case is the canonical example of this capability working as designed.
- **Upstream T9.001 monitoring:** for protocols where the governance token's voting weight is computed against a position whose value depends on an oracle, the T9.001 oracle-deviation signal is a leading indicator for downstream T16.002 risk.

## Real-world examples

- **Mango Markets — Solana — 2022-10-11 / 2022-10-15 to 2022-10-22** — Loss \~\$110M extracted via T9.001 oracle manipulation; \~\$47M retained post-T16.002 settlement vote. Avraham Eisenberg used MNGO holdings (acquired during and after the oracle-manipulation event) to vote a "settlement" proposal through Mango DAO that allowed retention of \~\$47M in exchange for "returning" \~\$67M of the borrowed funds and a non-pursuit commitment. The case is the canonical T16.002 anchor and the canonical T9.001 + T16.002 chain on the public record. Eisenberg subsequently faced CFTC civil enforcement (filed January 9, 2023) and S.D.N.Y. criminal trial (April 2024 jury convictions, vacated by Judge Subramanian May 23, 2025 on improper-venue and insufficient-materiality grounds — the market-manipulation factual record stands; the criminal *legal* record was vacated). See [`examples/2022-10-mango-markets.md`](../examples/2022-10-mango-markets.md).
- **Compound Proposal 289 — Ethereum — 2024-07-28 (overlap with T16.003)** — Attempted but prevented. The Golden Boys cohort accumulated COMP voting power across multiple addresses through direct on-chain purchases and delegation pull, then submitted Proposal 289 directing \~499,000 COMP (\~\$24M) from the Compound DAO treasury to a "goldCOMP" yield-bearing vault effectively controlled by the cohort. The proposal *passed the on-chain vote* but was prevented in the pre-execution timelock window via community recognition of the self-dealing pattern and counter-proposal deployment. The case overlaps T16.002 (direct accumulation against a thin-quorum token) and T16.003 (delegation-cluster takeover); both Techniques map. See [`examples/2024-07-compound-vote-takeover.md`](../examples/2024-07-compound-vote-takeover.md).
- **Cohort context** — Humpy's pattern-of-conduct at Balancer and SushiSwap before the Compound 289 attempt is a multi-protocol T16.002 cohort signal that the canonical worked example documents in its Discussion section. Smaller-DAO T16.002 cases through 2023-2026 are documented at the governance-forum / DAO-analytics-dashboard layer but rarely surface at OAK's worked-example threshold because the realised-loss size is typically below the v0.1 corpus inclusion bar.

## Mitigations

- **Protocols (OAK-M17):** time-locked governance + multi-block quorum is a baseline requirement but does NOT close T16.002 by itself — the attacker holds the voting position across all snapshots. The timelock window is load-bearing as a *review window* paired with operational counter-proposal capability (the Compound 2024 lesson).
- **Protocols — conflict-of-interest disclosure:** governance-process-layer requirement that proposers disclose any beneficiary relationship; not contract-enforced at v0.1 across most DAOs but a discoverable defender practice that surfaces self-dealing proposals in the pre-execution review window.
- **Protocols — counter-proposal operational capability:** rehearsed mechanism for deploying a counter-proposal during the timelock review window. The Compound 2024 case validates this primitive: a passed-vote governance attack on a top-tier protocol was prevented in the timelock window through community action.
- **Protocols (OAK-M02):** proposal-payload static analysis with explicit check that the proposal beneficiary does not overlap the proposing cohort.
- **Upstream — T9.001 oracle hardening:** for protocols where the governance token's voting weight depends on an oracle-priced position, T9.001 mitigation (TWAP, multi-venue inputs, deviation circuit-breakers) is the upstream T16.002 mitigation. The Mango Markets lesson restated.
- **Cohort-level surveillance:** track publicly-identified governance-coordination cohorts (Humpy / Golden Boys and similar pattern-of-conduct actors) across protocols; treat their accumulation patterns at any new protocol as elevated-risk for T16.002 / T16.003. T8.001 cluster-attribution applied to delegation flows is the operational shape.
- **Risk teams:** for governance-token holdings, monitor proposal queues for any proposal whose execution payload affects treasury and whose beneficiary overlaps the proposing cohort; maintain per-protocol governance-design + governance-process inventories.

## Discussion

T16.002's structural lesson is that **flash-loan-resistance at the voting-power-eligibility layer is necessary but not sufficient**. Snapshot-based voting power closes T16.001 entirely but leaves T16.002 fully open: the attacker holds the position throughout, so every snapshot reflects the attacker's holdings, and the snapshot mechanism itself becomes the attacker's voting-weight evidence. Closing T16.002 requires upstream mitigation (preventing the value-inflation that produced the voting position) or governance-process mitigation (cohort-surveillance, conflict-of-interest disclosure, counter-proposal capability). The Mango Markets case shows that even with a "legitimate" voting position (held throughout the lifecycle, no flash-loan), the proposal can be a hostile use of governance against the protocol's own treasury — and that the mitigation has to live at the layer above contract enforcement, in the governance-process layer that decides what proposals are eligible to be voted on.

The cohort is structurally distinct from T16.001 by atomicity-window (T16.001 = single block; T16.002 = full proposal lifecycle), and from T16.003 by acquisition-mechanism (T16.002 = direct holdings or T9.001-inflated; T16.003 = delegation-pull). Both T16.002 and T16.003 frequently co-occur (Compound Proposal 289 cohort had both direct holdings and delegation-pulled weight); contributors should map both when both mechanisms are load-bearing.

Attribution-strength notes: Mango Markets is `confirmed` (Eisenberg publicly self-identified, CFTC litigated, criminal record vacated May 2025 but factual record stands); Compound 289 is `pseudonymous` with `publicly-named cohort` attribution status (Golden Boys / Humpy publicly identified at the cohort layer, individual members pseudonymous-to-identified at varying levels). The publicly-named-cohort attribution status is structurally important for T16.002 / T16.003 cohort-surveillance: cohorts whose pattern-of-conduct is documented across multiple protocols (Humpy at Balancer → SushiSwap → Compound 289) are detectable via T8.001 cluster-attribution and constitute a discoverable defender-actionable signal at the governance-process layer.

False-positive considerations: legitimate DAO settlement votes and legitimate treasury-management proposals are routine. The detection signal is not "the proposal is treasury-affecting" but specifically "the proposal beneficiary overlaps the proposing cohort and the proposal text frames the action in routine-treasury-management language without disclosing the conflict-of-interest." Cohort-level analysis confirms that the legitimate use-case for "proposer self-deals treasury without disclosure" essentially does not exist outside attacker behaviour.

v0.x deferred items: a structured rubric for distinguishing "hostile-vote treasury drain" from "legitimate-but-contested governance settlement" (the Mango Markets settlement was framed by Eisenberg as a "negotiated bug bounty" and by the DAO as a "compulsory settlement under economic duress"; the framing is community-dependent and OAK does not opine on the legal-vs-OAK status); cohort-level metrics on the publicly-known-coordination-cluster overlap rate across DAO governance forums as a forward-looking T16.002 / T16.003 surface estimate; treatment of cross-DAO operator continuity (a single cohort accumulating governance positions across multiple DAOs concurrently) as a distinct sub-shape.
