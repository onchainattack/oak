# OAK-T13.001 — Paymaster Compromise

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T13 (Account Abstraction Attacks)
**Maturity:** observed
**Chains:** EVM (ERC-4337 EntryPoint v0.6 / v0.7 / v0.8 / v0.9 deployments); EVM L2s with native account abstraction (zkSync Era, Starknet) where the paymaster role is analogous; conceptually portable to any chain whose execution model separates gas-sponsorship validation from execution
**First documented:** 2023 (Alchemy / OpenZeppelin disclosure of the UserOperation packing inconsistency in EntryPoint v0.6 affecting `VerifyingPaymaster`); class characterised continuously through 2024–2026 in audit-firm advisories on deployed paymasters
**Aliases:** "paymaster drain", "sponsorship policy bypass", "postOp griefing", "paymaster DoS", "validatePaymasterUserOp bypass", "gasless-transaction abuse"

## Description

Umbrella entry for the paymaster-compromise class under ERC-4337 account abstraction. The paymaster is the on-chain component that sponsors gas for user operations (UserOps) — its compromise surface concentrates several distinct failure modes that were grouped under a single Technique in pre-v0.1 drafts but are split into four sub-Techniques at v0.1 because their mitigation surfaces are structurally distinct.

T13.001 sub-Techniques:

- **OAK-T13.001.001 — Paymaster Accounting Drain.** The paymaster releases sponsored funds in excess of what its accounting model accounts for — typically because validation-time payment is not unwound when `postOp` reverts, or because `postOp` revert paths are not enumerated against the gas-token mechanic (fee-on-transfer, rebasing, pausable / blacklist).
- **OAK-T13.001.002 — Paymaster Policy Bypass.** UserOps bypass the paymaster's sponsorship rules — per-user quotas, per-application allowlists, per-method gating, per-token spending limits — typically via off-chain-signer / on-chain-hash parity violations (Alchemy 2023 UserOperation-packing disclosure) or fail-open instruction parsers in the policy engine (Kora / Token-2022 advisory).
- **OAK-T13.001.003 — Paymaster Reentrancy.** A paymaster-specific instance of the T9.005 class restricted to the `validatePaymasterUserOp` / `postOp` call surface — the paymaster makes an external call before sponsorship-accounting state is finalised, and is re-entered via a token hook or peer-protocol callback.
- **OAK-T13.001.004 — Paymaster Griefing.** UserOps that drain the paymaster's deposited balance without producing useful protocol activity — gas-limit abuse against unused-gas-penalty mechanics, temporary-revert conditions in target contracts, bundle-level griefing against bundler-reputation budgets.

T13.001 (the umbrella) is retained as a Technique ID because the four sub-Techniques share a single contract surface (the paymaster), a single specification anchor (ERC-4337 `[erc4337spec]`), and a substantially overlapping mitigation literature (`[ozaa4337audit]`, `[osecpaymasters2025]`, `[tobsixmistakes2026]`, `[aviggiano4337checklist]`). Defenders evaluating a deployed paymaster should treat all four sub-Techniques as a single review surface.

## Architectural distinction from T9

T13.001 is **architecturally distinct** from the smart-contract-exploit Tactic (T9):

- vs. **T9.004 (Access-Control Misconfiguration):** the failure is not a missing or mis-scoped role; the paymaster is exercising exactly the role it was deployed to exercise (sponsoring UserOps), but its sponsorship decision was reached against incomplete or bypassable validation.
- vs. **T9.005 (Reentrancy):** sub-Technique T13.001.003 is a strict subset of T9.005 in mechanism, but the call surface (`validatePaymasterUserOp` / `postOp`) and the storage surface (paymaster sponsorship accounting, deposited-balance ledger at the EntryPoint) are specific to the ERC-4337 contract layout.
- vs. **T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation):** the oracle case is a price-feed corruption; T13.001 is a sponsorship-decision corruption against an otherwise-correct economic input.

## Discussion

T13.001 was a single Technique through pre-v0.1 drafts; v0.1 splits it into four sub-Techniques because the mitigation surfaces are structurally distinct: accounting-drain (T13.001.001) requires `postOp` revert-path enumeration and gas-token-mechanic auditing; policy-bypass (T13.001.002) requires hash-parity verification and deny-by-default policy-engine semantics; reentrancy (T13.001.003) requires guards on the validation / `postOp` surface as a state-sharing set; griefing (T13.001.004) requires EntryPoint-version pinning and bundler-reputation monitoring. Treating them as one Technique loses the per-sub-case audit checklist; treating them as fully independent Techniques duplicates the shared paymaster-surface context. The umbrella-with-sub-Techniques structure preserves both.

Contributors writing future paymaster-incident worked examples should map them to the most specific sub-Technique; cases that exercise multiple sub-Techniques in a single chain should list each.
