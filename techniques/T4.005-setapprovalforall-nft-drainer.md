# OAK-T4.005 — `setApprovalForAll` NFT Drainer

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T4 (Access Acquisition)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM (primary; Ethereum / Polygon / BNB Chain)
**First documented:** 2021 (early Bored Ape phishing wave); industrial-scale 2022+
**Aliases:** "NFT phishing drainer", "approval-for-all phishing", "BAYC phishing"

## Description

A class of phishing flows in which a victim is induced to submit an on-chain `setApprovalForAll(operator, true)` transaction granting an attacker-controlled operator address total transfer authority over one of the victim's ERC-721 (or ERC-1155) NFT collections. Once the operator authority is granted, the attacker can transfer any NFT in the approved collection from the victim's wallet via `transferFrom` / `safeTransferFrom` (ERC-721) or the ERC-1155 batch equivalent. From a defender's perspective, T4.005 is the NFT-collection-level analogue of T4.004's per-token approve flow — the failure mode is structurally the same (victim signs an authority grant they did not understand), but the granularity is the entire collection rather than a single token's allowance.

T4.005's entry vector is most often a compromised Discord server (with a fake "extra mint" or "claim airdrop" announcement) or a phishing dApp linked from social media. The victim's wallet-UX interpretation of `setApprovalForAll` calls is the cornerstone of the defender's signal: most legitimate NFT marketplace flows require this approval, so wallet warnings need to distinguish legitimate marketplace operators from attacker-controlled ones.

## Observed indicators

- ERC-721 / ERC-1155 `ApprovalForAll(owner, operator, approved=true)` event (event topic `0x17307eab39ab6107e8899845ad3d59bd9653f200f220920489ca2b5937696c31`) naming an attacker-clustered operator address shortly after victim engagement with a phishing context (compromised Discord channel, fake dApp).
- ABI signature `setApprovalForAll(address,bool)` (selector `0xa22cb465`) sent to an ERC-721 / ERC-1155 contract with operator pointing at a freshly deployed contract or an EOA with no other inbound legitimate flow.
- Operator addresses concentrating outsized aggregate authority across many distinct collections and many distinct victim wallets.
- Attacker-side `transferFrom` (ERC-721) or `safeBatchTransferFrom` (ERC-1155) calls executed shortly after the corresponding `ApprovalForAll` event, often draining multiple NFTs from a single victim in rapid succession (single-block sweep typical for high-value collections).
- Operator address NOT in canonical marketplace operator allowlist (OpenSea Seaport `0x00000000000000ADc04C56Bf30aC9d3c0aAF14dC`, Blur `0x000000000000Ad05Ccc4F10045630fb830B95127`, LooksRare, Magic Eden, Foundation, Rarible).

## Detection signals

- Real-time on-chain: `ApprovalForAll` event watcher; alert when operator clusters with previously-flagged drainer infrastructure or is non-allowlisted-and-newly-deployed.
- Per-wallet (OAK-M08 / OAK-M31): surface the user's outstanding `ApprovalForAll` grants at sign time and at periodic review intervals; warn on grants to non-allowlisted operators.
- Off-chain telemetry: integrate compromised-Discord-server reporting (community reports, Discord moderation tooling) as a pre-event indicator for in-progress NFT phishing campaigns.
- Per-operator NFT-velocity outlier detection: an operator producing many `transferFrom` calls within a short window across distinct victim wallets is a cohort-attribution signal.
- Mempool watcher for known drainer-operator target addresses (OAK-M06) provides intervention window between `ApprovalForAll` and the operator's first `transferFrom`.

## Real-world examples

- **Lawliet Bored Ape phishing — Ethereum — 2022-01-31** — Loss \~\$2.7M across 13 NFTs (7 BAYC, 5 MAYC, 1 Doodle). The attacker took control of the Discord server of NFT collection Moschi Mochi to post a fake announcement about an extra mint (1,000 NFTs for a chance to win a \$25,000 raffle). Lawliet engaged with the fake mint, sent 0.49 ETH for 14 scam NFTs, then signed a sequence of `setApprovalForAll` calls to attacker-controlled operators; the operator subsequently exercised these via `transferFrom` for each BAYC and derivative. Source: `[theblock2022boredape]`.
- **toddkramer.eth Bored Ape phishing — Ethereum — 2021-12** — Loss \~593 ETH (\~\$2.2M at the time) across 16 NFTs via an analogous `setApprovalForAll` flow; one of the earliest large-loss precedents and a structural template for the 2022 wave.
- **BAYC Discord webhook compromise — Ethereum — 2022-04-01** — Per `[baycdiscord2022]`, the BAYC Discord server's webhook integrations were briefly compromised; phishing announcements directed users to fake-mint dApps requesting `setApprovalForAll`. Yuga Labs publicly advised the community to avoid Discord-originated minting flows during the window.
- **BAYC community-manager Discord compromise — Ethereum — 2022-06-04** — Per `[fortunebaycjune2022]`, approximately \$360K in NFTs were drained via a Discord compromise of a BAYC community manager account; the attacker posted phishing announcements that drove users into `setApprovalForAll` flows.
- **Yuga Otherside Discord compromise — Ethereum — 2022-08** — Per `[yugaotherside2022]`, `[peckshieldyugaotherside2022]`, `[zachxbtyugaotherside2022]`, `[slowmistyugaotherside2022]`, `[theblockyugaotherside2022]`, the Otherside Discord was compromised; phishing announcements drove users into `setApprovalForAll` grants to attacker operators across BAYC, MAYC, and Otherdeed collections.
- **Discord-server-compromise vector — multi-incident — 2022 onward** — TRM Labs and others have documented a series of connected NFT Discord-server compromises distributing T4.005-class phishing dApps; Discord-compromise is the dominant entry path for high-value NFT collection phishing.

## Reference implementations

- `mg-detectors-rs` — coverage **gap** at v0.1 (wallet-layer phishing detection out of MG scope).
- Vendor coverage: wallet vendors with `setApprovalForAll` simulation and operator-allowlisting; NFT-marketplace-side integrations that label legitimate marketplace operator addresses (OpenSea registered operator filter `[openseaoperatorfilter2022]` is a precedent for marketplace-side enforcement); revoke.cash for outstanding `ApprovalForAll` revocation.

## Mitigations

- **OAK-M08 (Per-Spender Approval Audit and Revocation):** revoke unused `ApprovalForAll` grants on a recurring cadence using revoke.cash or comparable tools; never grant operator authority from an interaction initiated via a Discord announcement without out-of-band verification of the dApp URL.
- **OAK-M31 (Signing Risk Heuristics) — extended for NFT operator grants:** highest-leverage layer — maintain a marketplace-operator allowlist (OpenSea Seaport, Blur, LooksRare, Magic Eden, Foundation, Rarible); warn on `setApprovalForAll` to non-allowlisted operators; surface outstanding `ApprovalForAll` grants prominently and provide a one-click revoke flow.
- **OAK-M30 (Per-dApp Domain Allowlist):** block `setApprovalForAll` signing prompts originating from non-allowlisted domains, particularly social-media-shared "extra mint" dApps.
- **OAK-M19 (Air-Gap Cold Wallet Signing):** for high-value NFT vaults, route operator-grant signing through air-gapped device with explicit display of the operator address.
- **NFT communities:** Discord moderation policies that reduce the impact of compromised-server announcements (channel-lock procedures, mod 2FA enforcement, rate-limited announcement permissions, removal of webhook integrations from announcement channels); cross-channel verification for announcements about extra mints or airdrops.

## Discussion

T4.005 sits at the intersection of OAK's main scope and the broader NFT-specific pillar called out in `TAXONOMY-GAPS.md`. It is included in T4 at v0.1 because the failure mode is structurally identical to T4.004 (allowance grant exercised via subsequent transfer call) and because the attacker-side detection signals (operator-address clustering) parallel the spender-address clustering for T4.004. Other NFT-specific Techniques (wash-trade NFT volume inflation, fake-mint scams, royalty bypass) are deliberately deferred to a future v0.x update.

False-positive reasoning: legitimate marketplace listing flows require `setApprovalForAll` to the marketplace operator contract (OpenSea Seaport, Blur, LooksRare). The high-precision signal is operator non-membership in the canonical marketplace allowlist combined with originating-domain provenance. Edge case: a marketplace deploying a new operator contract version (e.g., OpenSea Seaport 1.5 → 1.6 upgrade) produces a transient surge of `ApprovalForAll` to a not-yet-allowlisted address that is functionally legitimate; allowlist updates need a defined SLA from marketplaces to wallet vendors.

The Discord-compromise entry vector is one of the most actionable defender lessons from the T4.005 cohort: defender controls live both at the wallet layer (allowlisting marketplace operators, surfacing outstanding `ApprovalForAll` grants) and at the community-moderation layer (Discord 2FA, channel-lock procedures, webhook-integration hygiene). Contributors writing future T4.005 examples should preserve the on-chain-and-off-chain bifurcation explicitly.

v0.x deferred items: cross-chain `ApprovalForAll` observability for collections deployed on multiple EVM chains (Polygon-based NFTs particularly); ERC-6551 token-bound-account semantics where the NFT itself is an authority over a wallet (T4.005 grant on a TBA-bearing collection compounds risk in ways not yet covered).
