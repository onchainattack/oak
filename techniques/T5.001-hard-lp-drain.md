# OAK-T5.001 — Hard LP Drain

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T5 (Value Extraction)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana
**First documented:** 2020 (industry reports)
**Aliases:** "hard rug", "LP withdrawal event"

## Description

A single-event removal of a substantial fraction of a pool's quote-side liquidity by the LP-controlling party, after which the token's tradable depth collapses and remaining holders cannot exit at the prior market price. From a defender's perspective, T5.001 is the textbook "exit event" — fully observable on-chain, occurring in a single (or near-single) transaction, and trivially attributable in retrospect. T5.001 is the canonical extraction event chained downstream of OAK-T1 (transfer-restriction or mint-authority setup), OAK-T2 (single-sided or locked-liquidity spoof), and OAK-T3 (holder-capture pump). Its detection-at-the-moment is straightforward; the defensive value lies in **pre-event** classification via T1/T2/T3 signals so that holders never enter exposed positions.

T5.001 also covers the NFT analogue: a mint-and-abandon pattern where the operator removes the project's runway capital (mint proceeds wallet) in a single sweep after primary-mint sell-out, leaving holders with no road-map delivery. NFT cases are scoped under T5.001 because the failure mode (one-event extraction by deployer cluster) and detection signal (deployer-cluster outflow) match the fungible-LP case; the asset class differs but the Tactic-level taxonomy does not.

## Observed indicators

- Large LP-burn-or-withdraw event by an address that traces to the deployer cluster (OAK-M04 funder-graph).
- Simultaneous collapse of pool depth (>50% in a single block) and trading-pair price.
- Pre-event pattern often matches an OAK-T2.002 (Locked-Liquidity Spoof) setup: nominal lock with weak custodian, short lock duration, or delegate-callable unlock.
- NFT analogue: mint-proceeds wallet sweeps to mixers / CEX deposit addresses within hours of mint-out (OAK-T7.001 chain).

## Detection signals

- On-chain real-time: LP token movements above a chain-specific threshold (e.g., >25% of pool LP supply within 1 block on EVM; equivalent on Raydium / Orca pool-state diff for Solana) from clusters previously flagged at T1/T2.
- Pre-event correlation: liquidity-removal pattern correlated with prior locked-liquidity-spoof signals (OAK-M05 authority-graph).
- Block-builder telemetry (OAK-M06): mempool view of large LP-burn calls broadcast by deployer-cluster addresses prior to inclusion.
- Cluster-level: same operator deploying repeated tokens with matching post-launch withdrawal signature (cohort-level attribution per `[chainalysis2025rug]`).

## Real-world examples

- **AnubisDAO (ANKH) — Ethereum — 2021-10-28 / 2021-10-29** — Loss \~13,556 ETH (\~\$60M at the time). Approximately 20 hours into the project's token sale, the LP was drained from one transaction and the proceeds laundered through Tornado Cash within 24 hours (chained as OAK-T7.001). Canonical hard-rug case study with extensive public attribution analysis. Forensic write-ups: `[decryptanubis2021]`, `[cointelegraphanubismixer2022]`; aggregate context: `[chainalysis2021scams]`.
- **SQUID (Squid Game token) — BNB Chain — 2021-11-01** — Loss \~\$3.38M. After the token's price spike, the operator cluster removed pool liquidity in a single event, dropping the price effectively to zero within minutes. Combined with the OAK-T1.001 transfer-restriction pattern (the contract blocked sells), SQUID is a textbook example of the T1 + T2 + T5 chain executing in the same operator's playbook. Forensic write-up: `[trmsquid2021]`.
- **Frosties (NFT) — Ethereum — 2022-01-09** — Loss \~\$1.1M (~\$1.3M including downstream laundering). Founders Ethan Nguyen and Andre Llacuna sold out the mint then within hours transferred the proceeds to wallets under their control and abandoned the project. First U.S. DOJ NFT-rug-pull prosecution; conspirators charged with wire fraud and money-laundering conspiracy. ## Mitigations

- **OAK-M04 (funder-graph clustering):** identify deployer-cluster ahead of LP creation; flag pools whose deployer cluster touches known repeat-rug clusters.
- **OAK-M05 (authority-graph enumeration):** map LP-token custodian and unlock authority before listing; combine with OAK-M25 (listing-time gate).
- **OAK-M11 (rate limiting / per-block caps):** at the venue layer, cap LP-removal velocity per block to convert any T5.001 attempt into a T5.002 (slow trickle) which is harder to monetise before defender response.
- **Traders / risk teams:** combine pre-event T1/T2 signals into a composite risk score before entry; T5.001 itself is too late to mitigate in-trade.
- **Protocols / launchpads:** require listing-level verification of LP authority structure before listing (OAK-M25).

## Discussion

T5.001 is the most-studied extraction Technique. Per-incident attribution is straightforward post-hoc; the open detection problem is **pre-event** classification, where the signal lives in T1/T2/T3 features rather than T5.001 itself. v0.1 scopes T5.001 to deployer-cluster-attributable single-event extractions; the borderline cases are: (a) governance-authorised LP migrations later disputed by holders (e.g., DAO-vote-driven LP-redistribution that holders consider a rug — out-of-scope at v0.1, candidate for OAK-T5.005-style "treasury management exit"); (b) contested NFT roadmap-abandonment cases where the operator did not directly drain LP but failed to deliver against the mint commitment (Pixelmon-class — currently scoped to T5.001 but a future v0.x split into "NFT mint-proceeds extraction" is plausible). Calibration gap (v0.x): the per-chain LP-removal velocity threshold is currently hand-tuned per detector; a calibrated dataset of confirmed T5.001 events with pool-baseline normalisation would improve detector precision.
