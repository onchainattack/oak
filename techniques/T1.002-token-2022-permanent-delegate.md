# OAK-T1.002 — Token-2022 Permanent Delegate Authority

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T1 (Token Genesis), OAK-T6 (Defense Evasion)
**Maturity:** observed
**Chains:** Solana
**First documented:** 2024 (industry advisories); industrial-scale abuse from late 2024 onward
**Aliases:** "permanent delegate token", "PD authority", "burn-on-buy scam"

## Description

A Solana SPL Token-2022 mint deployed with the `PermanentDelegate` extension, which grants a designated address standing authority to move or burn any holder's balance without holder consent or a per-call delegation. From a defender's perspective, this is an extension whose *presence alone* is the signal — its semantics are well-defined and documented in the SPL standard, so detection is a binary check on mint metadata rather than a heuristic.

Unlike T1.001 / T1.005 (EVM-side fee patterns), there is no behavioural dynamic to simulate: by the time the trader sees a transfer succeed, the delegate authority has already been embedded in the mint config and cannot be revoked except via the delegate's own keys. The defender's window is *pre-trade*, not post-trade. Industrial-scale exploitation of this extension on Solana has been observed since approximately Q3 2024 in the form of "burn-on-buy" rug-pulls, in which the delegate burns the buyer's tokens within seconds of purchase, leaving them with no recourse.

## Observed indicators

- Mint account on the SPL Token-2022 program (`TokenzQdBNbLqP5VEhdkAS6EPFLC1PHnBqCXEpPxuEb`) whose extension list contains `PermanentDelegate` (extension type `12` in the canonical `ExtensionType` enum). Read via `getAccountInfo` on the mint and parse extension TLV blob.
- The `PermanentDelegate` authority field is non-zero (an active delegate is configured, not a future-reservation slot).
- Delegate address is controlled by the deployer cluster — share funder, share authority, or appear in the same deployer's prior mints — rather than a published, governance-gated authority on a known allowlist (e.g. exchange custody, sanctioned-asset issuer's compliance authority).
- Mint additionally exhibits one or more of: `MintCloseAuthority` set; `TransferHook` set to a deployer-controlled program; freeze authority not renounced — combine with the PD extension as elevated risk.
- Cross-token: deployer cluster has previously issued mints whose lifecycle ended with PD-driven burns of holder balances (transaction-history pattern: `BurnChecked` against holder accounts, signed by the delegate authority, within seconds of `MintTo` or `Transfer` from the deployer-funded LP).

## Detection signals

- **Static, pre-trade (mint-config parse):** for every Token-2022 mint, parse the extension TLV; flag any mint whose extensions include `PermanentDelegate` and whose delegate authority is not on a vetted allowlist. This is a one-shot RPC call (`getAccountInfo` + extension parse) and yields a deterministic boolean — not a heuristic. A Helius / Triton / standard-RPC-backed indexer can stream this signal in real time.
- **Indexer query (Solana):** subscribe to mint creations on the Token-2022 program; for each, decode `MintConfig` and emit on detection of PD. Emit a second alert if the delegate signs `BurnChecked` against any non-deployer account post-mint.
- **Behavioural confirmation (post-mint, pre-trade for the next victim):** scan transaction history of the mint for `BurnChecked` instructions whose `authority` field equals the PD authority and whose target is not the deployer cluster — this is the "burn-on-buy" signature and confirms hostile intent. Even a single such burn is sufficient evidence at v0.1; the action is not reachable to non-malicious operators (non-malicious PD usage burns deployer-held or operator-held tokens, not arbitrary holder tokens).
- **Cross-extension cluster:** correlate PD-bearing mints with `TransferHook`, `MintCloseAuthority`, and unrenounced freeze authority — multi-extension stacks compound the operator's surface area and tend to appear in deliberately-malicious mints.
- **Cross-token deployer cluster:** group PD mints by funder; if a deployer's prior PD mints exhibit the burn-on-buy signature, treat new PD mints from the same deployer as confirmed-malicious (not just elevated-risk).

## Mitigations

- **OAK-M05 (Authority-Graph Enumeration)** — required: enumerate every Token-2022 extension authority on the mint; treat PD authority as a first-class authority surface alongside mint and freeze authorities.
- **OAK-M02 (Static-Analysis Pre-Deployment)** — for venues / launchpads, parse mint extensions at listing time and gate listing on a vetted-delegate allowlist.
- **OAK-M25 (Listing-Time Source-Verification + Audit-Status Gate)** — listing precondition for venues should include extension allowlist conformance.
- **OAK-M03 (Continuous Bytecode-Diff Monitoring)** — analogue: continuous mint-config monitoring; alert on any extension changes between block N and N+1 (Token-2022 supports some authority transfers post-deployment).

Operational: traders / risk teams filter out Token-2022 mints with `PermanentDelegate` enabled unless the delegate is on a published trusted-authority allowlist. Wallets / venues surface the extension prominently in pre-trade UI; do not allow approvals or swaps without explicit user acknowledgement of the extension's scope (Jupiter's pre-swap warning is the canonical pattern).

## Discussion

The Token-2022 extension family includes several other authority-bearing extensions (`TransferHook`, `ConfidentialTransfer`, `MintCloseAuthority`, `TransferFeeConfig`) that may warrant their own Techniques in v0.2. The closest near-neighbour is `MintCloseAuthority` (which lets the authority close the mint and reclaim rent, but does not directly affect holder balances) — this should likely be a sibling sub-Technique rather than a sub-pattern of T1.002.

Calibration gap at v0.1: legitimate uses of `PermanentDelegate` exist (compliance-gated stablecoins, regulated securities, KYC-bound tokens). The static-rule output of "PD present → flag" produces false positives against this legitimate-use cohort. The pragmatic resolution is a vetted-delegate allowlist (analogous to the timelock allowlist for T1.001), not a different static rule. v0.x should formalise the allowlist schema and admission criteria.

Attribution-strength caveat: at the cohort scale, "PD present + delegate is deployer-clustered + prior burn-on-buy signature" is a high-confidence malicious classification. At the per-mint scale, absent prior-burn evidence, the classification is "elevated risk" rather than "confirmed malicious"; do not collapse the two in defender tooling output.
