# OAK-T11.005 — Operator-side Fake-Platform Fraud

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T11 (Custody and Signing Infrastructure)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** chain-agnostic (deposit substrate is BTC / ETH / stablecoins on-chain; the fraud "platform" is off-chain operator-controlled UI/database)
**First documented:** 2014 (OneCoin) at the modern threshold; pre-pig-butchering-era (2014–2018) MLM-Ponzi cohort plus 2018-onward multi-asset-wallet Ponzi plus 2020-onward fake-CEX / pig-butchering cohort
**Aliases:** "fake-CEX", "pig-butchering platform", "fake custodian", "investment-fraud platform", "MLM fake-cryptocurrency Ponzi", "rug platform"
**Sub-Techniques:** OAK-T11.005.001 (Fake-CEX / Pig-Butchering Platform), OAK-T11.005.002 (Fake-Custodian / Fake-Asset-Manager Fraud — described inline), OAK-T11.005.003 (Compound-Operated Investment-Fraud Platforms)

## Description

End-to-end operator-controlled platform fraud where the *platform itself* is the vehicle of theft — a fake centralised exchange, fake custodial wallet / asset manager, fake yield product, or pig-butchering investment site whose deposit-acceptance, balance-display, and withdrawal-blocking are all operator-controlled UI/database fictions. There is no real exchange backend, no real custody, no segregated client funds. The operator collects retail deposits in BTC / ETH / stablecoins, displays fictitious balance growth in a database-backed UI, and rejects withdrawals (or extracts a "tax" / "verification fee" before further blocking) until the cohort exits.

T11.005 is **structurally distinct** from adjacent custody-and-signing-class Techniques and rug-pull classes:

- **T11.001** (Third-Party Signing-Vendor UI / Signing-Flow Compromise) — the *real* platform's vendor surface is breached and the customer organisation is the on-chain victim. T11.005 inverts this: there is no real platform; the platform itself is the fraud.
- **T11.002** (Wallet-Software Distribution Compromise) — substituted binary on a real wallet brand. T11.005's platform never had a benign predecessor.
- **T11.003** (In-Use Multisig Smart-Contract Manipulation) — manipulation of a real on-chain multisig. T11.005 typically operates without any on-chain protocol primitive at all.
- **T5.005** (Treasury-Management Exit) — a real operator's exit on a real DeFi platform; the operator had a fiduciary relationship with the protocol. T11.005 had no such relationship.
- **T4 phishing** — phishing acquires access to a *real* user's holdings on a *real* platform. T11.005's users deposit voluntarily into an operator-controlled wallet they believe to be a legitimate exchange / custodian.
- **T2.x rug-pull tokens** — token-side liquidity-establishment fraud. T11.005's vehicle is a *platform*, not a token; there is no AMM pool, no token contract, often no on-chain protocol whatsoever; the deposit destination is a single operator-controlled wallet and the "balance" is a Postgres row.

The cohort aggregates the **largest retail-attack surface in crypto by aggregated dollar loss**. FBI IC3 reported \$5.8B in pig-butchering-class losses in the U.S. alone in 2024; Chainalysis estimates pig-butchering revenue grew approximately 40% YoY in 2024.

T11.005 has three structurally-distinct sub-patterns plus one historical-anchor framing:

- **T11.005.001 — Fake-CEX / pig-butchering platform.** No real exchange backend; balance display and "trade" functionality are entirely UI-side fictions; withdrawals are blocked or gated behind escalating "tax" / "verification fee" demands; the deposit destination is a single operator-controlled wallet. Anchored in the 2020–2026 BeurAx-class cohort and the DOJ June 2025 Philippines compound \$225M USDT seizure / DOJ October 2025 Chen Zhi / Prince Group \$15B Bitcoin forfeiture (the largest in U.S. history).
- **T11.005.002 — Fake-custodian / fake-asset-manager fraud.** The operator presents an MLM "wealth management" or "AI-trading" yield product backed by a custodial wallet they control; the on-chain receivables are real but the trading / yield generation is fictitious; payouts to early entrants are funded entirely from later entrants' deposits (Ponzi structure). Anchored across PlusToken (2018-2019), HyperVerse / HyperFund (DOJ + SEC 2024), Forsage (SEC 2022), CryptoFX (SEC 2024), CoinDeal-Chandran (SEC + DOJ 2023).
- **T11.005.003 — Compound-operated investment-fraud platforms.** The fake-CEX / fake-investment-platform pattern of T11.005.001 distributed at industrial scale through forced-labour scam compounds in Southeast Asia (Cambodia, Burma / Myanmar, Laos), with romance-scam feeder narrative as the user-acquisition surface. Anchors: Chen Zhi / Prince Group (DOJ October 2025); Ly Yong Phat / O-Smach Resort cohort (OFAC September 2024); Huione Group laundering substrate (FinCEN section 311 final rule October 2025).
- **Historical anchor (pre-pig-butchering era):** OneCoin (Ruja Ignatova / Karl Sebastian Greenwood, 2014–2017, ~\$4B+; FBI Ten Most Wanted June 2022; Greenwood guilty December 2022, 20-year sentence September 2023). The OneCoin case anchors the pre-2018-pig-butchering era and demonstrates that the fake-platform structural class has a continuous lineage from MLM-token Ponzi (2014–2017) through Telegram-recruited multi-asset wallet Ponzi (2018–2019) into pig-butchering fake-CEX (2020–2026).

## Observed indicators

- **Off-chain (operator-side):** no verifiable corporate registration in the claimed jurisdiction; no audit; no proof-of-reserves; high-yield "guaranteed return" claims; no public order book; no observable on-chain trading flow consistent with claimed AUM; brand-impersonation of a legitimate exchange or custodian; MLM-style referral commissions baked into the user-acquisition flow.
- **On-chain:** deposit-aggregation pattern into a single or small number of operator wallets without observable matched-trading outflow; large fan-in concentration consistent with custodial-Ponzi receivables rather than exchange operational flow.
- **User-side:** un-fillable withdrawal requests; "tax" / "verification fee" / "anti-money-laundering deposit" preconditions for withdrawal; communication-channel collapse after exit.
- **Receivables-fingerprint:** pre-2018 cases launder predominantly through correspondent banking (OneCoin's "Fenero Funds" Cayman vehicles; ~\$400M routed via BNY Mellon / Bank of Ireland) because receivables were fiat; 2020-onward cases launder predominantly on-chain (mixers, cross-chain bridges, Russian-CEX off-ramps, Huione Group laundering substrate) because receivables are crypto.

## Detection signals

- **Off-chain OSINT:** regulator-warning subscription (FCA, SEC, KNF, SFC, BaFin, ASIC published warnings on unlicensed platforms); investigative-journalism cross-reference (BehindMLM, ICAC, BBC *Missing Cryptoqueen*-class long-form investigative reporting); corporate-registry verification in the claimed-headquarters jurisdiction; absence of a public blockchain explorer when the platform claims to operate one.
- **On-chain forensics:** deposit-aggregation-without-trading-outflow signal; cross-cluster overlap with Huione Group / Cambodia-compound laundering substrate; aggregated victim-deposit fan-in to a small wallet set.
- **User-side defensive signal:** small test-withdrawal precedes any meaningful deposit. The "if you cannot withdraw a small test amount before depositing more, the platform is a fake-CEX" rule is the canonical user-side mitigation.
- **Retail-bank-side debit-card monitoring** for known scam-platform deposit destinations is the dominant *cohort-level* defender intervention. The FBI's Operation Level Up (initiated January 2024) had notified 5,831+ victims and prevented \$359M+ in losses by April 2025.

## Mitigations

- **Regulator-side:** publication and continuous maintenance of licensed-VASP registries; cross-jurisdiction warning-feed coordination (KNF / SFC / SEC / FCA / FinCEN MSB / BaFin / ASIC); civil-forfeiture infrastructure at the cohort-recovery scale.
- **Platform-side warnings:** FBI IC3 PSAs; FinCEN 2023 pig-butchering alert; FinCEN 2025 Huione Group section 311 designation; CFTC retail-customer advisories; CISA / Treasury cyber-fraud joint advisories.
- **User-side:** small-test-withdrawal-before-deposit invariant; refusal to deposit to platforms without verifiable corporate registration / audit / proof-of-reserves; refusal to send "tax" or "verification fee" preconditional to withdrawal; cross-reference against published licensed-VASP registries before depositing.
- **Retail-bank-side:** debit-card monitoring for known scam-platform deposit destinations (FBI Operation Level Up coordination model — 5,831+ victims notified, \$359M+ saved by April 2025).
- **Off-chain CTI integration:** subscription to regulator-warning feeds; investigative-journalism cross-reference; corporate-registry verification automation for emerging platforms.

OAK Mitigation cross-references at v0.1 are a **gap** — no existing OAK-M* entry directly maps to the regulator-publication / retail-bank-coordination / off-chain-OSINT mitigation surface that T11.005 most leverages. v0.x candidate Mitigation entries: "OAK-M-x licensed-VASP-registry monitoring", "OAK-M-x retail-bank deposit-destination blocklist coordination", "OAK-M-x small-test-withdrawal-before-deposit invariant".

## Discussion

T11.005 is the structurally largest **retail-aggregate-loss** Technique class in OAK at v0.1 and the most under-served by v0.1 Tactic / Technique mapping. The cohort aggregates ~\$5.8B in U.S.-reported 2024 losses alone (FBI IC3) plus the broader global multiple of that figure; for comparison, T11.001 (Bybit + DMM + WazirX-class) federal-record dollar totals are an order of magnitude smaller in aggregated retail-victim count even where individual-incident totals are larger.

The Technique was held in TAXONOMY-GAPS through v0.1 because:

1. The class is "off-chain" in the load-bearing sense — the platform's database is the substrate of fraud, not an on-chain protocol — and earlier OAK drafts treated it as out-of-scope for an *on-chain* attack-knowledge framework.
2. The detection-and-mitigation surface lives at the regulator / retail-bank / off-chain-OSINT layer rather than the on-chain forensics layer that anchors most other OAK Techniques.
3. The cohort attribution is largely confirmed at the federal / regulator-record level (DOJ, SEC, CFTC, FCA, FinCEN, OFAC) rather than at the cluster-forensic level that anchors OAK-G actor profiles.

The promotion to `emerging` reflects that:

- The class is now multi-source-attested (DOJ, SEC, FBI IC3, FinCEN, OFAC, Chainalysis Crypto Crime Report, FCA, KNF, SFC, BBC investigative reporting).
- Multiple worked examples now anchor the class across distinct sub-patterns (OneCoin = T11.005.002 historical; CryptoFX = T11.005.002 affinity-fraud Latino; HyperVerse = T11.005.002 global-MLM; with a pipeline of T11.005.001 fake-CEX and T11.005.003 compound-operated cases tracked in TAXONOMY-GAPS for v0.x).
- The structural distinctions vs. T11.001 / T11.002 / T11.003 / T5.005 / T4 / T2.x are clean enough to support sub-Technique granularity.

`stable` will be appropriate once the v0.x worked-example pipeline (BeurAx-class, Chen Zhi / Prince Group, Philippines compound, Forsage, CoinDeal, PlusToken-as-T11.005.002-anchor, JPEX-as-borderline, Southeast Asia compound cohort, Huione Group laundering substrate) is committed under `examples/`.

The receivables-fingerprint observation (pre-2018 cases launder through correspondent banking; 2020-onward cases launder on-chain) is structurally important and recurs across the cohort. Contributors writing future T11.005 worked examples should preserve the receivables-fiat-vs-crypto dimension as a first-class observable. The laundering-chain fingerprint correlates with the era of the case rather than the structural class.

Operator-mobility-and-disappearance is a recurring T11.005 feature (Ignatova October 2017; Schneider May 2023; multiple Southeast Asian compound operators). Contributors should record (a) operator nationality, (b) jurisdiction footprint, (c) extradition-cooperation status with U.S. / EU / OECD jurisdictions, (d) post-incident operator-mobility events. Federal-record-confirmed attribution does not require operator-in-custody.

The realised-loss-versus-aggregate-claimed-loss gap is structurally informative. Per-victim recovery rates are typically <5%; the dominant recovery surface is DOJ civil-forfeiture at cohort scale. Contributors should report (a) realised recovery, (b) recovery surface (restitution fund vs. civil forfeiture vs. unrecovered), (c) aggregate-claimed-loss-to-realised-recovery ratio explicitly.
