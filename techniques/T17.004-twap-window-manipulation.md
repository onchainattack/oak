# OAK-T17.004 — TWAP / Time-Window Manipulation Against DAO Treasury / Vesting Math

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation)
**Adjacent Techniques:** OAK-T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation — generic oracle-feed manipulation; T17.004 is the specific case where the load-bearing surface is a *downstream contract's* TWAP / time-window settlement math rather than the oracle's input venue), OAK-T5.006 (Vesting-Cliff Dump — T5.006 is the *holder-side* dump at the cliff window; T17.004 is the *manipulator-side* shaping of the window's settlement reference), OAK-T8.001 (Operational Reuse — Cluster-Reuse Signal — load-bearing for window-manipulation cohort attribution)
**Maturity:** draft
**Chains:** EVM (Uniswap V2/V3 TWAP windows used as oracle reference; OpenZeppelin TimelockController / Compound Timelock-class windows; vesting-contract designs across OpenZeppelin VestingWallet / Sablier / Hedgey); cross-chain TWAP-consuming designs broadly inherit the surface
**First documented:** Class anchored conceptually 2020-2022 (Uniswap V2 / V3 TWAP-as-oracle deployment); canonical extraction-scale OAK anchor not yet landed at v0.4
**Aliases:** "TWAP manipulation", "window timing attack", "vesting-window manipulation", "treasury-swap window manipulation", "time-weighted price manipulation", "settlement-window timing"

## Description

Manipulation of a downstream contract's TWAP (time-weighted average price) window or vesting-cliff window such that the contract's settlement math executes at a price / rate that the manipulator has shaped. The defining shape is that **the load-bearing surface is the downstream contract's window-settlement math**, not the oracle's input venue itself — the oracle reads its inputs honestly, the venues quote prices honestly, but the manipulator concentrates trading volume inside the TWAP window (or pre-positions for the vesting-cliff window) such that the time-weighted average price (or the cliff-time spot price) is shaped into a configuration favourable to the manipulator at the settlement layer.

T17.004 is **structurally distinct from T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation — generic)**. T9.001 covers the generic case where the oracle's input is manipulated (thin-AMM-pool single-block move, vault-share donation, pull-reporter dispute-window bypass). T17.004 covers the specific case where the *downstream contract's TWAP-window selection* is the load-bearing manipulation surface. The two compose: the manipulator may use T9.001-class moves on the input venue *during* the TWAP window to shape the average price; the T17.004 distinction is that the *window-timing primitive* is the load-bearing exploit surface — closing T17.004 requires hardening the window-selection (randomised window, multi-window quorum, post-hoc window snapshot) regardless of how the input-venue prices are manipulated.

T17.004 is **structurally distinct from T5.006 (Vesting-Cliff Dump)**. T5.006 is the *holder-side* dump at the cliff window — insider holders who unlocked tokens at the cliff dump them on the open market, producing post-cliff price impact against retail / token-holder counterparties. T17.004 is the *manipulator-side* shaping of the window's settlement reference — the vesting contract's payout calculation (in unlock-as-USD-at-window-spot, or unlock-as-rate-against-TWAP, or unlock-as-stablecoin-at-window-rate designs) is shaped by the manipulator's control over the window-settlement math. The two can compose — the cliff-window dump (T5.006) and the window-settlement manipulation (T17.004) operate at the same temporal layer — but the load-bearing primitives are distinct.

T17.004 covers four structurally adjacent sub-shapes:

- **TWAP-window concentration.** The manipulator concentrates trading volume inside a downstream contract's TWAP window (e.g., Uniswap V3 TWAP fed into a lending oracle, a treasury-swap price-reference, a vesting-rate-conversion contract) such that the time-weighted average price is shaped, even though instantaneous prices outside the window are honest. Distinct from T9.001 because closing T9.001 by adding a TWAP window does not close T17.004 — the manipulation is *inside* the window, not against the window's input-venue.
- **DAO treasury-swap window timing.** A DAO governance proposal authorises a treasury swap at a price determined by a future TWAP window; the manipulator pre-positions and concentrates trading volume inside the window to shape the swap rate against the DAO treasury. Boundary case against T16.x governance attacks: T17.004 may compose with T16.005 (malicious-proposal snowballing) when the proposal text obscures the window-manipulation surface.
- **Vesting-rate-conversion window manipulation.** A vesting contract converts unlocked tokens to a stablecoin / counter-asset at a rate determined by a window-settlement primitive; the manipulator (the unlocking insider, or a separate cohort) shapes the window settlement to favour the unlock recipient. Distinct from T5.006 because the manipulation is at the *rate-determination* layer, not at the post-unlock dump layer.
- **Oracle-update-window timing.** Some oracles update at predictable windows (e.g., Chainlink's `updateAnswer` cadence, UMA's pull-oracle dispute-window structure); the manipulator pre-positions for the window-boundary moment to capture cross-venue spread or to force a settlement against a window-edge price. Boundary case against T17.001 (cross-venue arbitrage) when the oracle-update window is also the cross-venue propagation window.

## Why this Technique is `draft` at v0.4

T17.004 is introduced as `draft` rather than `emerging` because OAK does not have a clean dollar-loss anchor for the window-settlement-manipulation class as a *standalone* extraction-scale incident. The class is documented at the academic / market-microstructure layer (multiple papers on TWAP-window manipulation as a theoretical attack surface against Uniswap V3 TWAP-fed oracles) and is structurally adjacent to multiple T9.001 incidents (where the manipulation occurred during a TWAP window but the load-bearing surface was the oracle's *input* layer rather than the window-settlement layer), but the canonical case where the *window-settlement-math itself* is the load-bearing surface and the extraction is quantified at extraction-scale has not yet landed in the OAK corpus.

Specifically, the v0.4 documentation gap is:

- **TWAP-window manipulation cases are typically dual-classified as T9.001 by load-bearing-surface convention.** When the manipulation is a thin-AMM-pool move *during* a TWAP window, the convention is to classify it as T9.001 because the input-venue manipulation is the load-bearing surface even though the window-timing is what made the move profitable. T17.004 reserves the framing for cases where the *window-selection / window-settlement primitive* is the load-bearing surface — manipulation is feasible because the window is predictable, the window is short relative to the manipulator's capital, or the window-settlement math accepts a single-window-snapshot reference.
- **DAO treasury-swap window-manipulation public record is sparse.** DAO treasuries that swap against a future TWAP-window settlement are operationally common, but the public record of named-attacker window-manipulation cases at extraction-scale is sparse — most public cases are governance-side disputes (whether the treasury swap was proposed at the right window) rather than market-side window-manipulation extraction events.
- **Vesting-rate-conversion contract manipulation public record is sparse.** Vesting contracts with rate-conversion at the cliff window are operationally common, but the public record of named-attacker rate-conversion-window manipulation at extraction-scale is sparse.

Future contributors should anchor T17.004 once a public-record case lands at the dollar-loss scale comparable to T17.002 / T16.001-003 anchors. Promotion to `emerging` requires at least one canonical worked example where the window-settlement-math is the load-bearing surface (rather than the input-venue / oracle layer) and the extraction is quantified.

## Observed indicators

- **Pre-event:** trading-volume / order-flow concentration inside an upcoming TWAP window or at the boundary moment of a vesting-cliff / oracle-update window; cohort-attribution clusters that pre-position capital for window-boundary manipulation.
- **At-event (window-settlement):** TWAP-window average price diverges from the surrounding period's average price by an amount that exceeds the asset's typical intra-day price drift; the divergence direction favours the cohort whose downstream-contract settlement landed at the window-settled price.
- **Post-event:** the cohort that benefitted from the window-settlement is correlated with the cohort that concentrated trading volume inside the window; funder-graph clustering connects the two cohorts.
- **Forensic record:** the per-block trading-volume profile across the TWAP window; the downstream-contract settlement transaction; the cohort's net-position trajectory across the window.

## Detection signals

- **Window-randomisation at downstream contract layer:** the canonical mitigation. Downstream contracts whose settlement math consumes a randomised TWAP window (or a multi-window quorum, or a post-hoc window snapshot) defeat T17.004 by construction — the manipulator cannot pre-position for an unpredictable window.
- **Trading-volume-concentration monitor at TWAP-window boundary:** continuous monitoring of trading-volume distribution around predictable downstream-contract TWAP windows (DAO treasury-swap windows, vesting-cliff conversion windows, oracle-update windows). Volume concentration inside the window correlated with cohort attribution at the bot layer is T17.004-suspect.
- **Cross-window divergence monitor:** TWAP window-settled price vs. surrounding period's TWAP price; divergences exceeding the asset's typical intra-day drift are T17.004-suspect.
- **Cohort-attribution applied to window-boundary trading:** funder-graph clustering applied to accounts whose trading is concentrated at downstream-contract window boundaries; clusters repeatedly profitable on window-settlement direction are T17.004-suspect.
- **Pre-deployment design review of downstream-contract window-settlement math:** audit of treasury-swap / vesting-conversion / oracle-update window-selection primitives; flag designs that consume single-window snapshots without randomisation, multi-window quorum, or post-hoc snapshot.

## Mitigations

- **Protocols (OAK-M09 — TWAP-window-randomisation / multi-window quorum):** highest-leverage layer. Downstream contracts whose settlement math consumes a randomised TWAP window (or multi-window quorum, or post-hoc window snapshot) defeat T17.004 by construction.
- **Protocols (OAK-M02 — pre-deployment design review of window-settlement math):** audit and formal verification of treasury-swap / vesting-conversion / oracle-update window-selection primitives; flag designs that consume single-window snapshots without randomisation, multi-window quorum, or post-hoc snapshot.
- **Protocols (OAK-M11 — settlement-throughput caps):** for treasury-swap / vesting-conversion windows where the settlement amount exceeds the venue's depth-vs-trade-size response, caps on per-window settlement throughput bound the per-incident extraction payoff.
- **Protocols — multi-venue oracle reference for window-settlement math:** treasury-swap / vesting-conversion contracts that consume a multi-venue oracle reference (rather than a single-venue TWAP) defeat single-venue window-manipulation by construction. Mitigation surface overlaps with T17.001 mitigation.
- **Risk teams — window-boundary volume-concentration monitoring + cohort-attribution:** continuous monitoring of trading-volume distribution around predictable downstream-contract TWAP windows correlated with cohort-attribution at the bot layer.

## Discussion

T17.004's structural lesson is that **window-randomisation is the load-bearing mitigation** against window-settlement manipulation. The class is structurally distinct from T9.001 (generic oracle manipulation) by the load-bearing-surface test: closing T9.001 by adding a TWAP window does not close T17.004 — the manipulator can still concentrate volume inside the window. Closing T17.004 requires that the window-selection primitive itself is unpredictable (randomised window, multi-window quorum, post-hoc snapshot).

The cohort is structurally distinct from T9.001 by *load-bearing surface*: T9.001's load-bearing surface is the oracle's input venue; T17.004's load-bearing surface is the downstream contract's window-selection / window-settlement math. The cohort is structurally distinct from T5.006 (vesting-cliff dump) by *which side of the unlock is the manipulation*: T5.006 is the holder-side dump on the post-unlock spot market; T17.004 is the manipulator-side shaping of the unlock's rate-conversion settlement.

T17.004's `draft` status reflects the v0.4 documentation gap honestly. The class is real and well-documented at the academic / audit-finding / theoretical-attack-surface layer, but the canonical case where the window-settlement-math itself is the load-bearing surface and the extraction is quantified has not yet landed in the OAK corpus. Most existing TWAP-window incidents are dual-classified as T9.001 because the input-venue manipulation is the load-bearing surface even though the window-timing made the move profitable. Promotion to `emerging` requires at least one canonical worked example where the window-selection / window-settlement primitive is the load-bearing exploit surface rather than the input-venue layer.

Attribution-strength considerations: T17.004 attribution is typically `pseudonymous-cohort` rather than `pseudonymous-attacker` — the cohort that benefits from window-settlement manipulation is identifiable at the funder-graph-cluster layer combined with trading-volume-concentration analysis, but per-incident named-attacker attribution is rare.

False-positive considerations: legitimate trading inside TWAP windows is operationally routine; the detection signal is not "trading occurred during a TWAP window" but specifically "trading-volume concentration inside the window correlated with cohort-attribution at the bot layer, AND a window-settled price that diverges from surrounding-period price by more than the asset's typical intra-day drift, AND the cohort that profits from the divergence overlaps the cohort that concentrated the volume." Cohort-level analysis is required to distinguish T17.004 from baseline window-period trading.

v0.x deferred items: a canonical worked example at extraction-scale where the window-settlement-math is the load-bearing surface (the v0.4 documentation gap that promotes T17.004 from `draft` to `emerging`); a structured rubric for distinguishing TWAP-window-concentration / DAO-treasury-swap-window / vesting-rate-conversion / oracle-update-window sub-shapes; cohort-level metrics on window-boundary trading-volume distribution as a forward-looking T17.004 surface estimate.
