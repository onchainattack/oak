# OAK-T11.006 — Cold-storage Seed-phrase Exfiltration at Rest

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T11 (Custody and Signing Infrastructure)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** chain-agnostic (the substrate-of-extraction is the BIP39 seed phrase / private key material itself; downstream extraction occurs across whichever chains the affected wallet holds)
**First documented:** 2022-04 (iCloud-backup MetaMask / Iacovone case) for the implicit-cloud-custody sub-pattern; 2022-12 (LastPass encrypted-vault exfiltration) for the user-initiated plaintext-storage sub-pattern; cohort window remains open at v0.1 reporting horizon
**Aliases:** "seed-phrase at rest exfiltration", "password-manager seed-storage compromise", "iCloud-backup wallet drain", "third-party-storage seed-phrase compromise"

## Description

Compromise of seed-phrase / private-key material held in third-party storage services where the user did not realise they had created a custody surface. The structural feature is *seed-phrase material at rest in a third-party storage substrate* whose security posture is independent of the wallet vendor and the user's on-chain hygiene. Compromise of the third-party service produces seed-phrase material to attackers without any direct user-action signing or wallet-software compromise.

T11.006 has **two structurally-distinct sub-patterns**:

- **T11.006.001 — User-initiated plaintext-equivalent storage.** The user explicitly stores seed-phrase material in a third-party service (LastPass Secure Notes; 1Password / Bitwarden Secure-Notes-equivalent; password-protected notes in productivity tools; email drafts; cloud-synced text files), with vault encryption typically bound to a master password whose strength varies across users. Compromise path: the third-party service's encrypted vaults are exfiltrated; attackers brute-force weak master passwords offline; the resulting plaintext yields seed phrases that are used to drain on-chain wallets. The exfiltration-to-drain time window is unbounded — once the encrypted material is in attacker hands, the brute-force window cannot be closed. Canonical: 2022-12 LastPass encrypted-vault exfiltration → 2023–2025 multi-year crypto-drain cohort, ~$35M+ aggregated across ~150+ victims plus ~$150M Larsen heist (federal-record-attested via March 2025 NDCA forfeiture filing); see [`examples/2022-12-lastpass-vault-cohort.md`](../examples/2022-12-lastpass-vault-cohort.md).

- **T11.006.002 — Implicit cloud-custody via default-on cloud-backup.** The wallet vendor's mobile app does not opt out of OS-level cloud-backup defaults, auto-syncing the encrypted vault to a cloud surface tied to a separate-identity security posture (Apple ID for iOS / iCloud; Google account for Android; Microsoft account for Windows). Compromise path: the user's cloud-account credential is compromised via SIM-swap, Apple-ID phishing, social-engineering of Apple Support, or shared-device theft; the attacker accesses the cloud-backup containing the encrypted wallet vault; the vault password is brute-forced offline if weak. Canonical: 2022-04 iCloud-backup MetaMask seed-phrase cohort (Iacovone case ~$650K-$655K plus broader 2022–2024 cohort; MetaMask issued public iOS-backup-disable security guidance two days after Iacovone's public disclosure); see [`examples/2022-04-icloud-metamask-seed-phrase-cohort.md`](../examples/2022-04-icloud-metamask-seed-phrase-cohort.md).

T11.006 is **structurally distinct** from adjacent T11 sub-Techniques:

- **T11.001** (Third-Party Signing-Vendor Compromise) — vendor-side UI / signing-flow compromise with organisational-customer victim shape (Bybit / Safe{Wallet}). T11.006's failure surface is third-party storage at rest; victims are individual end-users.
- **T11.002** (Wallet-Software Distribution Compromise) — vendor-side build-pipeline / update-channel surface (Atomic Wallet). T11.006 does not require any wallet-software compromise; the wallet binary is genuine.
- **T11.003** (In-Use Multisig Smart-Contract Manipulation) — contract-modification of a real on-chain multisig. T11.006 operates entirely off-chain at the storage-service layer.
- **T4 phishing** — phishing acquires on-chain authority signing on a real platform. T11.006's substrate-of-extraction is the seed phrase itself, recovered offline from third-party storage; no on-chain authority signing is required and no user-action signing event correlates with the extraction.

The legitimate-use overlap is **partial**: password managers and cloud-backup are legitimate user-side tools for non-crypto credentials. The class-specific failure mode is the *combination* of (a) seed-phrase material stored in a third-party service and (b) the third-party service's compromise. The user-side mental model "I'm self-custodial because I hold my own keys" collapses when the seed-phrase material is held by a third-party service whose security posture is outside the user's control.

## Observed indicators

- **Service-side:** breach disclosure or exfiltration event affecting a third-party storage service with non-trivial crypto-holding user fraction (LastPass Dec 2022; Apple ID phishing campaigns; Google / Microsoft account compromise cohorts).
- **Per-victim cohort fingerprint:** victims with no other commonality (no SIM swap, no email compromise, no mobile-phone malware, no on-chain phishing) other than having stored seed-phrase material in the same third-party service. Process-of-elimination cohort tracking by ZachXBT, Taylor Monahan, and TRM Labs has been the canonical methodology for the LastPass cohort.
- **Time-shifted drain pattern:** attack-vector-time-shift between the exfiltration event and the realised drains is a structural feature — the LastPass cohort's drains span 2023–2025 from a December 2022 exfiltration because brute-forcing weak master passwords offline takes time.
- **Apple-ID / Google-account compromise antecedent:** for the implicit-cloud-custody sub-pattern, the proximate compromise is at the cloud-account level (SIM-swap, Apple-ID phishing, support-call social-engineering), which precedes the wallet-vault decryption.

## Detection signals

- **User-side audit:** enumerate where seed-phrase material has been stored — password manager Secure Notes, cloud-synced text files, email drafts, screenshots in cloud-synced photo galleries, browser-saved passwords.
- **Wallet-vendor audit:** verify whether the wallet's mobile app opts out of OS-level cloud-backup (iOS `UIDocument.disableBackup` / `NSURLIsExcludedFromBackupKey`; Android `allowBackup=false`). Wallet-vendor cloud-backup behaviour varies across vendors and OS versions.
- **Cohort-tracking (per-victim process-of-elimination):** ZachXBT / Taylor Monahan / TRM Labs methodology — interviewing affected victims about non-storage attack vectors and finding none, producing the storage-commonality observation independently of vendor-side disclosure.
- **Federal-record signals:** forfeiture filings naming a third-party-storage commonality (March 2025 NDCA Larsen filing) are the slow-but-strong attribution signal; industry-forensic attribution typically lands 12–18 months earlier.

## Real-world examples

- **LastPass encrypted-vault exfiltration → multi-year crypto-drain cohort — multi-chain — 2022-12 (breach) / 2023-2025 (drains) (canonical T11.006.001 case)** — At least ~$35M aggregated across ~150+ individual victims tracked by ZachXBT, Taylor Monahan (MetaMask), and TRM Labs through 2023–2025; the single largest documented victim is Ripple co-founder Chris Larsen, ~$150M (January 2024, federally tied to the LastPass breach in a March 2025 U.S. forfeiture filing). TRM Labs traced over ~$28M converted to BTC and laundered through Wasabi Wallet's CoinJoin in late-2024 / early-2025 plus a further ~$7M wave in September 2025. Cohort attribution: `inferred-strong` at the operator-cluster level (Russian-cybercriminal infrastructure, OAK-G03-adjacent; on-chain off-ramps include Cryptex which is OFAC-sanctioned for ransomware-related laundering); LastPass / GoTo continues to publicly dispute the causal link. See [`examples/2022-12-lastpass-vault-cohort.md`](../examples/2022-12-lastpass-vault-cohort.md).
- **iCloud-backup MetaMask seed-phrase cohort — multi-chain (EVM-dominant) — 2022-04 onward (canonical T11.006.002 case)** — Canonical anchor case ~$650K-$655K (Dominic Iacovone — NFT collector — April 15, 2022). Attack onset: Apple ID phishing (spoofed Apple Support call requesting a six-digit verification code); attacker accessed iCloud Backup containing the MetaMask vault; brute-forced the vault password offline; drained ~$650K-$655K in ETH, ERC-20 tokens, and NFTs. MetaMask issued public security guidance on April 17, 2022 advising iOS users to disable iCloud Backup for MetaMask app data. Broader cohort losses through 2022–2024 not centrally tabulated but documented in MetaMask security-team's public guidance and per-victim Reddit / Twitter testimonies. Attribution: `pseudonymous` at the per-incident operator-cluster level. See [`examples/2022-04-icloud-metamask-seed-phrase-cohort.md`](../examples/2022-04-icloud-metamask-seed-phrase-cohort.md).

- `mg-detectors-rs` — coverage **gap** at v0.1. T11.006's primary detection surface is off-chain at the storage-service / cloud-account layer, not the on-chain runtime risk-engine surface MG covers.
- Vendor coverage outside MG: industry-forensic per-victim cohort tracking (ZachXBT, Taylor Monahan / MetaMask security team, TRM Labs); breach-tracking and have-I-been-pwned-class services for storage-service compromise notification; Apple / Google account-security advisories; wallet-vendor security advisories on cloud-backup behaviour (MetaMask iOS-backup-disable guidance is the canonical example). No widely-deployed runtime detector observes the seed-phrase-at-rest surface as of v0.1.

## Mitigations

- **User-side seed-phrase storage hygiene (highest-leverage):** cold storage on physical media (paper, metal); no third-party-service storage of seed-phrase material; no auto-synced cloud-storage including iCloud / Google Drive / OneDrive / Dropbox; no browser-saved-passwords or password-manager Secure-Notes-equivalent for seed phrases.
- **Wallet-vendor responsibility:** opt out of OS-level cloud-backup at the app level (iOS `UIDocument.disableBackup` / `NSURLIsExcludedFromBackupKey`; Android `allowBackup=false`); document the cloud-backup posture in user-facing security guidance; issue immediate post-incident advisories when cloud-backup-as-implicit-custody surface is exploited (MetaMask's April 2022 response is the canonical pattern).
- **Seed-phrase rotation invariant:** if seed-phrase material was *ever* stored in a third-party storage service whose vaults have been exfiltrated, rotate the seed phrase regardless of how strong the master password / encryption was thought to be at the time of storage. The brute-force window is unbounded once the encrypted material is in attacker hands.
- **Cloud-account hygiene:** strong, unique cloud-account passwords; hardware-backed two-factor authentication (not SMS-based); awareness of Apple Support / Google Support social-engineering vectors that solicit verification codes via spoofed-caller-ID calls.
- **Per-victim cohort methodology adoption:** for industry forensics — process-of-elimination cohort analysis (ZachXBT / Taylor Monahan methodology) can establish attribution even when vendor-side disclosure is constrained or actively disputed.

OAK Mitigation cross-references at v0.1 are a **gap** — no existing OAK-M* entry directly maps to the seed-phrase-storage-hygiene mitigation surface that T11.006 most leverages. v0.x candidate Mitigation entries: "OAK-M-x cold-storage seed-phrase invariant"; "OAK-M-x wallet-app cloud-backup opt-out invariant"; "OAK-M-x seed-phrase rotation post-third-party-breach trigger".

## Discussion

T11.006 closes a structural gap in OAK v0.1's T11 coverage. The dominant v0.1 T11 worked examples (Bybit / Safe{Wallet} for T11.001, Atomic Wallet for T11.002, WazirX for T11.003) all share an *organisational-customer-or-vendor-side compromise* shape; T11.006 introduces a **third-party storage-service-at-rest** failure surface that is structurally distinct and that the v0.1 T11.001 / T11.002 / T11.003 sub-Techniques do not cover.

The structural OAK lesson is that **a third-party service that stores plaintext-equivalent seed-phrase material at rest is a custody surface the user did not realise they had created**. The user's mental model of self-custody — "I hold my own keys" — collapses when the seed-phrase material is stored in a third-party service whose security posture is outside the user's control. The lesson generalises beyond LastPass / iCloud: any third-party service that stores plaintext-equivalent seed-phrase material at rest (LastPass, 1Password, Bitwarden if Secure-Notes-equivalent is used, iCloud Notes / Drive, Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, browser-saved passwords, screenshots in cloud-synced gallery, email drafts) constrains the user's effective custody posture to the security posture of the third-party service.

**Attack-vector-time-shift** is a structural feature of T11.006.001 specifically. The LastPass exfiltration event was December 2022 but realised drains span 2023–2025 because brute-forcing weak master passwords offline takes time. Defender practice for any user whose seed-phrase material was ever stored in a third-party service whose vaults were exfiltrated should treat the seed phrase as **permanently compromised** regardless of how strong the master password / encryption was at the time of storage. Seed-phrase rotation is the only operational response.

**Per-victim cohort attribution methodology** is the canonical forensic surface for T11.006. ZachXBT and Taylor Monahan's methodology — interviewing each victim about non-storage attack vectors (SIM swap, email compromise, mobile-phone malware, phishing) and finding none — produced the LastPass-commonality observation independently of any vendor-side disclosure. The pattern recurs across the T11 family where vendor-side disclosure is constrained by litigation strategy or organisational-reputation considerations; the per-victim cohort methodology is the cleanest forensic surface in those cases.

**Vendor-side denial-of-causation** is a recurring pattern. LastPass / GoTo continues to publicly dispute the causal link from the December 2022 vault exfiltration to the multi-year crypto-drain cohort. The pattern recurs across the T11 family (Atomic Wallet's constrained post-mortem disclosure under litigation pressure). Contributors writing future T11.006 worked examples should treat vendor-side disclosure as a first-class observable and should not under-weight industry-forensic attribution at the `inferred-strong` level when the federal record has not yet caught up.

The two sub-patterns differ at the **per-incident shape** layer. T11.006.001 (user-initiated storage) is opt-in by user action — the user chose to put seed-phrase material into the storage service. T11.006.002 (default-on cloud-backup) is opt-in by inaction — the user did nothing to opt out of an OS-level default that the wallet vendor also did not opt out of at the app level. Wallet-vendor app-data backup behaviour varies across vendors, and defender practice for any user with non-trivial holdings should include auditing the wallet app's cloud-backup behaviour explicitly.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once the per-incident worked-example anchor set expands beyond the current two cohort files. Pipeline anchors for v0.x include additional per-incident cases within the LastPass cohort with quantified loss data, and the broader 2022–2024 iCloud-backup cohort with cumulative-loss figures beyond the Iacovone case.
