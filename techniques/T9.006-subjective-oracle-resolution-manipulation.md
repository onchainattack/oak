# OAK-T9.006 — Subjective-Oracle Resolution Manipulation

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T9 (Smart-Contract Exploit), OAK-T17 (Market Manipulation)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM (Polygon-resident Polymarket / Ethereum-resident UMA DVM canonical at v0.1; cross-chain analogues across Kleros, Reality.eth, Augur REP)
**First documented:** Polymarket UMA whale-vote-capture March 2025; cohort scale-out 2025–2026 across Polymarket-class subjective-oracle prediction markets
**Aliases:** "subjective oracle attack", "DVM vote capture", "resolution-spec manipulation", "prediction-market oracle attack", "non-numeric oracle manipulation"

## Description

Smart-contract attacks against oracle systems that adjudicate **natural-language claims** rather than **numeric price feeds**. Distinct from T9.001 (price-feed oracle manipulation) at the artefact level: the "oracle" is a token-weighted vote over a natural-language proposition (UMA DVM, Kleros, Reality.eth, Augur REP), not a numeric reading of a market price. The mitigations OAK lists for T9.001 (TWAP, multi-venue feeds, deviation halts — OAK-M09) are mechanical and apply to numerical price feeds; **none of them apply to subjective-oracle resolution**.

Four properties make subjective-oracle systems qualitatively different from price-feed oracles:

1. **The oracle adjudicates language, not numbers.** "Agrees to a deal" / "wearing a suit" has no TWAP. Spec-ambiguity is the attack surface; the spec itself is the vulnerability.
2. **Oracle market cap < adjudicated volume.** UMA's market cap was approximately \$95M during the 2025–2026 dispute window; subsequent disputed markets (Zelenskyy-suit at \$237M) had volume larger than the oracle token's market cap. The economic cost of corrupting the vote is a fraction of the prize. This is a stable disequilibrium that worsens as the host platform scales.
3. **Voter–bettor identity overlap.** UMA tokenholders are not a separate pool from market participants; nothing prevents a whale from voting on a market they hold a position in. Classical oracle-defence assumptions (voter neutrality) are structurally absent.
4. **The resolution source itself is attackable off-chain.** When a market resolves on a real-world claim ("a deal was announced"), the resolution input — news headlines, official statements — can be manipulated upstream of the oracle.

T9.006 has **four structurally-distinct sub-patterns**, each anchored to one or more Polymarket-cohort worked examples at v0.1:

- **T9.006.001 — DVM vote capture by economically-interested holder.** Direct corruption of the oracle layer: a whale or coordinated tokenholder cohort with positions in the adjudicated market votes their holdings to drive resolution to their preferred outcome. Canonical: Ukraine mineral deal market (March 2025, ~\$7M, single whale ~25% of vote).
- **T9.006.002 — Resolution-spec ambiguity exploitation.** Attack on the spec layer: the natural-language claim is interpretively underdetermined; voters resolve against the bettor-majority's reasonable interpretation, citing spec ambiguity. Canonical: Zelenskyy-suit market (July 2025, ~\$237M).
- **T9.006.003 — Off-chain resolution-source coercion.** Attack on the resolution-input layer: a third-party off-chain reporter / journalist whose reporting is the resolution input is pressured (threats, extortion) post-publication to alter or suppress their account. Canonical: Times of Israel / Emanuel Fabian case, Iran-strike market (March 2026). **First publicly documented case of physical coercion of a human reporter as an oracle attack vector in crypto.**
- **T9.006.004 — Operational-insider trading on subjective-resolution prediction markets.** Attack on the **information-symmetry assumption between bettors**: a participant with classified-information access to (and operational involvement in) the underlying real-world event trades on that knowledge before public disclosure. The bettor is not just *informed* about the outcome — they are one of the *causal actors producing it*. Canonical: Van Dyke / Venezuela / Operation Absolute Resolve (DOJ + CFTC, January–April 2026); IDF reservist / Iran-strike timing (Tel Aviv District Court, February 2026).

T9.006 is **structurally distinct** from adjacent Techniques:

- **T9.001 (Oracle Price Manipulation)** — covers numeric price-feed oracle manipulation. Mechanical mitigations (TWAP, deviation halts, multi-venue feeds) apply. None apply to T9.006.
- **T9.003 (Governance Attack)** — adjacent at the DVM-vote-capture layer (T9.006.001) but distinct in that T9.003 attacks protocol-governance decisions over protocol parameters / treasury / upgrades, while T9.006 attacks oracle-resolution decisions over external real-world facts. The voting-pool-vs-prize-pool disequilibrium that makes T9.006 structurally exploitable is a property specific to subjective-oracle adjudication.

## Observed indicators

- Sharp resolution-window probability movements on the disputed market (e.g., YES surge from <10% to 100% in the resolution window) inconsistent with the underlying real-world signal.
- Voter–position correlation: UMA voters with material holdings in the adjudicated market, particularly whale-scale positions.
- Resolution-spec language with unmachine-checkable predicates (interpretive language, fashion / wardrobe claims, "credible reporting consensus"-class sources).
- Single-source resolution input where a single journalist / single publication is the dominant resolution input.
- Newly-created accounts with sharp longshot wins in narrow time windows on markets gated by classified events (the operational-insider sub-pattern).
- Public characterisations from the host platform (Polymarket, Augur, Kleros) that the resolution was "incorrect" / "unprecedented governance attack" / "captured" — the platform's own admissions are first-class detection signals at the cohort level.

## Detection signals

- **Pre-event (market-design layer):** flag markets with unmachine-checkable resolution criteria; flag markets whose volume exceeds a fraction (e.g., 50%) of the oracle token's market cap as elevated dispute risk; flag markets with single-source resolution inputs.
- **At-event (vote-capture layer, .001):** voter-position-correlation analysis across UMA / Kleros / Reality.eth / Augur REP votes; cohort-attribution of large UMA holders to bettor positions.
- **At-event (resolution-spec layer, .002):** divergence between media-consensus-implied resolution and oracle-resolution outcome.
- **At-event (off-chain coercion layer, .003):** monitoring of high-volume markets where reporting cohorts are narrow; bettor-to-resolution-source social-media interaction patterns; preserved-evidence threat-monitoring on identified resolution sources.
- **At-event (operational-insider layer, .004):** account-age-vs-first-bet correlation on markets gated by classified events; sharp longshot wins concentrated in newly-created accounts; KYC linkage at the off-ramp (Kraken / brokerage account) plus account-creation metadata; counter-intelligence cross-reference on operationally-involved actors.
- **Cohort-level baseline (operational-insider, .004):** ACDC analytics published baseline rates — longshots win ~14% baseline cohort rate, ~51.8% in military / defense markets — establishing what counts as anomaly.

## Mitigations

- **Market-design (highest-leverage):** machine-checkable resolution criteria. Markets resolving on cryptographically-verifiable inputs (block-height events, on-chain state, signed oracle attestations) are not exposed to T9.006.001 / T9.006.002. Markets resolving on language are.
- **Oracle-design:** oracle-weight floor relative to adjudicated volume. A UMA-class oracle should refuse to adjudicate markets whose volume exceeds some fraction of the oracle token's market cap. This is a structural change to UMA, not a Polymarket-side mitigation.
- **Voter-position-disclosure with conflict-of-interest slashing:** voters with positions in the adjudicated market should be excluded or slashable. UMA does not currently enforce this.
- **Resolution-source multiplicity:** markets requiring two or more independently-published sources for resolution, with no single human reporter's account being load-bearing. Market-design mitigation, not platform-design mitigation.
- **Bettor-position disclosure to potential resolution sources:** journalists whose reporting is cited in a resolution spec should be informed that their account may have economic influence. Operationally awkward but the only way to extend traditional journalistic threat-modelling into the crypto-prediction-market context.
- **Platform-level threat-monitoring of high-volume markets where reporting cohorts are narrow:** identify markets where a single journalist or single publication is the dominant resolution input; warrant heightened monitoring of bettor behaviour around the resolution window.
- **Operational-insider-detection:** account-age-vs-first-bet correlation on markets gated by classified events; sharp-longshot-win analysis; KYC linkage at the off-ramp; counter-intelligence cross-reference on operationally-involved actors. Polymarket / Chainalysis surveillance partnership operationalised this in April 2026.

## Discussion

T9.006 occupies a structurally distinct slot in OAK's T9 (Smart-Contract Exploit) Tactic. T9.001 (price-feed oracle manipulation — Mango Markets, BonqDAO, Vee Finance) is a *liquidity attack*: the attacker borrows or accumulates capital, moves a thin pool, and books the price reading. Mitigations are mechanical (TWAP, multi-venue feeds, deviation halts — OAK-M09). T9.006 has **no mechanical mitigation** because the oracle adjudicates language rather than numbers.

The four-sub-pattern split (.001 vote-capture / .002 spec-ambiguity / .003 off-chain-coercion / .004 operational-insider) is the analytically distinctive feature of the Technique. Each sub-pattern attacks a structurally different layer of the subjective-oracle stack:

- **.001** attacks the oracle layer (DVM vote)
- **.002** attacks the spec layer (natural-language interpretation)
- **.003** attacks the resolution-input layer (off-chain reporter)
- **.004** attacks the information-symmetry assumption between bettors (insider with classified knowledge of the underlying event)

The Van Dyke (.004) case is structurally novel because the bettor is one of the **causal actors producing the outcome** — this crosses from classical insider trading (informational asymmetry) into a class structurally adjacent to market-manipulation-via-action. The on-chain settlement substrate makes this attack class qualitatively different from traditional Section 10(b)/Rule 10b-5 insider trading: position and timing are publicly inspectable; wallet-attribution via off-ramp KYC is the load-bearing forensic step; geofencing is partial; the market itself surfaces the anomaly via account-age-vs-first-bet correlation.

**Note on T9.006.004 framing:** an earlier `TAXONOMY-GAPS.md` framing of T9.006.004 used a different sub-pattern (platform-override of oracle outcome — Barron Trump / DJT memecoin market). At v0.1 promotion the existing example anchors (Van Dyke; IDF reservist) self-categorise as T9.006.004 with the operational-insider definition; the platform-override sub-pattern lacks a worked-example anchor at v0.1 and is held as a v0.x candidate for a separate sub-Technique slot. Contributors should treat T9.006.004 as the operational-insider sub-pattern at v0.1 freeze and propose a separate ID for the platform-override sub-pattern at v0.x.

The **Polymarket cohort 2025–2026 is structurally the cohort-level anchor for T9.006**. The host platform (Polymarket on Polygon) plus the oracle layer (UMA DVM on Ethereum) is the substrate where all four sub-patterns are anchored at v0.1. Cross-platform analogues (Augur REP-token resolution, Kleros jurors, Reality.eth, Polymarket competitor venues) are pipeline anchor candidates for v0.x as those platforms scale to material volume.

The case generalises beyond crypto. Subjective-oracle prediction markets are economically equivalent to over-the-counter contingency contracts whose settlement criterion is a natural-language proposition. The same attack surface exists in any system where (a) the resolution input is non-numeric, (b) the resolution arbitrator is a token-weighted vote, and (c) the voting-pool economic weight is smaller than the adjudicated-market economic weight. T9.006 contributors should treat this as the structural framing and ensure the Technique's framing generalises beyond the Polymarket-cohort anchor.

Promotion to `stable` will follow once each sub-Technique carries multiple independent worked-example anchors. Currently .001 and .002 are anchored at the Polymarket-Ukraine and Polymarket-Zelenskyy cases respectively (with adjacent UFO-declassification and US-Iran ceasefire cases tracked as pipeline anchors); .003 is anchored at the Fabian / Iran-strike case alone; .004 is anchored at Van Dyke / Venezuela and IDF reservist / Iran-strike, establishing multi-jurisdictional confirmation but on a single host-platform substrate (Polymarket). The `emerging` classification at v0.1 reflects that the threat-class characterisation is mature and the per-incident anchor cohort is forming; `stable` will follow once each sub-pattern has multi-jurisdictional, multi-platform anchor coverage.
