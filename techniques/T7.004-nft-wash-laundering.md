# OAK-T7.004 — NFT Wash-Laundering

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T7 (Laundering)
**Maturity:** observed
**Chains:** EVM (primary; Ethereum / Polygon)
**First documented:** 2021 (early NFT-marketplace surge); systematic 2022 onward
**Aliases:** "NFT money laundering", "self-financed NFT trade", "wash-laundering through art"

## Description

A class of laundering flows in which an attacker uses self-financed NFT trades to convert proceeds into nominally-legitimate "art purchase" or "art sale" income at controlled valuations. The pattern is: attacker purchases an NFT from a self-controlled wallet at the desired-laundered value; the NFT may then be sold onward to a third party (rare) or retained as a "legitimately-acquired asset" (more common); the funds delivered to the seller side are now nominally-legitimate sales proceeds. From a defender's perspective, T7.004 is structurally distinct from OAK-T3.002 (Wash-Trade Volume Inflation) — even though the on-chain trade pattern is similar — because the *goal* differs: T3.002 inflates apparent volume to manipulate price discovery for downstream marks; T7.004 launders illicit proceeds by routing them through a self-financed art sale.

T7.004 is one of the lower-volume Techniques in the T7 family by aggregate dollar value: per `[chainalysis2022nft]`, NFT-specific money laundering was less than 1% of total crypto-wide money laundering in 2021, against ~$8.6B total crypto-wide money laundering monitored that year. However, the per-transaction value can be high (high-end NFT sales reach hundreds of thousands of dollars) and the social-legitimacy framing (an "art collector" rather than a "deposit address holder") creates compliance complexity that downstream venues have not consistently solved.

## Observed indicators

- A single user account selling NFTs to self-financed addresses many times (`[chainalysis2022nft]` documented 262 such users in 2021 with >25 self-financed sales each; 110 of them netted ~$8.9M in profit from the pattern).
- Per-marketplace wash-trade volume share: studies have documented platform-level wash-trade rates of ~94.5% on LooksRare and ~84.2% on X2Y2 in 2022-2023 — high rates that mix T3.002-style (platform-incentive-driven wash) and T7.004-style (laundering) patterns.
- Self-financed-cluster signature: source-of-funds analysis for the buyer-side wallet in an NFT trade, with attention to flows that originate from the seller-side cluster directly or indirectly.
- Outlier-valuation-against-collection-floor: NFT trades whose price is materially decoupled from contemporaneous floor-price for the same collection, executed between cluster-overlapping wallets.
- Counterfeit-or-low-liquidity collection signature: per `[chainalysisnftcounterfeit2022]`, T7.004 increasingly intersects with fake-mint / counterfeit collections (T12.002) where the laundering wallet self-mints and self-trades within a controlled collection, defeating floor-price comparison entirely.

## Detection signals

- Per-trade source-of-funds analysis: for high-value NFT trades, trace the buyer-side funding back N hops; alert on trades where the buyer-side funding originates from a cluster overlapping with the seller-side cluster.
- Per-cluster wash-trade rate: per the academic and industry methodology, compute the fraction of a user's NFT trades that involve a self-financed counterparty across a window. See OAK-M26 (wash-trade-rate metrics at marketplace layer).
- Goal-discrimination heuristic: distinguish T3.002 (price-discovery-targeting wash) from T7.004 (laundering wash) by the downstream behaviour — T3.002 typically aims to attract third-party buyers via inflated apparent activity; T7.004 typically does not need third-party buyers and the asset may be retained or further routed.
- Cross-reference with T7.001 / T7.002 / T7.003 cohorts: a wallet that exhibits T7.004 patterns *and* receives inflows from previously-mixer-routed clusters is a higher-confidence T7.004 candidate than one that exhibits the on-chain trade pattern alone.

## Real-world examples

- [`examples/2023-02-blur-airdrop-wash-cohort.md`](../examples/2023-02-blur-airdrop-wash-cohort.md) — Blur airdrop incentive-wash cohort (2022-10-19 through 2024). Anchors T7.004 in its **motive-ambiguous** framing: the on-chain trade pattern is structurally indistinguishable from laundering-motive wash-trades at the trace level even though the dominant cohort motive was airdrop-farming. The case is the canonical T3.002 / T7.004 motive-ambiguity reference and the cleanest illustration that marketplace-layer detection requires goal-discrimination heuristics rather than trade-pattern alone — primary classification T12.001, T7.004 referenced as adjacent.
- **Aggregate cohort — multi-marketplace — 2021** — Per `[chainalysis2022nft]`, 262 users were identified as selling NFTs to self-financed addresses more than 25 times in 2021; 110 of them collectively netted ~$8.9M in profit. The remaining 152 users in the cohort did not net profit on the wash-trades themselves, indicating the cohort spans both profit-seeking and pure-laundering motives. NFT-specific money laundering remained <1% of total crypto-wide laundering in 2021.
- **Platform-level wash-trade rates — LooksRare / X2Y2 — 2022-2023** — Industry research has documented platform-level wash-trade rates of ~94.5% (LooksRare) and ~84.2% (X2Y2) at peak — driven primarily by token-emission-farming incentive structures (T3.002 motive) but providing on-chain cover for T7.004 motive flows that mix into the same trace. Both marketplaces subsequently adjusted incentive structures.
- **CryptoPunks-class high-value wash trades — Ethereum — 2022** — Per `[theblock2022boredape]` and contemporaneous industry coverage, several high-value Bored Ape Yacht Club / CryptoPunks-class NFT trades during the 2022 NFT-bull-market peak exhibited self-financed counterparty signatures (cluster-overlap between buyer-side and seller-side wallets) at headline-grabbing valuations. The cohort-level evidence is documented; per-incident attribution is harder to establish than the price-discovery motive.
- **Blur farming as wash-trade-equivalent — Ethereum — 2023** — Per `[blurzeroroyalty2022]` and follow-up industry coverage, the 2023 Blur point-farming incentive structure produced on-chain trade patterns indistinguishable at the trace level from T7.004 wash-trades, even though the dominant motive was Blur-token-airdrop farming rather than laundering. The case is the canonical example of the T3.002 / T7.004 motive-ambiguity problem and the reason marketplace-layer detection requires goal-discrimination heuristics rather than trade-pattern alone.

## Mitigations

- **NFT marketplaces (highest-leverage):** integrate per-trade source-of-funds analysis at the marketplace layer; flag trades where buyer-side funding originates from the seller-side cluster; consider per-cluster wash-trade-rate caps as a marketplace-level policy. See OAK-M26 (wash-trade-rate metrics at marketplace layer).
- **Risk teams / regulated venues:** treat NFT-sale proceeds as elevated-risk for KYC-tier review, particularly for sellers whose wash-trade-rate exceeds a calibrated threshold. See OAK-M27 for the Travel Rule mapping.
- **Compliance providers:** publish per-marketplace wash-trade-rate metrics on a recurring cadence; the Chainalysis 2021 retrospective methodology is well-established and has been extended in subsequent industry research.

## Discussion

T7.004 is included in v0.1 partly as a **completeness move** for the T7 Tactic and partly because the T3.002-vs-T7.004 distinction is genuinely useful for defenders evaluating NFT marketplaces — the same on-chain pattern can serve two different operator goals, and the appropriate mitigation differs. As of v0.1, the cohort-scale metrics in `[chainalysis2022nft]` remain the canonical reference; per-incident named cases are sparse in the public forensic record because the laundering motive is harder to establish than the price-discovery-targeting motive.

**False-positive reasoning.** The on-chain trade pattern alone does not distinguish T7.004 from incentive-driven T3.002 farming or from legitimate same-collector intra-portfolio rebalancing. Effective T7.004 detection requires (i) source-of-funds attribution for the buyer-side wallet, (ii) goal-discrimination by downstream behaviour, and (iii) cross-reference with upstream T7.001 / T7.002 / T7.003 cohort attribution. Single-signal detection produces unworkable false-positive rates given the legitimate intra-collector trade volume.

**Post-OFAC substrate evolution.** As the T7.001 / T7.002 / T7.003 surfaces have attracted increasing compliance-provider attention, T7.004 has retained appeal precisely because the social-legitimacy framing of "art" creates KYC-tier ambiguity at downstream venues. v0.x deferred work: a per-incident named-case catalogue where the laundering motive is publicly established (rather than inferred), and a structured T3.002 / T7.004 motive-discrimination rubric usable at marketplace-layer screening.

This Technique sits at the intersection of OAK's main scope and the NFT-specific pillar called out in `TAXONOMY-GAPS.md`. As the NFT pillar expands in a v0.x update, T7.004 may be reframed alongside other NFT-specific Techniques; v0.1 includes it under T7 because the laundering frame is more operationally salient than the NFT-specific frame for this particular pattern.
