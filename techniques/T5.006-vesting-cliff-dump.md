# OAK-T5.006 — Vesting Cliff Dump

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T5 (Value Extraction)
**Maturity:** emerging
**Chains:** EVM, Solana, Cosmos, BNB Chain (any chain hosting time-locked allocation contracts)
**First documented:** 2021–2024 (industry retrospectives at token-unlock-tracker level)
**Aliases:** "cliff dump", "unlock dump", "investor unlock sell-pressure"

## Description

A class of OAK-T5 (Value Extraction) patterns in which team / early-investor / advisor / KOL allocations become liquid at a scheduled vesting cliff event, and the recipients coordinate large sales into the public market in a short window — converting illiquid allocation into liquid extraction. The contractual basis for the unlock is fully legitimate: the tokens were granted under a published vesting schedule, the cliff is reached, and the recipient exercises a transfer right that is theirs to exercise.

T5.006 is therefore **not** a contract-correctness failure (the smart contract behaves exactly as designed) and **not** an operator rug pull (no one is breaking any agreement). It is included in OAK because the *defensive question* that holders, market-makers, and treasury risk teams face is real even when no contract is violated: are the cliff-unlock outflows consistent with publicly-stated investor / team intent (gradual disposal, market-making participation, OTC placement), or are they consistent with coordinated front-of-window dumping into the public order book?

T5.006 is distinct from:

- **OAK-T3.003 (Coordinated Pump-and-Dump)** — T3.003 participants are anonymous coordinated speculators with no contractual claim on the asset; T5.006 participants are identifiable team / investor recipients exercising contractually-legitimate authority.
- **OAK-T5.005 (Treasury-Management Exit)** — T5.005 assets are treasury-multisig-controlled and the contractual basis is what is under scrutiny; T5.006 assets are individually-allocated tokens whose contractual basis is intact, and the scrutiny is on coordination of disposal across recipients.
- **OAK-T5.001 / T5.002 (LP Drains)** — LP-drain patterns concern pool-side liquidity removal by an LP-control cluster; T5.006 concerns sell-side flow from non-LP recipient wallets into the public market.

## Observed indicators

- **Per-cliff outflow concentration** — a substantial fraction of recipient wallets (team, investor, advisor cohorts) move tokens off allocation contracts into exchange-deposit clusters within a short window of the cliff timestamp (hours to days).
- **Pre-cliff price action** — sustained price weakness in the days leading up to a publicly-known cliff date, consistent with informed market-makers / hedgers anticipating sell pressure; in some cases accompanied by perp-market open-interest skew or borrow-rate spikes for the asset.
- **Pre-cliff insider OTC placement** — a "selling-while-vesting" pattern in which recipients route allocations through OTC desks before the cliff (via assignable claims, structured forwards, or off-chain agreements settling on cliff), so that the on-cliff transfer is from recipient to OTC counterparty rather than to exchange. Visible on-chain only as unusual recipient-cluster outflow shapes.
- **Cohort coordination** — clustered destination addresses (same exchange, same OTC desk, same market-maker), or clustered timing (same block range), across nominally-independent investor cohorts.
- **Inconsistency with public commitments** — outflow profile materially diverges from what the project / lead investors stated publicly about disposal intent (e.g., "long-term aligned", "gradual disposal over 12+ months", "no sales planned at cliff").

## Detection signals

- **Per-token unlock-tracker integration** — ingest cliff calendars from token-unlock-trackers (TokenUnlocks / Tokenomist, CryptoRank, DefiLlama Unlocks, DropsTab) as scheduled-event input; pre-position monitoring on each known cliff for tokens in scope.
- **Per-recipient outflow monitoring** — for each known allocation contract, enumerate recipient cluster addresses; monitor outflow from those clusters within a window around the cliff (typically T-7d to T+30d) and label by destination class (CEX deposit, OTC desk, on-chain DEX swap, internal transfer).
- **Cliff-window concentration metric** — per cliff event, compute the share of unlocked supply that reaches exchange-deposit or DEX-swap destinations within the first N days; compare against the share that remains in recipient custody.
- **Pre-cliff price / derivatives signal** — track spot price drift, perp open-interest skew, and borrow-rate moves in the T-14d window before each scheduled cliff for tokens above a market-cap / liquidity threshold; persistent pre-cliff weakness across cliffs is itself a defensive signal.
- **Cohort clustering** — cluster recipient outflows by destination-address overlap, timing overlap, and routing-pattern overlap; flag clusters whose coordination shape exceeds what would be expected from independent disposal decisions.
- **Public-statement reconciliation** — text-extract investor / team public statements about disposal intent; flag cliff-window behaviour that materially diverges.

## Real-world examples

- [`examples/2021-05-dfinity-icp-vesting-cliff-dump.md`](../examples/2021-05-dfinity-icp-vesting-cliff-dump.md) — DFINITY / ICP Genesis vesting-cliff cohort, May 2021. ICP price declined \~95% from the May 2021 listing peak (\~\$630 → \~\$30) across the first vesting-cliff year; the T5.006-relevant surface is the multi-recipient-cohort cliff unlock structure (seed / strategic / team allocations with monthly cliff-unlock windows per Tokenomist data).
- [`examples/2024-03-arbitrum-arb-vesting-cliff-dump.md`](../examples/2024-03-arbitrum-arb-vesting-cliff-dump.md) — Arbitrum ARB team-and-investor vesting-cliff dump, March 2024. \~1.1B ARB (\~$2.2B) unlocked at the March 16, 2024 cliff; ARB declined \~41% in the first month post-cliff and \~63% through August 2024. The highest-dollar-volume T5.006 event at v0.1 and the clearest case of cliff-concentrated sell-pressure coexisting with legitimate governance and protocol operations.
- [`examples/2024-02-dydx-vesting-unlock-dump.md`](../examples/2024-02-dydx-vesting-unlock-dump.md) — dYdX DYDX vesting-cliff unlock, February 2024. ~150M DYDX (~$430M) unlocked at the February 1, 2024 cliff; DYDX declined ~48% from pre-cliff over Q1 2024. Structurally informative because the cliff dump occurred concurrently with dYdX's v4 migration to a Cosmos app-chain — legitimate protocol operations and cliff-dump sell-pressure coexisting.

The pattern is well-characterised at the **token-unlock-tracker cohort level** rather than as named single incidents. Cohort framing is the canonical reference for v0.1: TokenUnlocks / Tokenomist, CryptoRank, DefiLlama Unlocks, and DropsTab each publish, for hundreds of post-2021-launch tokens, the scheduled cliff calendar plus retrospective price action around each cliff. Industry retrospectives at Chainalysis cover the broader market-manipulation context within which cliff-dump behaviour sits.

Many post-2021 token launches have documented vesting-cliff-dump patterns: investor-cliff unlocks correlated with sharp price drops, and pre-cliff insider OTC-sale ("selling-while-vesting") cases are visible in the cohort. As of v0.1, OAK does not name single incidents — the unlock-tracker datasets are the canonical reference, and contributors with public forensic write-ups of specific named cliff-dump cases are encouraged to submit them via PR. ## Mitigations

- **Project / token-issuer side:**
  - **Longer / smoother vesting curves** — replace large cliff unlocks with linear or near-linear release schedules that distribute sell-pressure across a wider time window.
  - **Gradual cliff release** — where a cliff is required (e.g., for tax / contractual reasons), split it into multiple smaller cliffs over weeks rather than a single large unlock event.
  - **Vesting-cliff disclosure standards** — publish, per cohort, the disposal intent at cliff (sell / hold / OTC); commit to disclosure of cliff-window net-flow ex-post. A clear stated intent is the precondition that lets defenders distinguish coordinated dumping from disclosed-and-expected disposal.
- **Listing-venue / launchpad side:**
  - Require vesting-cliff disclosure (full schedule, recipient cohorts, intent statement) as a listing precondition.
  - Require cliff-window post-disclosure (actual recipient-cluster outflow vs stated intent) as a listing-maintenance condition.
- **Trader / risk-team side:**
  - Pre-position around scheduled cliffs for any token in active book; size exposure relative to cliff calendar and historical cliff-window concentration metrics.
  - Treat materially-divergent cliff-window behaviour vs stated intent as a standalone risk signal independent of any single cliff's price outcome.
- **Detection-engineering side:**
  - Wire token-unlock-tracker calendars as scheduled-event input rather than treating each cliff as a surprise.
  - Build per-recipient-cluster outflow baselines so cliff-window concentration is measurable against the cohort's normal disposal cadence.

**Canonical mitigations:** OAK-M17, OAK-M25, OAK-M28.

## Discussion

T5.006 is the first OAK Technique whose underlying activity is **fully contractually legitimate**. It is included because OAK is a defender-side framework, and the defender's question is empirical (what is the cliff-window outflow shape?) rather than normative (is the unlock allowed?). Holders, market-makers, and treasury risk teams who treat every scheduled cliff as a neutral event end up systematically on the wrong side of cliff-window flow; documenting the pattern lets defenders pre-position rather than react.

OAK takes no partisan position on whether a given recipient's disposal at a legitimate cliff is "wrong". The framework documents (a) the defensive question, (b) the indicators and signals that let it be answered for any given cliff, and (c) the disclosure-standard mitigations that let issuers and listing venues reduce ambiguity in advance. v0.x evolution should add per-recipient-cluster outflow detectors and a disclosure-standard schema (machine-readable cliff disclosure + post-cliff reconciliation) so that "stated disposal intent" can be checked against on-chain reality without per-token manual analysis.

A v0.x split is plausible into (a) **on-cliff dumping** (recipient → exchange / DEX in cliff window) and (b) **pre-cliff OTC pre-placement** ("selling-while-vesting" patterns visible only as recipient-side flow shape) — the two have meaningfully different detection methodologies and warrant separate sub-Techniques once the data supports calibration.
