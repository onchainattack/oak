# OAK-T13.001.004 — Paymaster Griefing

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T13 (Account Abstraction Attacks)
**Maturity:** observed
**Chains:** EVM (ERC-4337 EntryPoint v0.6 / v0.7 / v0.8 / v0.9 deployments); EVM L2s with native account abstraction
**First documented:** 2023 (EntryPoint v0.7 added the unused-gas-penalty mechanism specifically to address paymaster-DoS surface); 2026 (EntryPoint v0.9 closed the temporary-revert griefing vector)
**Aliases:** "paymaster DoS", "paymaster balance griefing", "postOp griefing", "unused-gas-penalty abuse", "bundler-reputation griefing"

## Description

The attacker submits UserOps designed to drain the paymaster's deposited balance without producing useful protocol activity. The attack does not redirect funds to the attacker — its goal is cost-imposition on the paymaster, typically as a competitor-harm or platform-disruption operation, or as a prelude to forcing the paymaster offline.

Worked sub-patterns:

- **(i) Unused-gas-penalty abuse.** Artificially-high gas-limit submission to trigger an unused-gas-penalty path the paymaster did not budget for. The v0.7 "unused gas penalty" mechanism was itself added to address paymaster-DoS surface, and an early implementation neglected to include the penalty in the value passed to `postOp`, causing the paymaster to charge users less than the gas it actually had to pay `[erc4337spec]`.
- **(ii) Temporary-revert griefing.** A temporary "must-revert" condition in a target contract forces a victim's correctly-signed UserOp to execute while the condition is active — the UserOp validates normally, but the inner call reverts and the paymaster still pays the gas. The EntryPoint v0.9 griefing-vector fix in 2026 addressed exactly this class `[projecteleven2026v09]`.
- **(iii) Bundle-level griefing.** A single malicious UserOp causes `validatePaymasterUserOp` to fail for the rest of the bundle, exhausting the paymaster's bundler-reputation budget and causing the bundler to throttle the paymaster `[ozaa4337audit]`.

T13.001.004 is part of the OAK-T13.001 (Paymaster Compromise) umbrella; see the parent for the architectural distinction from T9.

## Observed indicators

- Paymaster deposited balance trends downward without a matching trend in the protocol activity the paymaster was deployed to sponsor (griefing signature).
- A spike in sponsored UserOps with high `verificationGasLimit` / `callGasLimit` / `paymasterPostOpGasLimit` values that consume gas without producing meaningful protocol effect.
- Sponsored UserOps whose inner call consistently reverts in a target contract that exhibits temporary "must-revert" conditions (e.g., a guard that toggles based on attacker-controlled state).
- The paymaster's reputation score at major bundlers (Pimlico, Stackup, Alchemy, Biconomy) being throttled without a corresponding spike in legitimate UserOp volume.

## Detection signals

- **Paymaster-fund-balance monitoring.** Runtime metric: paymaster's deposited balance at the EntryPoint, sampled per block, with alerts on drawdown rate exceeding the paymaster's expected sponsorship-throughput envelope. A persistent griefing campaign produces a drawdown signature distinguishable from organic sponsorship activity.
- **EntryPoint-version pinning.** Confirm the paymaster runs against an EntryPoint version that closes documented griefing vectors: ≥v0.7 for the unused-gas-penalty mechanism; ≥v0.9 for the temporary-revert griefing fix.
- **Bundle-level UserOp inspection.** Log per-UserOp the gas limits, the inner-call outcome (success / revert), and the gas charged vs gas paid; a population of high-gas-limit UserOps with reverting inner calls is a (i) and (ii) signature.
- **Bundler-reputation interaction monitoring.** Monitor whether the paymaster's reputation score at any major bundler is being throttled; throttling without a corresponding spike in legitimate UserOp volume is a (iii) sub-case signal `[ozaa4337audit]`.
- **Per-UserOp policy-rule audit.** Reconcile the per-sponsored-UserOp policy-rule decision against the paymaster's deposited-balance trajectory; a balance-drawdown that exceeds the policy's expected sponsorship cost is a griefing signal even when each individual UserOp's policy-rule match is correct.

## Real-world examples

- **EntryPoint v0.9 griefing-vector fix — 2026-03 (canonical sub-case (ii) anchor)** — EntryPoint versions before v0.9 allowed an attacker to create a temporary "must revert" condition in a target contract, then submit a victim's correctly-signed UserOp that validated normally but reverted on inner execution; the paymaster was charged for the gas regardless. v0.9 closes this griefing class. The fix is the canonical worked example for the temporary-revert sub-case at the EntryPoint layer `[projecteleven2026v09]`.
- **EntryPoint v0.7 unused-gas-penalty fix — 2024 (canonical sub-case (i) anchor)** — The v0.7 unused-gas-penalty mechanism was added specifically to address paymaster-DoS surface; an early implementation neglected to include the penalty in the value passed to `postOp`, causing paymasters to undercharge users for high-gas-limit UserOps. The fix is the canonical worked example for the unused-gas-penalty sub-case `[erc4337spec]`.
- **OpenZeppelin EIP-4337 bundler-throttling griefing finding — 2023-2024 (canonical sub-case (iii) anchor)** — OpenZeppelin's Ethereum-Foundation engagement on EIP-4337 surfaced bundler-throttling griefing as one of the 7+ high-severity findings; a single malicious UserOp could exhaust a paymaster's bundler-reputation budget `[ozaa4337audit]`.
- **Cohort framing — 2023 onward** — Each named incident is an audit-disclosed surface or an EntryPoint-version-fix anchor rather than a public production loss; the class is documented in the EntryPoint specification and bundler-vendor literature `[osecpaymasters2025]`, `[tobsixmistakes2026]`. Contributors with public forensic write-ups of named paymaster-griefing incidents are encouraged to submit them via PR.

## Reference implementations

- `mg-detectors-rs` — coverage **gap** at v0.1.
- Pre-deployment vendor coverage: ERC-4337 specialist audit firms (see umbrella T13.001).
- Runtime / operational coverage: bundler-vendor reputation systems (Pimlico, Stackup, Alchemy, Biconomy) provide partial coverage for sub-case (iii) by throttling paymasters whose validation behaviour exceeds reputation budgets; deployed-contract monitoring (Forta, OpenZeppelin Defender, BlockSec PhalconHQ) can be tuned for paymaster balance-drawdown signatures.

## Mitigations

- **Paymaster operators:** pin the EntryPoint version (v0.7+ for the unused-gas-penalty fix; v0.9+ for the temporary-revert griefing fix); enforce per-call gas caps at the validation boundary, per-user quotas, per-application allowlists; monitor deposited-balance drawdown against expected sponsorship throughput; track bundler-reputation score at every major bundler and investigate any throttling not explained by legitimate UserOp volume; where economically feasible, run the paymaster behind a circuit-breaker that pauses sponsorship if drawdown rate exceeds a threshold.
- **Paymaster architects:** validate that gas-limit fields are bounded at the validation boundary; design `validatePaymasterUserOp` to reject UserOps whose inner-call effect is not constrained to the sponsorship policy's intended surface; design `postOp` to charge for unused-gas penalty correctly (do not rely on EntryPoint-version-pinning alone if the contract is intended to be portable across versions).
- **Risk teams:** when reviewing a new paymaster integration, require (1) the EntryPoint version pinning statement and the upgrade plan for v0.9+, (2) the per-call gas cap / per-user quota / per-application allowlist documentation, (3) the deposited-balance monitoring posture and circuit-breaker threshold, (4) the bundler-reputation tracking posture; treat a paymaster that cannot supply (1)–(4) as a standing T13.001.004 surface.

## Discussion

T13.001.004 is the sub-Technique whose mitigation depends most directly on protocol-version-keeping discipline: each EntryPoint version (v0.6 → v0.7 → v0.8 → v0.9) closes a documented griefing surface, and a paymaster pinned to an outdated version inherits that version's griefing exposure. Operationally this means T13.001.004 mitigation is a recurring task tied to ERC-4337 evolution rather than a one-time design audit.

The bundler-reputation interaction makes T13.001.004 partly an off-chain control: even a structurally well-designed paymaster can be throttled out of useful service by sustained sub-case (iii) griefing. Bundler-vendor coordination on griefing-pattern reporting and shared reputation reset semantics is an open coordination problem in the ecosystem at v0.1.

Sub-case (ii) — temporary-revert griefing — is interesting because it weaponises legitimate UserOps. The victim's UserOp is correctly signed, correctly authorised, and correctly evaluated by the policy engine; the griefing happens at the inner-call layer that the paymaster's policy engine does not necessarily inspect. EntryPoint v0.9's fix is the protocol-layer answer; paymaster-side defence-in-depth requires either inner-call effect analysis at validation time (expensive) or aggressive balance-drawdown circuit breakers (operationally simpler).
