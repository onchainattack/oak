# OAK-T3.001 — Sybil-Bundled Launch

**Parent Tactics:** OAK-T3 (Holder Capture)
**Maturity:** stable
**Chains:** EVM, Solana
**First documented:** 2024–2025 (industry observation; Liu et al. 2025 covers the related airdrop-sybil case with transferable methodology)
**Aliases:** "bundled buys", "atomic launch", "sniper bundle", "bundler-cluster launch"

## Description

A launch in which a set of nominally independent buyer addresses, all funded from a common upstream source, acquire a large share of the initial supply within the first blocks/slot of trading. From a defender's perspective, the signal is the **funder graph**, not the individual addresses — the Technique relies on the appearance of distinct holders, and that appearance is broken by tracing funding sources.

Two operationally distinct sub-patterns share the T3.001 artefact:

- **Launch-time bundling** — deployer-funded wallets buy atomically in the same block/slot as pool creation (Solana Jito-bundle and EVM private-mempool variants).
- **Airdrop / incentive sybil** — operator-funded wallets generate qualifying activity over weeks/months to capture per-address airdrop allocations. The on-chain signature is the same funder-graph pattern; the goal differs.

OAK keeps both under T3.001 at v0.1 because the detection surface (subgraph feature propagation per `[liu2025sybil]`) transfers across the two sub-patterns. v0.x may split airdrop-sybil into its own technique once a clean discriminator (qualification-period activity vs single-block buy) is published.

## Observed indicators

- Multiple early-buyer addresses funded from a common upstream wallet within depth-N hops (typically N=2–4) and within a short pre-launch window (hours to days).
- Acquisition transactions clustered in the first block(s) or slot(s) of trading; on Solana, multiple buys within the same Jito bundle alongside the pool-creation instruction.
- Combined supply share controlled by the cluster substantially above what organic launch dynamics would produce (>30% in the first block is a strong signal; pump.fun bundlers commonly exceed 50%).
- Same-counterparty repeat patterns across multiple unrelated tokens deployed by the same funder cluster (cross-incident T8.001 link).
- For airdrop-sybil: identical action sequences (same swaps, same protocols, same order, near-identical amounts) across many wallets within short windows.

## Detection signals

- **Funder-graph cluster query (Dune-style):** for each early-buyer of a token, walk back N hops on the native-token transfer graph; group buyers sharing a common funder-ancestor; flag groups whose combined supply share at block T+k exceeds threshold.
  - Example skeleton: `count(distinct buyer) where funder_ancestor_depth_3 = X and first_buy_block <= launch_block + 1`
- **Temporal density:** density of acquisition transactions in the first N blocks/slots after pool creation; on Solana, count buy-instructions sharing the same Jito bundle as `initialize_pool`.
- **Cross-token recurrence (T8.001 link):** maintain a per-funder index; alert when a known funder-cluster appears upstream of a new launch.
- **Forta-agent triggers:** subscribe to DEX `PoolCreated` / `addLiquidity`; on emission, fetch first-N-block buyers, compute funder-ancestor signature, post to alert sink if depth-2 same-parent ratio > calibrated threshold.

## Mitigations

- **Traders / risk teams:** weight early-buyer concentration in entry decision; treat clusters above threshold as a hard skip. See OAK-M04 (Funder-Graph Clustering).
- **Launchpads:** apply per-source acquisition caps in the first N blocks; publish post-launch concentration reports. See OAK-M05 (Authority-Graph Enumeration) for the related authority-side check.
- **Airdrop issuers:** publish sybil-filter methodology and exclusion thresholds before snapshot; treat issuer-side sybil filtering as a load-bearing pre-distribution control.

## Discussion

Funder-graph depth is a calibration parameter — too shallow misses indirect funding paths, too deep produces false positives (e.g., a popular CEX hot wallet appears as a common ancestor for unrelated retail buyers). v0.2 should publish reference depth/threshold combinations per chain and per funding-pattern class.

The launch-bundling vs airdrop-sybil split is a candidate v0.x sub-Technique decomposition (T3.001.001 / T3.001.002). At v0.1 the unified framing is justified because (a) both detection signatures share the funder-graph artefact and (b) the same operator clusters are observed running both patterns. False-positive control: CEX-funded retail clusters look superficially similar to bundler clusters; the discriminator is intra-cluster timing variance — bundler clusters fund within minutes and buy within seconds, retail CEX clusters fund and buy across days with high temporal variance.
