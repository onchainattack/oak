# Upbit exchange hot-wallet drain — Ethereum — 2019-11-27

**Loss:** 342,000 ETH (\~\$50M USD at-the-time, with the at-current-price valuation rising substantially across 2020–2025 as ETH appreciated; the 342,000-ETH-denominated number is the load-bearing figure for OAK's purposes because it is the figure on which the South Korean police investigation, the 2024 US-extradition request, and the on-chain laundering-tail tracing through 2020–2024 all rest). The loss represented the bulk of Upbit's Ethereum hot-wallet float at the moment of the event. **Recovery:** mixed and partial. (1) **Operator-funded full customer restitution**: Upbit (operated by Dunamu Inc.) committed publicly to repaying all affected customer-asset balances in full from corporate reserves on the day of the event; user-side restitution was completed within the months following the breach without affecting customer-balance availability. (2) **On-chain forensic tail (long)**: South Korean police, FBI, and industry-forensic analysts (Chainalysis, Elliptic, Whale Alert, and adjacent tracing providers) tracked the stolen ETH across multiple cross-chain hops, mixer routing through Tornado Cash and Sinbad-cluster addresses, and intermediary-exchange laundering surfaces across 2019–2024. (3) **South Korean police and Justice Ministry confirmed-attribution finding**: the South Korean National Police Agency (NPA) Cyber Bureau publicly confirmed the case as the work of North Korean state-aligned actors (Lazarus / Andariel cluster of OAK-G01) in November 2024 — one of the first South Korean-government-level confirmed-attribution findings against a Lazarus-cluster cryptocurrency-theft case at exchange scale. (4) **US extradition request (2024)**: per concurrent reporting in November 2024, the United States issued an extradition request for North Korean operators implicated in the Upbit case as part of the broader US-government effort to dismantle the OAK-G01 / TraderTraitor cluster's cryptocurrency-theft infrastructure. **OAK Techniques observed:** **OAK-T11 broadly construed (custody-side compromise — operator-side hot-wallet key compromise)** producing a single concentrated authorised withdrawal of 342,000 ETH from Upbit's Ethereum hot wallet to an attacker-controlled address. Adjacent: **OAK-T7.001 (Mixer-Routed Hop)** for the long-tail laundering activity through Tornado Cash and Sinbad-cluster routing across 2019–2024. **Attribution:** **confirmed** — South Korean NPA Cyber Bureau and Justice Ministry public confirmation in November 2024 attributing the case to the OAK-G01 / Lazarus / Andariel cluster, plus the concurrent US-extradition request for named DPRK operators. The Upbit case is one of the first South Korean-government-level confirmed-attribution findings against a Lazarus-cluster cryptocurrency-theft case at exchange scale, and is the canonical pre-2020 OAK-G01 case with a confirmed-attribution disposition at the OAK v0.1 cutoff.

## Summary

Upbit, operated by Dunamu Inc., was at the time of the event the largest cryptocurrency exchange in South Korea by trading volume, headquartered in Seoul, with a continuing operating-licence position under the South Korean exchange-regulatory regime. On 2019-11-27 (KST) Upbit's Ethereum hot-wallet infrastructure suffered a single concentrated authorised withdrawal of 342,000 ETH — approximately \$50M USD at-the-time — to an attacker-controlled address. Upbit detected the activity rapidly (the public-disclosure timeline indicates same-day detection) and announced the breach publicly on 2019-11-27, suspending all deposit-and-withdrawal operations and committing to full customer-asset-balance restitution from corporate reserves.

The proximate technical mechanism per public-record analysis was operator-side compromise of Upbit's Ethereum hot-wallet key material with authority to authorise the single 342,000-ETH withdrawal. The exact entry vector was not publicly disclosed by Upbit / Dunamu in technical detail at the OAK v0.1 cutoff; the public-record analysis is consistent with the canonical 2018–2019 OAK-G01 / TraderTraitor entry-vector pattern of social-engineering-led malware delivery against operator endpoints, with lateral movement to wallet-management infrastructure. The single-concentrated-withdrawal shape of the extraction (in contrast to the multi-asset distributed extractions characteristic of Coinrail 2018 and DragonEx 2019, or the multi-week sustained-drain shape characteristic of Cryptopia 2019) is consistent with an attacker who reached signing authority over the Ethereum hot-wallet inventory and chose to consolidate the full available balance into a single transaction at the moment of compromise.

Upbit's response is the case's most operationally distinctive feature for OAK's recovery-mechanism documentation. Within hours of detection, Upbit / Dunamu committed to full customer-asset-balance restitution from corporate reserves, and customer-side restitution was completed across the months following the breach without affecting customer-balance availability. The Dunamu corporate balance-sheet position (the parent company is one of the largest South Korean financial-services unicorns, with substantial corporate reserves and continuing exchange-revenue capacity) was sufficient to absorb the loss without resorting to insolvency, customer-balance haircuts, tokenised-restitution mechanisms, or external-investor capital-injection — making the case the canonical 2019-era reference for the operator-funded full-restitution-from-reserves recovery mechanism at exchange scale.

The on-chain laundering tail is the case's structurally distinctive feature for OAK's T7-axis documentation. South Korean police, FBI, and industry-forensic analysts (Chainalysis, Elliptic, Whale Alert) tracked the stolen ETH across multiple cross-chain hops, mixer routing through Tornado Cash and Sinbad-cluster addresses, and intermediary-exchange laundering surfaces across 2019–2024 — a five-year laundering tail that produced the cumulative on-chain forensic record on which the November 2024 South Korean confirmed-attribution finding and the concurrent US-extradition request rest. Contributors writing T7-class laundering Technique pages should treat the Upbit case as the canonical 2019-era reference for the long-tail-on-chain-laundering-tail surface that subsequently became the load-bearing laundering pattern across the post-2020 OAK-G01 cohort.

For OAK's purposes the Upbit 2019 case is the canonical reference for three structurally distinctive features. First, it is the canonical pre-2020 OAK-G01 worked example with a confirmed-attribution disposition on the OAK record, anchoring the 2017–2019 OAK-G01 cohort on the attribution-strength axis. Second, it is the canonical 2019-era reference for the *operator-funded full-restitution-from-reserves* recovery mechanism at exchange scale, with the corporate balance-sheet position of the parent operator (Dunamu) sufficient to absorb the loss without resorting to alternative restitution mechanisms. Third, it is the canonical 2019-era reference for the long-tail-on-chain-laundering-tail surface, with a five-year laundering tail traced through Tornado Cash, Sinbad-cluster routing, and intermediary-exchange surfaces across 2019–2024.

## What defenders observed and learned

- **Pre-event:** the load-bearing failure was operator-side compromise of Ethereum hot-wallet signing authority sufficient to authorise a single 342,000-ETH withdrawal — meaning that an attacker who reached the signing surface had unrestricted capacity to consolidate the full available hot-wallet inventory into a single transaction. The defender lesson is that *velocity / volume / concentration anomaly thresholds on hot-wallet egress* are a load-bearing control surface; the post-2020 industry baseline of (1) per-transaction concentration thresholds calibrated against typical individual-withdrawal magnitudes, (2) aggregate-velocity thresholds calibrated against typical hour-or-day egress rates, (3) out-of-band approval workflows triggered by either threshold breach, and (4) just-in-time / break-glass elevation rather than persistent operator access to wallet-management infrastructure is partly retro-engineered against the single-concentrated-withdrawal extraction shape that Upbit 2019 demonstrates.
- **At-event (detection — fast, but not preventive):** detection occurred within hours of the extraction event, and operational response (deposit-and-withdrawal suspension, public disclosure, restitution commitment) followed rapidly. The post-event detection latency was substantially better than the multi-day Zaif 2018 detection latency or the multi-week Cryptopia 2019 sustained-drain detection latency — reflecting the post-Coincheck 2018 industry baseline of continuous-balance-reconciliation controls maturing into operational practice across the 2018–2019 East-Asian-exchange peer surface. But same-day detection did not prevent the loss; the loss was prevented only by the absent control surface (velocity / volume / concentration thresholds and out-of-band approval workflows on hot-wallet egress) that would have stopped the extraction at the moment of authorisation rather than detecting it after.
- **Post-event (recovery — operator-funded full restitution from corporate reserves):** Dunamu's corporate-reserve-funded full customer-asset-balance restitution is the case's most operationally distinctive feature for OAK's recovery-mechanism documentation. The mechanism — operator commits to full restitution from corporate reserves on the day of the event, customer-balance availability is not affected, restitution is completed across the months following the breach — is the cleanest available 2019-era reference for the operator-funded full-restitution-from-reserves pattern at exchange scale, alongside the smaller-magnitude operator-funded full-restitution patterns at NiceHash 2017 (operator-funded from continuing earnings rather than from corporate reserves) and at the corporate-and-owner-funded Coincheck 2018 mechanism.
- **Post-event (on-chain forensic tail — five-year laundering tail):** the stolen ETH's long-tail laundering activity through Tornado Cash, Sinbad-cluster routing, and intermediary-exchange surfaces across 2019–2024 produced the cumulative on-chain forensic record on which the November 2024 South Korean confirmed-attribution finding and the concurrent US-extradition request rest. The defender lesson is that patient on-chain forensics across multi-year horizons — measured in years rather than days — is itself a defender capability, and that the post-2020 industry baseline of standing inter-tracing-provider laundering-cluster maintenance (Chainalysis, Elliptic, TRM Labs, Whale Alert, and adjacent providers) produces the cumulative cluster-level intelligence that supports law-enforcement attribution dispositions across multi-year windows. The Upbit case is one of the cleanest available 2019-era references for this pattern, alongside the comparable long-tail forensic surface on the Bitfinex 2016 case (which produced the February 2022 DOJ arrests at the six-year horizon).
- **Post-event (attribution disposition — confirmed):** the November 2024 South Korean NPA Cyber Bureau and Justice Ministry public confirmation, plus the concurrent US-extradition request, produce a `confirmed` attribution disposition on the OAK record. The case is one of the first South Korean-government-level confirmed-attribution findings against a Lazarus-cluster cryptocurrency-theft case at exchange scale, and is the canonical pre-2020 OAK-G01 case with a confirmed-attribution disposition at the OAK v0.1 cutoff. Contributors writing the OAK-G01 actor page should treat Upbit 2019 → 2024 disposition as the load-bearing pre-2020 confirmed-attribution anchor for the cluster.

## Discussion

Upbit 2019 is the OAK record's canonical pre-2020 OAK-G01 worked example with a confirmed-attribution disposition, anchoring the 2017–2019 OAK-G01 cohort on the attribution-strength axis. The case is structurally distinctive on three axes: the single-concentrated-withdrawal extraction shape (distinct from the multi-asset distributed and multi-week sustained-drain shapes that characterise other 2018–2019 cohort members); the operator-funded full-restitution-from-reserves recovery mechanism (distinct from the four canonical exchange-recovery patterns OAK previously documented and from the Zaif 2018 external-investor-capital-injection pattern); and the five-year-on-chain-laundering-tail (the canonical 2019-era reference for the long-tail laundering surface that subsequently became the load-bearing laundering pattern across the post-2020 OAK-G01 cohort).

The November 2024 South Korean attribution disposition is the case's structurally significant feature for OAK's attribution-axis documentation. The South Korean NPA Cyber Bureau and Justice Ministry public confirmation, plus the concurrent US-extradition request, produce a `confirmed` attribution disposition that anchors the 2017–2019 OAK-G01 cohort on the attribution-strength axis. The case is one of the first South Korean-government-level confirmed-attribution findings against a Lazarus-cluster cryptocurrency-theft case at exchange scale, and contributors writing the OAK-G01 actor page should treat Upbit 2019 → 2024 disposition as the load-bearing pre-2020 confirmed-attribution anchor for the cluster. The five-year delay between the event (2019-11-27) and the confirmed-attribution disposition (2024-11) is itself an analytically significant feature of the case, and is consistent with the Bitfinex 2016 → February 2022 six-year-horizon disposition pattern as canonical references for the multi-year horizon on which exchange-scale confirmed-attribution dispositions actually run.

The operator-funded full-restitution-from-reserves recovery mechanism is the case's structurally distinctive feature on the recovery-mechanism axis. Dunamu's corporate-reserve-funded full customer-asset-balance restitution — operator commits to full restitution on the day of the event, customer-balance availability is not affected, restitution completed across the months following the breach — is the cleanest available 2019-era reference for the operator-funded full-restitution-from-reserves pattern at exchange scale.
