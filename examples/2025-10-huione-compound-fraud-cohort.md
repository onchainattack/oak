# Huione Group / Prince Group Southeast Asia compound-operated investment-fraud cohort — multi-chain (BTC / ETH / USDT on Tron dominant) — 2020–2025

**Loss:** $15B Bitcoin forfeiture (Chen Zhi / Prince Group — DOJ October 2025, the largest single Bitcoin forfeiture in U.S. history); Ly Yong Phat / O-Smach Resort OFAC-designated September 2024; Philippines compound $225M USDT seizure June 2025; Huione Group laundering-infrastructure throughput estimated in tens of billions across 2020–2025 by FinCEN section 311 final rule October 2025. Cumulative aggregate victim losses across the Southeast Asian compound ecosystem are the single largest retail-victim loss class in crypto — exceeding all bridge exploits, lending-protocol hacks, and custody-exchange hacks combined in retail-victim-count terms.

**OAK Techniques observed:** **OAK-T11.005.003** (Compound-Operated Investment-Fraud Platforms — primary; the industrial-scale distribution of T11.005.001 through forced-labour scam compounds in Southeast Asia). **OAK-T11.005** (Operator-side Fake-Platform Fraud — parent class; the compound structure co-locates multiple T11.005.001 fake-CEX platforms, T11.005.002 fake-custodian/"AI-trading" products, and fake-recovery-service operations under a single compound's operational control). **OAK-T7** (Laundering — the Huione Group laundering infrastructure is identified by FinCEN as the primary laundering substrate for Southeast Asian compound-operated fraud, processing victim deposits at industrial throughput). **OAK-T8** (Operational Continuity / Attribution Signals — the compound operators' mobility across jurisdictions and cross-platform wallet-address overlap constitute the T8.001 cluster-reuse signal at the compound-organisational layer). **OAK-T15** (Off-chain Entry-Vector / Pre-Positioning — the romance-scam and friendship-scam feeder narratives, operated at industrial scale by trafficked workers, are the defining victim-acquisition surface).

**Attribution:** **confirmed (cohort-operator-level)** via OFAC designation (Ly Yong Phat / O-Smach Resort, September 2024), DOJ forfeiture filing (Chen Zhi / Prince Group, October 2025), and FinCEN section 311 final rule (Huione Group, October 2025). Individual platform operators within each compound are `pseudonymous` at the named-individual level; the compound-level operators (Ly Yong Phat, Chen Zhi) are `confirmed` at the federal-record level.

**Key teaching point:** **The Southeast Asian scam-compound model is the industrial-organisational shape that produces T11.005.001 (Fake-CEX) at scale.** The compound structure — forced labour, multi-platform fraud stacking, integrated laundering infrastructure, and jurisdictional arbitrage — converts the per-platform fake-exchange fraud into an industrial operation whose aggregate extraction exceeds every other crypto-attack class combined in retail-victim-count terms. The detection surface is at the federal-record / OSINT layer (OFAC, FinCEN, DOJ, UNODC) rather than at the on-chain forensics layer; the on-chain signals are downstream of the compound's operational structure and require off-chain attribution context to interpret.

## Summary

The Southeast Asian scam-compound ecosystem emerged circa 2020 and expanded through 2025 into the single largest retail-aggregate-loss class in crypto. The defining operational innovation is the **compound model**: forced-labour compounds in Cambodia, Burma (Myanmar), Laos, and the Philippines house thousands of trafficked workers who operate romance-scam and friendship-scam feeder narratives at industrial throughput, targeting victims globally in dozens of languages.

The compound structure layers three fraud products against the same victim cohort:

1. **Fake-CEX / pig-butchering platform (T11.005.001)** — the victim is directed to deposit into a fake exchange whose balance display, trade history, and P&L dashboard are database fictions. The platform displays fictitious profits to induce escalating deposits; withdrawal is blocked or gated behind perpetual "tax" / "verification" preconditions.
2. **Fake-custodian / fake-asset-manager (T11.005.002)** — the victim is sold an "AI-trading" yield product or "professional managed account" whose returns are fictional. The product does not trade; deposits are aggregated into the compound's laundering infrastructure.
3. **Fake-recovery service** — previous victims of (1) or (2) are re-contacted by the same compound through a different feeder channel, claiming to be a "recovery service" or "law firm" that can recover the lost funds for an upfront fee. The recovery service is a secondary fraud operated by the same compound.

The laundering infrastructure (Huione Group) processes victim deposits through a multi-hop chain involving OTC desks, shell-company bank accounts, real-estate purchases, and casino-chip conversion. The industrial throughput — tens of billions of dollars across 2020–2025 per FinCEN — makes the compound model the dominant retail crypto-fraud laundering surface globally.

## What defenders observed

- **The compound structure converts individual fraud into industrial-scale extraction.** The compound model's defining features — forced labour for victim-acquisition throughput, multi-layer fraud stacking, integrated laundering infrastructure, and jurisdictional arbitrage — produce aggregate extraction that exceeds all other crypto-attack classes combined in retail-victim-count terms. The FBI IC3 reported $5.8B in pig-butchering-class losses in the U.S. alone in 2024. The compound model is the operational substrate behind that aggregate.
- **The Huione Group laundering infrastructure is the binding constraint on compound operations.** FinCEN's section 311 final rule (October 2025) identifies Huione Group as the primary laundering substrate for Southeast Asian compound-operated fraud — victim deposits across multiple compounds and multiple fake platforms concentrate through Huione-associated wallets, OTC desks, and shell-company banking infrastructure. Disrupting the laundering substrate (FinCEN section 311, OFAC designations, DOJ civil forfeiture) is the highest-leverage federal-action surface.
- **Jurisdictional arbitrage is the compound model's primary defence.** Compounds operate in jurisdictions with weak or compromised law-enforcement cooperation (Myanmar's KK Park, the Laos Golden Triangle Special Economic Zone, contested areas of Cambodia) and relocate across jurisdictions in response to enforcement pressure. The cross-border mobility pattern is the T8.001 operator-continuity signal applied at the compound-organisational layer.
- **Retail-bank-side debit-card monitoring is the highest-cadence operational prevention surface.** The FBI's Operation Level Up model — notifying retail banks when their debit cards are being used to deposit to known compound-operated platform addresses — prevented $359M+ in losses across 5,831+ victims by April 2025. The model operates at the retail-bank/payment-rail layer, which is upstream of the on-chain deposit and therefore the highest-cadence prevention surface for the compound-operated class.

## What this example tells contributors writing future Technique pages

- **T11.005.003 is the primary classification for the compound-organisational shape.** The compound model is structurally distinct from per-platform T11.005.001: it is the organisational infrastructure that produces T11.005.001 at scale, not a per-platform fraud. The multi-layer fraud stacking (fake-CEX + fake-custodian + fake-recovery), the forced-labour victim-acquisition throughput, and the integrated laundering infrastructure are the defining T11.005.003 signals.
- **The federal-record surface is the canonical detection layer for T11.005.003.** OFAC SDN designations, DOJ civil-forfeiture filings, and FinCEN section 311 actions are the primary public-record anchors. On-chain victim-deposit-wallet cluster overlap with OFAC-designated entities is the on-chain signal, but the signal is downstream of the compound's organisational structure and requires off-chain attribution context to interpret.
- **The T11.005.003 / T7 / T8 co-occurrence is structural.** Every compound-operated fraud case involves laundering infrastructure (T7 — Huione Group) and operator-continuity signals (T8 — cross-jurisdiction mobility, cross-platform wallet-address overlap). Contributors should map all three when documenting compound-operated cases.
